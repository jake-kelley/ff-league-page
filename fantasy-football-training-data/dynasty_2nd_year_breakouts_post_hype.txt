DYNASTY 2nd / 3rd YEAR BREAKOUTS AND POST-HYPE SLEEPERS
========================================================
Sources synthesized: Dynasty Nerds breakout WRs, RotoBaller second-year breakout candidates, FantasyPros breakout columns, Footballguys post-hype articles, PFF rookie-to-year-2 transition data.

THE BREAKOUT WINDOW
-------------------
Most NFL skill players who become fantasy stars do so in YEARS 2-3 of their NFL career. This is a critical dynasty arbitrage window because:
- Year 1 production is suppressed by adjustment.
- Owners overreact to year-1 performance.
- Year-2 breakouts buy at year-1 prices.

WIDE RECEIVER 2nd-YEAR BREAKOUT PROFILE

PREDICTIVE FACTORS:
- NFL R1-R2 capital.
- 600+ rookie yards.
- Target share trending up late season.
- Stable QB situation entering year 2.
- Same OC year-over-year (continuity helps).

HISTORICAL TEMPLATES:
- Justin Jefferson (1,400 yards rookie -> 1,600 yards Year 2).
- Ja'Marr Chase (1,455 yards rookie - established immediately).
- Garrett Wilson (1,103 yards rookie -> stayed elite Year 2).
- Drake London (Year 1 quiet -> Year 3 break-out).

WR3-YEAR-2-BREAKOUT HIT RATE (NFL R1 WRs):
- Roughly 50-60% of NFL R1 WRs hit a Top-24 finish by Year 2.
- The remaining 40-50% need Year 3 or never hit.

WR YEAR 3 IS THE LAST CHANCE
- Year 3 is the second-best breakout window.
- WRs with 60+ targets in Year 2 break out 50%+ of the time in Year 3 regardless of Year-2 efficiency.
- After Year 3, breakout rate plummets.

IDENTIFYING YEAR-2 WR BREAKOUT CANDIDATES

GREEN FLAGS:
- High snap share by season's end.
- Underlying advanced metrics (Y/RR, target share rising).
- Off-season investment (team didn't draft a WR; QB stable).
- Coaching praise / promoted role.

RED FLAGS:
- Team drafted another WR in NFL R1.
- Coordinator change.
- QB downgrade.
- Recurring injuries.

RUNNING BACK 2nd-YEAR BREAKOUT PROFILE

PREDICTIVE FACTORS:
- Year-1 starter production at >12 fantasy points/game.
- Vet RB ahead of him departing in FA.
- Team reluctance to draft another RB.

LESS COMMON FOR RBS:
- Most NFL RBs reveal their ceiling in Year 1.
- Year-2 RB breakouts are RARER than WR.
- Examples: James Conner (Year 4 in PIT), Phillip Lindsay (UDFA blowup).

PROFILE TYPES:
- COMMITTEE-EMERGENCE RB: was 2nd-RB Year 1 -> RB1 in Year 2 due to FA departure.
- INJURY-RECOVERY: rookie injury limited Year 1; full role Year 2.
- ROLE EXPANSION: limited touches Year 1 due to vet -> workhorse Year 2.

QUARTERBACK 2nd-YEAR BREAKOUT PROFILE

PREDICTIVE FACTORS:
- Drafted Top-32 in NFL Draft.
- Year-1 reps (didn't sit).
- Rushing upside.
- Stable supporting cast.

HISTORICAL TEMPLATES:
- C.J. Stroud (top-3 from rookie season; sustained).
- Jordan Love (post-Aaron-Rodgers emergence).
- Anthony Richardson (rookie limited by injury, year 2-3 ramp).

QB BREAKOUT TIMELINE:
- Year 1: developmental for many.
- Year 2-3: production peak window.
- Year 4: established or declining.

TIGHT END BREAKOUT PROFILE

TE TIMELINE IS LATER:
- Year 3 is the typical TE breakout year.
- Year 2 is too early for most.
- Year 4-5 is fine for late-developing TEs.

PREDICTIVE FACTORS:
- NFL R1-R2 capital.
- Athletic profile (good combine).
- Receiving role in college.
- Slow build-up over Year 1-2 with role expansion.

POST-HYPE SLEEPER STRATEGIES

WHO IS A POST-HYPE SLEEPER?
- A player who was hyped at draft / Year 1 but hasn't broken out.
- Year 3-5 of NFL career.
- Currently unowned or low-value in dynasty.

ASSESSING IF THE SLEEPER STILL HAS BREAKOUT POTENTIAL

ASK:
1. Why didn't he break out?
2. Has the underlying problem changed?
3. Is there a NEW catalyst (FA WR change, new QB, new OC)?
4. What's his current age vs cliff?

GOOD POST-HYPE SLEEPER SIGNALS:
- Coaching change in his favor.
- Departing teammate at his position.
- New QB upgrade.
- Improved offensive system.

BAD POST-HYPE SIGNALS:
- Same situation 4 years running.
- Performance hasn't improved year-over-year.
- Team has invested at his position twice.

POST-HYPE TIMELINE

WR:
- Year 4: still possible breakout (~15% rate).
- Year 5: rare (~5%).
- Year 6+: career profile, not breakout.

RB:
- Year 3: realistic breakout window if role opens.
- Year 4: rare without team change.
- Year 5+: practically never.

QB:
- Year 4: starter probability solidified by now.
- Year 5+: career profile, not breakout.

TE:
- Year 4: still realistic.
- Year 5: possible (TE develops slower).
- Year 6+: career profile.

POST-HYPE BUYING TACTICS

ENTRY POINT:
- Buy when Year 1-2 was disappointing AND a clear catalyst is emerging.
- Pay 3rd-round rookie pick equivalent.

EXIT POINT:
- Sell after a 4-game hot stretch (perceived breakout).
- Don't hold beyond Year 4-5 if no improvement.

EXAMPLE SHELL:
- Year-3 NFL R1 WR with new QB upgrade.
- Currently valued at WR60.
- Acquire for late 3rd or low-tier vet.
- If he breaks out: now WR25, sell for rookie 2nd or hold as starter.
- If he doesn't: cut after Year 4 with no role expansion.

THE 2026 OFFSEASON ANGLE
------------------------
Always look for:
- Year-2 WRs from 2025 NFL Draft with rocky rookie years.
- Year-3 RBs from 2024 with role changes coming.
- Year-2 QBs from 2025 stepping into starter role.
- Year-3 TEs from 2024 with system fits.

THE COMMON ANALYST FRAMEWORK
----------------------------
Most analysts identify their year-2 breakout candidates in February-March each offseason. By April-May, market reprices. The buy window is FEBRUARY before the market hype catches up.

THE 2nd-YEAR-BUY ARBITRAGE
--------------------------
The "Year-2 Breakout" is the single most repeatable dynasty arbitrage:
- Buy in Feb-March at calc.
- Hold through breakout.
- Either keep (if you wanted him) or sell-high in October.

This pattern, executed yearly, generates significant alpha.

POST-HYPE MISTAKES

1) HOLDING ETERNALLY
- Year 5 hasn't broken out -> stop hoping.

2) BUYING WITHOUT CATALYST
- Just because he's cheap doesn't mean he's a buy.

3) OVERPAYING FOR HYPE
- "He must break out" market premium = no longer post-hype.

4) IGNORING WORKLOAD
- A WR with 35% target share isn't post-hype - he's just bad.

5) IGNORING TEAM INVESTMENT
- Drafted multiple WRs in 2 years = your guy is buried.
