# DYNASTY MARKET INEFFICIENCIES & ARBITRAGE OPPORTUNITIES
Sources: peakedinhighskool.com trade value charts, FantasyTradeAnalyzers strategies, FantasyCalc methodology, KTC discussions, dynasty community arbitrage discussions.

## THE CORE THEORY
Dynasty markets are inefficient because:
1. Information asymmetry across owners (some research, some don't).
2. Cognitive biases (recency bias, hot-streak overweighting).
3. Emotional attachment to players.
4. Time-pressure: managers don't update values often.
5. Format-specific mispricing (calc cross-format error).

These inefficiencies create REPEATABLE arbitrage. Owners who systematically exploit them gain 15-25% additional value annually.

### CATEGORY 1: AGE ARBITRAGE

OPPORTUNITY:
- Aged 28+ vets producing at peak.
- Market overweights age; under-weights production.
- Contenders need them; rebuilders are dumping.

EXAMPLE PATTERN:
- 29 yo elite WR with 1,300-yard season.
- Calc value: 5,500 (discounted for age).
- True 1-year contender value: 7,500+.
- TRADE: rebuilder sells to contender at 5,500 → contender wins lineup; rebuilder gets future picks.

EXECUTION:
- Both sides win (asymmetric values).
- Repeat annually with same vets.

### CATEGORY 2: ROOKIE PICK ARBITRAGE

PRE-NFL DRAFT:
- Picks priced as "options."
- Class strength uncertainty.
- BUY future picks here at discount.

POST-NFL DRAFT:
- Picks crystallize.
- Class hype peaks.
- SELL picks at peak.

ROUND-TO-ROUND:
- 1.01 vs 1.06: market sometimes treats them as similar tier.
- They're not. 1.01 = 100, 1.06 = 56 (44% gap).
- TRADE 1.01 ↔ 1.06 + 2nd: structural arbitrage.

### CATEGORY 3: FORMAT MISPRICING

CALC IS USUALLY 1QB OR SF DEFAULT:
- TE Premium changes top TE values 25%+.
- Calc users in TEP often misvalue elite TEs.
- BUY young elite TEs in TEP-aware leagues at calc.
- TEP is the most exploitable format mispricing.

SUPERFLEX QB12-20:
- 1QB minds undervalue mid-tier SF QBs.
- Buy QB16 from a 1QB-minded SF owner cheap.
- Flip to QB-needy SF owner at premium.

### CATEGORY 4: NARRATIVE ARBITRAGE

NEGATIVE NARRATIVE OVER-CORRECTION:
- "Coaching change is bad for him" - often wrong.
- "He's lost a step" - often premature.
- "Rookie buried him" - often resolves.

BUY: against the panic narrative.
SELL: with the hype narrative.

### CATEGORY 5: PHASE ARBITRAGE

CONTENDER vs REBUILDER ASSET ASYMMETRY:
- The rebuilder has surplus aging vet (which contender needs).
- Contender has surplus picks/youth (which rebuilder needs).
- Direct trade: both win.

This is THE single most repeatable arbitrage pattern.

### CATEGORY 6: TIME-PRESSURE ARBITRAGE

DEADLINE PRESSURE:
- Late-October contender NEEDS production.
- Pays 1.5-2x calc.
- Sell vets here.

OFFSEASON DOLDRUMS:
- January-February markets are cold.
- Fair-value buys available.

### CATEGORY 7: PERCEPTION GAP ARBITRAGE

PUBLIC PERCEPTION vs REALITY:
- Hyped name with declining metrics = sell.
- Quiet name with rising metrics = buy.
- Use Y/RR, target share trend over counting stats.

### CATEGORY 8: LIQUIDITY ARBITRAGE

LIQUID PLAYERS ARE EASIER TO TRADE:
- A 6,000 calc value liquid player > 7,000 calc illiquid.
- The "liquidity premium" is real.
- Trade up in liquidity even at marginal calc loss.

### CATEGORY 9: STARTUP DRAFT ASYMMETRIES

POST-STARTUP TRADES:
- Owners over-drafted at certain positions.
- Some have surplus you can pry cheap.
- Within 2 weeks of startup is the highest-value window.

### CATEGORY 10: BYE WEEK ARBITRAGE

DOUBLE-BYE OWNERS:
- Two starters share a bye.
- Forced to trade for coverage.
- Sell coverage at premium.

### CATEGORY 11: YEAR-OVER-YEAR PATTERN ARBITRAGE

POST-FA NEW LANDING:
- Veteran on new team = market overcorrects.
- Underlying ability matters more than situation change.

POST-NFL DRAFT TARGETED:
- Player whose team drafted competition.
- Often cannibalization is overestimated.

### CATEGORY 12: DRAFT CAPITAL UNDERVALUATION (ROOKIES)

NFL R3-R4 ROOKIES IN GOOD LANDING SPOTS:
- Often available at discount in dynasty rookie drafts.
- NFL R1-R2 dominates dynasty 1st round.
- R3-R4 with role can be Year-2 startable; available at 3rd-round dynasty pick.

### COMPOUND ARBITRAGE STRATEGIES

THE CONTENDER-REBUILDER SWING:
1. Contender pays 1.5x calc for vet you sell.
2. Rebuilder accepts 0.7x calc for vet you sell.
3. Net: you take buyer's price (high), seller's price (low) on different transactions.
4. Generates ~30-50% calc-to-execution premium.

THE PICK CYCLE:
1. February: BUY future picks at discount.
2. April: SELL same picks at peak (post-NFL Draft).
3. May: BUY rookies at peak rookie-draft slots.
4. October: SELL rookies as they break out.

### TRACKING ARBITRAGE OPPORTUNITIES

JOURNAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Date noticed.
- Asset name.
- Type of arbitrage.
- Calc value.
- Your fair value.
- Opportunity duration.
- Action taken.
- 6-month outcome.

PATTERN REVIEW:
- Quarterly: identify which arbitrage types you're best at.
- Annually: refine your edge.

### CAUTIONS

ARBITRAGE WINDOWS CLOSE:
- The market reprices.
- Your edge requires constant work.
- Don't assume what worked last year still works.

LEAGUE-SPECIFIC INEFFICIENCIES:
- Some leagues are highly efficient (engaged owners).
- Some are inefficient (passive owners).
- Calibrate to YOUR league.

EXECUTION DIFFICULTY:
- "On paper" arbitrage doesn't always materialize.
- Owners must accept your offers.
- Communication and reputation matter.

## PAYOFF EXPECTATION
Skilled arbitrage = +10-15% roster value annually.
Compound over 5 years = +60-90% better roster vs passive owner.

## THE BIG LESSON
You don't beat dynasty by being smarter than the calc. You beat it by:
1. Identifying gaps between calc and TRUE value.
2. Finding owners on the wrong side.
3. Closing trades quickly before the gap closes.

Dynasty IS a market. The discipline to spot and trade inefficiencies is the actual competitive edge.
