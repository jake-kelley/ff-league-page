DYNASTY TANKING - ETHICS, STRATEGY, AND PREVENTION
====================================================
Sources synthesized: Footballguys Harstad "Making Tanking Obsolete," Pro Football Network tanking prevention, Dynasty Nerds tanking reduction, RotoWire dynasty rebuild & tanking strategies, Footballguys forum tanking threads, Dynasty League Football tanking discussions, League Tycoon tanking guide.

WHAT IS TANKING
---------------
Intentionally setting your team up to lose games to secure higher rookie draft picks. Two forms:
1. LINEUP TANKING: deliberately starting weaker players. Generally considered cheating.
2. ASSET TANKING: trading current production for picks. Acceptable in dynasty.

WHY TANKING IS A DYNASTY-SPECIFIC ISSUE
---------------------------------------
- Rookie picks are tied to standings.
- The 1.01 is materially more valuable than the 1.10.
- Eliminated teams have nothing to play for late-season except next year's pick.
- Dynasty rewards multi-year planning, which makes tanking RATIONAL but it harms league health.

THE LEGITIMATE "ASSET TANK" (REBUILD)
-------------------------------------
You can ethically structure your team for a future championship:
- Trade aging vets for young assets / picks.
- Don't add waiver-wire helps when you're out of contention.
- Sit injured-or-questionable players (within reason) - "not pushing" them.
- Activate younger bench guys for development reps.

This is NOT tanking - it's rebuilding. The line is whether you're playing your BEST CURRENTLY-AVAILABLE LINEUP each week.

THE ILLEGITIMATE LINEUP TANK
----------------------------
- Starting your bench WR over your starting WR.
- Sitting healthy starters intentionally.
- Auto-drafting / not setting lineup.
- Refusing to claim a clearly better player off waivers.
This violates good-faith competition and is often grounds for league action.

WHY TANKING IS BAD FOR LEAGUES
------------------------------
- Removes meaningful Week 13-17 games.
- Reduces engagement of 30-40% of league at season's end.
- Creates blowout point margins that distort tiebreakers.
- Damages competitive culture - "earn your team."

ANTI-TANKING MECHANISMS (COMMISSIONER OPTIONS)

1) POTENTIAL POINTS METHOD
- Track total fantasy points scored by eliminated teams.
- Highest-scoring eliminated team gets the 1.01.
- INCENTIVIZES playing best lineups.

2) CONSOLATION BRACKET FOR PICKS
- Eliminated teams play in losers' bracket.
- Winner of losers bracket gets 1.01, runner-up 1.02, etc.
- Rewards effort and competitive participation throughout season.

3) WEIGHTED LOTTERY (NBA-STYLE)
- Bottom 4-6 teams enter a weighted lottery for top picks.
- 1st-place draft odds decline as record improves.
- Reduces "zero benefit" to losing the most.

4) DRAFT PICK INVERSION
- Worst record gets MIDDLE pick (not top); best non-playoff team gets top pick.
- Rewards trying.

5) COMMISSIONER LINEUP ENFORCEMENT
- Commissioner authority to substitute "obvious" tank lineups with optimal.
- Repeated violations = inactive owner status.

6) FORMULA: "Total Points + Wins" tiebreaker for picks
- Encourages BOTH winning and scoring.

PRO/CON OF VARIOUS METHODS

POTENTIAL POINTS:
+ Easy to implement, transparent.
+ Rewards engagement.
- Can be gamed by stacking high-scoring matchups.
- Doesn't reward "weak teams that overachieve."

CONSOLATION BRACKET:
+ Maintains weekly H2H structure.
+ Ladder-style incentive.
- Harder to manage on Sleeper (some platforms support natively).
- Can still be gamed by intentional consolation losses.

WEIGHTED LOTTERY:
+ NBA-tested model.
+ Reduces extreme "race to bottom."
- Removes some value of winning early picks.
- Some owners feel "luck" shouldn't decide picks.

REBUILD MANAGER GUIDE - HOW TO DO IT WITHOUT TANKING

1. Sell aged vets for picks via in-season trades. Don't carry dead weight.
2. Add wavier-wire RBs/WRs who get role expansion - they have lottery value AND help current team.
3. Promote rookies/young bench to starting lineup. Development reps + you might get lucky.
4. Don't tank lineup. Play your best 9 each week. The marginal "loss" is 5% draft slot - not worth integrity.
5. Communicate. Tell league chat: "I'm a seller. Make me offers." Sets expectations.

LATE-SEASON TRADE STRATEGY (REBUILDER)
--------------------------------------
- Weeks 9-12 (peak trade window): contender desperation = your premium.
- Sell every productive vet you can.
- Don't accept current-year picks from contenders - get future.

TANKER TELLS (SIGNALS YOU'RE BEING NAUGHTY)
-------------------------------------------
- Sitting clear top-12 RB for an unranked bench player.
- Not setting lineup at all 2+ weeks in a row.
- Refusing to claim a starter off waivers when teams are decimated.
- Dropping a productive player for no replacement.

HOW LEAGUEMATES SHOULD HANDLE TANKING
-------------------------------------
1. Document via screenshots.
2. Bring to league chat respectfully.
3. Vote on remediation per constitution.
4. If commissioner doesn't act, consider league restructure for next season.

THE LONG-TERM CASE AGAINST TANKING (FOR THE TANKER)
---------------------------------------------------
Even if you "get away with" tanking:
- Reputation damage.
- Other owners less likely to trade with you.
- League health degrades, fewer competitive opportunities.
- Karma: eventually you'll be on the contending side and someone will tank against you.

PHILOSOPHY OF "COMPETITIVE REBUILDING"
--------------------------------------
The best dynasty managers REBUILD WITHOUT TANKING. They:
- Trade vets aggressively in season.
- Add WW pieces with future relevance.
- Play full lineups for points-scored standing tiebreakers.
- Use rebuild years to test bench players.
- Never embarrass themselves in lineup decisions.

This is the high-integrity path AND statistically still produces top picks because most rebuilds produce 4-6 win seasons regardless.

CASES WHERE A LATE-SEASON SIT MIGHT BE LEGITIMATE
-------------------------------------------------
- Your starter is questionable on a short week.
- Your starter is on a team mathematically eliminated and could rest.
- Your starter has dropped on snap share for 3+ weeks.
- Bye-week roster squeezes.

If the SIT can be defended on football grounds (not draft-pick grounds), you're probably fine.

COMMISSIONER PSYCHOLOGY TIP
---------------------------
Make tanking unprofitable RATHER than punitive. Anti-tanking systems should ALIGN incentives with effort, not just punish bad behavior.
