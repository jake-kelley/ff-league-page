DYNASTY TRADE ANALYSIS - PRINCIPLES AND METHODS
================================================
Sources synthesized: Fantasy Footballers "Dynasty Trade Secrets" series, Footballguys & FantasyPros analyst trade columns, KeepTradeCut crowdsourced methodology, Dynasty Nerds trade framework, PlayerProfiler trade guide, FantasyPros analyzer methodology, FantasyCalc methodology, DynastyProcess trade calc, Dynatyze, RotoTrade.

THE BASIC TRADE FRAMEWORK
-------------------------
Every dynasty trade can be evaluated against a 5-factor matrix:
1. Total value (sum of trade calc on each side)
2. Best player (which side gets the highest single asset)
3. Roster context (does this fit your team's phase?)
4. Time horizon (does this fit your competitive window?)
5. Liquidity (are the assets you receive easy to flip later?)

If at least 4 of 5 factors favor you, the trade is positive-EV.

VALUE TIERS - THE ONLY THING THAT MATTERS LONG-TERM
---------------------------------------------------
The market overweights raw value. The actual driver of dynasty success is value WITHIN TIERS. A 7,500-value WR1 is materially better than a 6,800-value WR1 IF the difference matters for hitting league-best lineups. But three 2,500-value WR4s are NOT better than one 7,500-value WR1, because the WR1 alone wins lineup weeks.

Practical rule: trade three "below tier" assets for one "above tier" asset whenever possible.

THE "STUDS WIN" RULE - THE MOST IMPORTANT TRADE PRINCIPLE
---------------------------------------------------------
In a 2-for-1 (you give 2 mid pieces, get 1 stud), the stud side wins long-term ~75% of the time even when calc says even. Why?
- Stars are scarce; depth is replaceable.
- Lineup math: you can only start 1 player per slot. Two RB3s is not 2x an RB1; it's still RB3 production.
- Roster compression: as you accumulate stars, your bench fills with churn-able lottery tickets. Both sides win.
- Roster expansion: as you accumulate depth, your stars erode (aging) faster than your depth appreciates.

ASYMMETRIC VALUE - HOW TO ACTUALLY WIN TRADES
---------------------------------------------
A trade is asymmetric when the SAME asset has different VALUE TO different teams.

Asymmetry sources:
- Phase: a contender values production now; rebuilder values future.
- Roster: a team thin at WR values WR more than the calc.
- Bye weeks: matters less long-term but locally. Doubles up = forced trade leverage.
- Schedule: a contender approaching playoffs needs MATCHUP wins.

How to identify and exploit asymmetry:
- Read the league. Figure out everyone's roster, age, phase.
- The contender's least-needed bench piece is your most-needed core piece (and vice versa).
- A rebuilder will pay a premium for picks; a contender pays a premium for known production.
- Time the offer to maximize asymmetry: contender's deadline (NFL trade deadline alignment), rebuilder's offseason (post-startup hype).

THE 30-DAY TREND HEURISTIC
--------------------------
- Trend +500 last 30 days = at-or-above the market. Sell to anyone who'll match the calc number.
- Trend -500 last 30 days = market hasn't fully priced the bad news. Buy if you believe the underlying.
- Trend stable = use full calc as anchor.

BUY-LOW SIGNALS (when to acquire)
---------------------------------
- Player just had a single down week or game causing panic.
- Coordinator change with positive scheme implication for the player.
- Coming off injury (within reason) - market often overcorrects on injuries.
- Drafted into "bad" landing spot but the player is talented (talent endures).
- "Boring" workhorse on bad team that suddenly added an RB threat - panic is overblown.
- Year-2 WR with poor rookie targets but R1 NFL draft capital.
- Aging-but-elite vet on a contender roster late in season (his owner is panicking about decline).

SELL-HIGH SIGNALS (when to ship)
--------------------------------
- Hot 4-game start that exceeds career profile.
- Production driven by unsustainable factors (TD rate, target spike, injury to teammate).
- Player heading into final contract year on a non-committed team.
- Aging player coming off a career high.
- Hyped breakout candidate before he plays a snap.
- Rookie pick value spiking right after the NFL draft.

NEGOTIATION TACTICS
-------------------
1. ALWAYS open with the question, not the offer. "Are you interested in [Player X]?" or "Where is your roster headed?" - elicits info, doesn't anchor you.
2. DON'T anchor low. A lowball offer poisons the negotiation. Open at fair-or-slight-tilt.
3. USE TIERS, not specific players. "I'd love to upgrade RB. Got a top-15 RB I could pry?" Lets the other GM offer first - they'll often offer more than you'd ask.
4. NEVER complete a trade in your head before sending. Always have a backup target if it fails.
5. SHOW the work. "Calc has X at 5,200 and Y+pick at 5,400. Even with a slight bump for me." People respond better to evidence-backed offers.
6. WALK AWAY. The threat to walk away gives you leverage IF you actually mean it.
7. TRADE-PLUS-CASH. In money leagues, side payments can clear logjams. Within league rules.
8. PERSONAL REPUTATION. In long-running leagues, fairness compounds. Be a hard-but-fair negotiator; don't swindle. Repeat-game theory.
9. EMOTIONAL PUSH-BACK. If a manager refuses, ask why. The reason ("I love this player") tells you not to retry; ("I want a 1st") tells you what to add.
10. PUBLIC vs PRIVATE. Don't shop publicly except when wanting to start a market. League chat trades > DM trades for getting bids.

TRADE FREQUENCY
---------------
Volume traders win. Studies of high-trade leagues show top finishers average 3-5x the trades of bottom finishers. Why? Each trade is a positive-EV bet (with effort). More bets = more wins.

THE 3-FOR-1 PLAYBOOK
--------------------
The single most repeatable winning trade pattern. Identify a top-tier player on a roster (often a contender's stud) whose owner has thin depth. Offer 3 mid-tier players (your team's surplus) totaling slightly less than the stud's calc value. Many owners take it because they "fix multiple positions." You consolidate to a bigger asset.

THE PICK ARBITRAGE
------------------
Pick value differs based on owner's standings. The contender's 1st is "late 1st" expected slot; the rebuilder's 1st is "early 1st." If you can swap these (with a piece going the other way), you systematically extract value.

CALCULATOR USAGE - GUARDRAILS
-----------------------------
- KeepTradeCut: best for 1QB and SF crowdsourced consensus. Updates ~weekly.
- FantasyCalc: algorithmic + crowdsourced; updates daily; provides redraft+dynasty composite.
- Dynasty Trade Calculator (DLF): expert-driven, slower to update, opinion-heavy.
- DynastyProcess: data-driven, mostly an academic baseline.
- Sleeper trade reviewer: very loose, easily exploited.

ALWAYS check 2 calcs minimum. They disagree by 10-25% on borderline players, and that disagreement is the actual market spread.

WARNING: Calculators don't know:
- Your league's scoring nuances.
- Your roster context.
- Your standings.
- Real trade frequency / liquidity.

Use them as a starting point, never the final word.

POST-TRADE EVALUATION (LEARN FROM EVERY TRADE)
----------------------------------------------
After every trade, write down:
- Why you did the trade (asset reason, roster reason).
- Time horizon (when you expected to "win" the trade).
- One-year-later check-in: did the math play out?
This is how you become a better trader. Most managers don't track and never improve.
