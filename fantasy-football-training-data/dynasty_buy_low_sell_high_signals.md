# DYNASTY BUY-LOW / SELL-HIGH - DETAILED SIGNALS AND PLAYBOOKS
Sources: FantasyPros buy-or-sell columns (Jan-Feb-Mar 2026), Pro Football Network, RotoBaller, Fantasy Points "buys and sells" columns, PlayerProfiler vet sells, FantasySixPack vets to buy/sell.

## CORE PRINCIPLE
Buy-low and sell-high is NOT timing the market in absolute terms. It's identifying when a player's PERCEIVED value diverges from their TRUE value, and acting before the gap closes. In dynasty, value is perceptual; you can manipulate perception (hype, narrative) only weakly, but you can RECOGNIZE perception gaps consistently.

### FIVE TYPES OF PERCEPTION GAPS

### 1) RECENCY BIAS GAP
- Owner overweights last 4-game stretch.
- Player A had a 4-game cold spell -> his trade value just dropped 15%.
- If underlying usage/role hasn't changed, this is a buy-low.
- Inverse: a hot 4-game stretch with bad underlying numbers = sell-high.

### 2) NARRATIVE GAP
- Beat reporter / podcast hype creates artificial inflation or deflation.
- Camp darling stories often outsize actual change.
- Negative offseason narratives (off-field, "lost weight wrong way," "bad chemistry") often blow over.
- Buy: against the narrative. Sell: with the narrative at peak.

### 3) CONTEXT GAP
- Coaching change, OC change, QB change, offensive scheme change.
- Owner of player either over- or under-corrects based on incomplete info.
- Look at HISTORICAL example: a similar coaching change in past = templates a likely outcome.

### 4) AGE GAP
- 28+ year-old players are systematically undervalued at peak production.
- Their owners worry about "next year" and panic-sell. Smart contenders BUY.
- Inverse: 22-23 yo "ascending" players are systematically overvalued in offseason hype waves.

### 5) PHASE GAP
- Contender values production now; rebuilder values future.
- If you're a contender and a rebuilder owns a productive vet they'll never start, you can typically buy at 70-80% of calc.

## WR BUY-LOW PROFILES
- Year-2 WR with NFL R1-R2 capital, sub-50 catches as rookie. Most break out year 2-3.
- Productive vet WR (60+ catches) at age 28-30 on a "fading" team. Value drops; production usually doesn't.
- Post-injury WR coming off ACL (year 1 back is 70-80% of pre-injury; year 2 back is 95%+).
- WR who got buried by a hot rookie WR on same team (panic over-correction; targets often equalize).
- WR on team that just changed QBs. If new QB is upgrade, the WR's 30-day trend may not have caught up.

## WR SELL-HIGH PROFILES
- WR on hot 4-game streak driven by unsustainable TD rate (career TD/target ratio significantly exceeded recent stretch).
- Aging WR (29+) just put up a top-10 season - sell IMMEDIATELY before age cliff signals.
- Hyped post-hype darling who finally had a 70-catch season at age 27 (career profile cap).
- WR who just landed in a "perfect" landing spot via FA - market overprices the spot.
- WR on team where a rookie WR just got drafted in NFL R1 (target cannibalization coming).

## RB BUY-LOW PROFILES
- 25-and-under RB on uninspiring offense (the talent endures even if O is bad).
- RB whose owner has too many handcuffs and is liquidating.
- RB coming off injury with clear path to lead role.
- Rookie RB drafted to good landing spot but trapped behind a Year-1 vet who's likely on his way out.

## RB SELL-HIGH PROFILES
- 27+ RB just had RB1 season. Sell IMMEDIATELY. The cliff usually hits within 12-18 months.
- RB coming off career-high in carries (workload burden carries forward as decline accelerator).
- RB on team that drafted a rookie RB in NFL R1-R2 (committee imminent).
- RB hitting free agency with declining team interest (likely committee landing).
- RB whose volume is propped by injury to teammate (regression incoming when teammate returns).

## QB BUY-LOW PROFILES
- Young QB (year 2-3) with rough rookie year on bad team that just got HC/OC upgrade.
- QB demoted briefly mid-season but kept the starting job - panic dropped him 20%.
- Mobile QB coming off injury (rushing floor returns at full strength faster than passing).
- QB on bad offense with hint of upgrade coming (new WR1 in FA, OL improvement).

## QB SELL-HIGH PROFILES
- 35+ QB coming off elite season (Stafford-types). Sell to SF contenders.
- QB1 season driven by unsustainable TD rate.
- QB on team just signaled to draft a rookie QB (incoming competition).
- Run-first QB who already showed mobility decline (Cam-arc).

## TE BUY-LOW PROFILES
- Young TE (24-26) coming off mediocre stat year but with elite athletic profile.
- TE who lost target competition due to teammate hot stretch (regression to baseline coming).
- TE on team transitioning to pass-heavier scheme.
- TE who just changed teams to a TE-friendly system (Kelce-Reid template).

## TE SELL-HIGH PROFILES
- 30+ elite TE coming off TE1 season. Sell hard.
- TD-dependent TE coming off lots of TDs (TD rates regress more than other stats).
- TE coming off a season inflated by injuries to other receivers.

## PICK BUY-LOW SIGNALS
- Pre-NFL combine: when owners haven't done class research, pick prices are sticky and often LOW.
- Late-season trade deadline: rebuilders are dumping picks. Contenders LOW-balling.
- Post-rookie-draft (mid-May): some leagues have leftover pick momentum, owners willing to deal.

## PICK SELL-HIGH SIGNALS
- April pre-draft hype peak. Picks often fetch 120%+ of offseason baseline.
- Post-NFL combine for picks tied to specific class strength positions.
- Trade-deadline pressure on contenders willing to give up far-future picks for production.

## TIMING WINDOWS - CALENDAR-BASED INEFFICIENCIES
- LATE JANUARY: rebuilder season. Sell vets to contenders before market reprices.
- LATE MARCH (post-FA): all WRs reprice. Identify mismatches.
- LATE APRIL (post-NFL Draft): all rookies and roles reprice. Most volatile single window.
- LATE MAY: post-rookie-draft. Owners overcorrect on April hype.
- LATE AUGUST (preseason ends): camp narratives crystallize. Final off-season buy/sell window.
- WEEKS 9-11 (NFL trade deadline aligned): most leverage of the year. Contenders desperate, rebuilders accepting losses.

## THE ONE-WAY SELL DOOR
For aging RBs and 30+ WRs, every offseason that passes WITHOUT selling reduces realizable value. The sell window is open before age 27 (RB) or 30 (WR); after that, the door narrows fast. Many owners can't psychologically sell stars; that emotional friction is your edge.

## THE 30/60/90 DAY TREND ANALYSIS
Use FantasyCalc's 30-day trend AND a manual 60/90 day comparison:
- 30-day spike (>15%): selling window open.
- 30-day drop (>15%) without underlying change: buying window open.
- Persistent 90-day decline: real decline; don't fight the trend.
- Persistent 90-day rise: real ascending; pay if you want shares but check if you're "late."

## EXAMPLE TRADE SHELL (2-FOR-1 BUY-LOW)
You: 2 mid-tier vets (5,200 + 4,800) = 10,000 calc.
Them: 1 ascending young WR (8,500 calc) you've identified as buy-low.
You're "overpaying" by 1,500 calc IN THE MOMENT, but if your buy-low thesis is right, his value rises 30%+ next 6 months while your two vets' values STAY OR DECLINE.
Net long-term: significant positive EV.

## EXECUTION DISCIPLINE
1. Maintain a personal watchlist of 10-15 buy targets and 5-8 sell targets. Update monthly.
2. Send 2-3 trade offers per week during high-leverage windows.
3. Track every trade you make. Review which "buy-low" actually played out.
4. Don't let pride keep you in losing trades. If your thesis breaks, exit at reasonable price - don't bag-hold.
