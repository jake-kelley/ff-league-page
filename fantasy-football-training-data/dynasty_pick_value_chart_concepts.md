# DYNASTY PICK VALUE - CHARTS, CONCEPTS, AND PRACTICAL USAGE
Sources synthesized: FantasyPros monthly trade value charts, Yahoo Justin Boone rookie pick chart, Footballguys rookie value chart, PFF dynasty trade value, Draft Sharks PPR chart, KTC pick values, FantasyCalc pick values, Footballguys "How to Use Dynasty Draft Picks."

## WHY PICK VALUES MATTER
Picks are the ONLY asset class in dynasty whose value is predictable in tier shape regardless of player. They are the ARBITRAGE BASELINE for every trade. Owners who master pick valuation extract 15-25% MORE value annually than those who don't.

### THREE LAYERS OF PICK VALUE

### LAYER 1: SLOT VALUE
- 1.01: top-tier asset, often comparable to a top-30 dynasty player.
- 1.02-1.04: tier 1, still elite.
- 1.05-1.08: tier 2, mid-1st.
- 1.09-1.12: tier 3, late 1st (much less value than 1.01).
- 2nd round: ~30-40% of 1st round average.
- 3rd round: ~10-15% of 1st round average.
- 4th round: lottery, ~3-5%.

### LAYER 2: YEAR VALUE
- Current year (post-NFL Draft): peak value.
- Current year (pre-NFL Draft): ~ 75% of post-draft value.
- 1 year out (next year's pick): ~ 60-75% of current year same slot.
- 2 years out: ~ 40-55%.
- 3 years out (where leagues allow): ~ 25-35%.

### LAYER 3: STANDINGS-EXPECTED VALUE
- A pick from a contender's team = "late" expected slot.
- A pick from a rebuilder's team = "early" expected slot.
- "Unknown future pick" = midpoint expected.

## THE FUTURE PICK DISCOUNT
A 2027 1st (when it's "next year") trades at ~ 60-75% of a 2026 1st. Why?
- Time-discount: you don't get the asset for 1 year.
- Class uncertainty: you don't yet know how strong the class is.
- Slot uncertainty: you don't know your position.

Smart strategy: BUY future picks if you're rebuilding (you'll be drafting high). SELL future picks if you're contending (you'll be drafting low and the discount is bigger than the slot loss).

## CLASS STRENGTH IMPACT
- Strong class: pick values inflate 15-30%; mid-rounds become tradable.
- Weak class (like 2026): pick values compress; 1.01-1.04 retain value, but 1.05+ deflate.
- ALWAYS check class consensus before trading picks.

## POST-NFL-DRAFT PICK VOLATILITY
- Pre-draft: picks are theoretical.
- Post-NFL-Draft (late April): picks crystallize. The 1.01-1.04 prices SPIKE if elite prospects landed well. Late 1sts and 2nds DEFLATE if class lacks depth.
- This 7-day window is the most volatile pick trading period of the year.

## THE "TRADE BACK" PLAY (CONSERVATIVE)
Trade 1.04 -> 1.07 + 2.05.
- Acceptable when there are only 3 elite prospects and you'd be picking 4th anyway.
- You gain a 2nd-round dart throw + maintain a competitive 1st.
- Worse if class has clear top-5 (you give up real value).

## THE "TRADE UP" PLAY (AGGRESSIVE)
Trade 1.06 + 2.02 -> 1.02.
- Acceptable when you've identified a clear-tier prospect at 1.02.
- The gap from 1.06 to 1.02 should be a clear talent gap, not just narrative.
- Costly if class is flat at the top.

## THE "PICK FOR PRODUCTION" PLAY (CONTENDER)
Trade 1.06 + 2.04 -> a productive vet.
- Best for contenders who don't pick high.
- The vet should have 2+ years of expected production.
- Don't do this for short-window vets unless you're truly title-favorite.

## THE "FUTURE PICK ARBITRAGE" PLAY
Trade 2026 1.06 -> 2027 1st.
- The 2027 class projects stronger.
- You don't yet know your slot - upside.
- Caveat: only do if your team won't be a contender in 2027.

## NEVER TRADE
- The 1.01 BEFORE NFL Draft (peak value comes AT or just AFTER the draft).
- Future 1sts as a contender if they could be 1.01-1.04.
- Multiple picks for one mediocre vet.

## ALWAYS EVALUATE
- Class strength (your league consensus).
- Your team's window.
- The opponent's standings projection (what's their pick really worth?).
- Asymmetry: do you NEED this pick? Do they NEED what you have?

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC PICK VALUE ADJUSTMENTS
- 12-team standard: baseline values.
- 10-team: pick values DEFLATE because there are fewer pick rounds and shallower starting requirements.
- 14+ team: pick values INFLATE because depth matters.
- SF: pick values INFLATE because rookie QBs add a position layer.
- TEP: pick values INFLATE because rookie TEs are now real assets.

## REAL-WORLD PICK TRADE PRICE EXAMPLES (1QB CIRCA 2025-2026)
- 1.01 ≈ A top-15 WR (if top WR in class).
- 1.02-1.04 ≈ Mid-WR2 dynasty value.
- 1.05-1.08 ≈ Low-WR2 / high-WR3.
- 1.09-1.12 ≈ Mid-WR3.
- Mid-2nd ≈ Productive vet WR3 value.
- Late 3rd ≈ Stash / lottery value only.

### IN SF
- 1.01 ≈ A top-10 dynasty asset (often a top QB prospect).
- 1.02-1.04 ≈ Top-20 overall.
- Mid-1sts ≈ Top-40 overall.

## KEY MISTAKES IN PICK TRADING
1) Treating all 1sts as equal. The 1.01 vs 1.10 gap is enormous.
2) Hoarding picks beyond your draft slots. If your roster is full, picks lose value.
3) Trading future picks without considering YOUR future standing.
4) Underrating 2nds (often great value if class has depth).
5) Selling picks too early in offseason (peak value is March-April).
6) Buying picks at peak (March-April premium baked in).

### THE SEASONAL ARBITRAGE CYCLE FOR PICKS

PEAK SELL: April (post-NFL draft, hype climax).
LOW BUY: June-August (post-rookie draft, attention drops).
MID-SELL: October-November (deadline contender desperation).
LOW BUY: January-February (offseason baseline).

## PICK VALUE LOAD DISTRIBUTION (FOR ROSTER PLANNING)
Aim for:
- 2-3 picks in upcoming rookie draft.
- 2-3 picks in next year's draft.
- 1-2 picks 2 years out.

This balanced distribution gives you constant rookie infusion + future flexibility.
