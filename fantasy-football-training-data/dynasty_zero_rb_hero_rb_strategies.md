# DYNASTY ROSTER CONSTRUCTION - ZERO RB / HERO RB / ANCHOR RB / ROBUST RB
Sources synthesized: Fantasy Six Pack RB strategy comparison, Footballguys RB strategy guide, Fantasy Points zero-RB targets, FantasyLabs hero RB explanation, Draft Sharks zero-RB skepticism, Fantasy Alarm strategy guide.

### THE FOUR MAIN APPROACHES (DYNASTY)

### A) ROBUST RB (TRADITIONAL)
- Take RBs in rounds 1-3 heavily.
- Goal: lock in workhorse production early.
- Pros: front-loaded weekly floor.
- Cons: aging-out fastest. RBs decline by year 4-5.
- Dynasty fit: marginal in 2024-2026 era. RBs depreciate too fast.

### B) HERO RB (ANCHOR RB)
- One elite RB in rounds 1-2.
- WRs/QBs in rounds 2-5.
- RB depth via mid-rounds + waivers + lottery rookies.
- Pros: balance + flexibility.
- Cons: need that one stud to hit; otherwise position thin.
- Dynasty fit: strong. Most popular winning approach in 2024-2026.

### C) ZERO RB DYNASTY
- Skip RB entirely until rounds 4-7.
- Hammer WRs, QBs, TEs early.
- Build RB via WW + lottery rookies.
- Pros: youngest roster construction. Best long-term hold value.
- Cons: 1-2 year window of poor RB production likely.
- Dynasty fit: strong for rebuilders / 2-3 year planners.

### D) ANCHOR RB (subset of Hero RB)
- One RB in rounds 1-3 only.
- Maximizes RB1 slot while minimizing RB2 risk.
- Often paired with Zero RB the rest of the way.

### WHY DYNASTY DIFFERS FROM REDRAFT FOR THESE STRATEGIES

REDRAFT ZERO RB:
- Works because RBs are TD-dependent and injury-prone in single seasons.
- RB volume comes from waivers when starters get hurt.
- One-year horizon means aging doesn't matter.

DYNASTY ZERO RB:
- Works for slightly different reasons.
- RB depreciation is the real driver.
- Locking in old RB1s in startup buys depreciating assets.
- Going WR-heavy gives you LONGER-window assets.

## HERO RB - WHY IT WORKS BEST IN MODERN DYNASTY
1. The top 5-6 RBs are real, scarce, weekly difference-makers.
2. Owning one of them locks in 1 weekly advantage.
3. Skipping the next 4-6 RB tiers (where bust rate is high) preserves capital.
4. Depth at WR offsets RB risk.
5. Waivers and rookie picks provide reasonable RB2/RB3 production.

## ROBUST RB - WHY IT FAILS IN DYNASTY
1. 3 RB1s in startup = 3 aging assets.
2. Year 2-3, you're losing 1-2 of them to age cliff.
3. Trading them late doesn't recover startup capital.
4. WR depth missing - hard to start strong lineup.

## ZERO RB DYNASTY - WHY IT'S CONTROVERSIAL
SUPPORTERS:
- Long-window assets only.
- Maximum optionality.
- Aligns with "youth wins" philosophy.

SKEPTICS:
- Year 1 lineup is weak; can't compete.
- Depending on waivers/lottery is high variance.
- Misses the RB premium in playoff lineups.

### ROOKIE DRAFT TIE-INS

HERO RB:
- In rookie drafts, hammer WRs/QBs.
- Use RB picks for breakout candidates with starting role projection.

ZERO RB:
- Rookie RBs become your Year 2 production.
- Take 2-3 RB shots per rookie draft.

### POSITION MIX TARGETS (12-team, 25-roster, after startup)

HERO RB FINISH:
- 1 elite RB
- 1 RB2 + 2 lottery RBs
- 6-8 WRs (4 startable + 2-4 lottery)
- 1-2 QBs
- 1-2 TEs
- 4-5 picks

ZERO RB FINISH:
- 0-2 RBs from startup (mid-late rounds)
- 4-6 lottery RBs from picks/waivers
- 8-10 WRs (5-6 startable + lottery)
- 2-3 QBs
- 1-2 TEs
- 5-6 picks

## THE OFTEN-IGNORED HYBRID
Many winning dynasty teams use a HERO + ZERO HYBRID:
- Take one elite RB rounds 1-2.
- Then go full Zero RB the rest of the way.
- Hammer WRs/QBs/TEs in rounds 3-10.
- Lottery RBs in rounds 11+.
- Result: 1 anchor RB + WR-rich roster.

## DYNAMIC ADJUSTMENT (IN-SEASON)
- If your Hero RB underperforms: pivot to Zero RB approach by trading him for a young WR.
- If your Zero RB strategy hits a Year-2 RB jackpot: lean into RB depth via 1-for-2 trades.
- DON'T stay rigidly committed to a strategy if circumstances change.

### COMMON MISTAKES BY STRATEGY

ROBUST RB:
- Holding old RBs too long.
- Not selling at peak production.
- Reaching for RBs in middle rounds of rookie drafts.

HERO RB:
- Getting attached to your Hero RB past his peak.
- Not investing enough in WR depth.
- Drafting "Hero RB lite" (an OK RB instead of a true elite).

ZERO RB:
- Refusing ANY RB pick in rookie drafts (creates total reliance on waivers).
- Skipping breakout-candidate veterans available cheap.
- Not having enough rookie picks to sustain RB churn.

ANCHOR RB:
- Treating an RB drafted at 1.10 as your "anchor" - he's not.
- Anchors must be top-6 RBs.

### PRACTICAL HEURISTICS

1QB Dynasty (12-team, PPR):
- Hero RB beats Zero RB on 5-year championship odds.
- Robust RB underperforms both.

SF Dynasty (12-team, PPR):
- Zero RB performs slightly better than 1QB version.
- QBs become so important that early RBs cost you premium QB slots.

TEP Dynasty:
- Hero RB still works.
- Adds an early TE element to make sure you don't punt the position.

## WAIVER WIRE & ZERO RB SUSTAINABILITY
Zero RB dynasty REQUIRES active waivers. Each year:
- 2-4 RBs emerge from RB2/RB3 status to RB1 production.
- These are the lifeblood of zero-RB rosters.
- Set FAAB aside ($30-50% of budget) for in-season RB strikes.

## PSYCHOLOGY OF DRAFT STRATEGY
- Pick a strategy BEFORE the draft.
- Stick with it through the draft (don't pivot mid-round).
- Re-evaluate after the draft, not during.
- The discipline of a clear strategy beats the agility of "mixing it up."
