DYNASTY RUNNING BACK STRATEGY
==============================
Sources synthesized: Dynasty League Football RB content, Fantasy Footballers RB life-cycle, RotoBaller dynasty RB articles, FantasyPros dynasty RB tiers, FTN Fantasy RB strategy, Sharp Football RB analysis.

THE FUNDAMENTAL TRUTH ABOUT DYNASTY RB
--------------------------------------
RB is the FASTEST-DECAYING asset in dynasty. Average career production window is roughly 3-5 useful years for fantasy. The peak window is 23-26 years old, and almost no RB returns elite production after age 28. Treat RB as a depreciating commodity, not a long-term asset.

RB ARCHETYPES

A) ELITE WORKHORSE (CMC, Bijan, Saquon archetype)
- 250+ touches with 60+ catches.
- Top-3 dynasty value among RBs.
- 3-year window of premium production.
- Trade value: elite, but DEPRECIATES 2x faster than elite WR.

B) BELL-COW WITHOUT THE TARGETS (Henry-archetype)
- 280+ carries, 30 catches.
- High floor in non-PPR; capped ceiling in PPR.
- Fantasy value declines fast at 28+.

C) PASS-CATCHING SPECIALIST (Pass-down RB)
- 50+ catches, ~150 carries.
- PPR floor; non-PPR ceiling caps.
- Value highly dependent on PPR setting.

D) ROTATIONAL COMMITTEE (RB2/RB3 in shared backfield)
- 12-15 touches per game.
- Volatility week-to-week.
- Trade value low except as injury insurance.

E) HANDCUFF (Backup to a star RB)
- Direct insurance value.
- Low value unless starter is injury-prone.
- Shouldn't be paid more than rookie 3rd value typically.

F) AGING DECLINING (28+ with proven track record)
- Production may persist, but value plummets.
- Sell at peak production - not after.

G) ROOKIE LOTTERY TICKET (NFL R3+ in good landing spots)
- Stash on taxi or end of bench.
- Year 2-3 break-out potential.

THE AGE 27/28 CLIFF
-------------------
The most well-documented age effect in fantasy football. RBs at 27-28 years old, even those coming off elite seasons, have a high probability of meaningful production decline within 12-18 months. Sell BEFORE 27th birthday.

RB DECAY STAT
-------------
- Years 1-3: ascending or breakout window.
- Years 4-5: peak typically.
- Year 6: first meaningful decline year for many RBs.
- Year 7+: rare to see startable RB production.

WORKLOAD ACCUMULATION TAX
-------------------------
A 25 yo RB with 800 career carries is closer to a 28 yo on the curve than a clean 25 yo. Track:
- Total career touches.
- High-touch seasons (300+ touch seasons accelerate decline).
- Years as primary back (years of contact).

RB DRAFT STRATEGIES (STARTUP)

A) HERO RB (one elite + delay)
- Round 1: take an elite RB (Bijan/Saquon tier).
- Rounds 2-5: hammer WRs.
- Rounds 6+: depth + lottery RBs.

B) ZERO RB DYNASTY
- Skip RB until rounds 4-7.
- Hammer WRs/QBs early.
- Build RB depth via trades + lottery rookies.
- High-variance but young roster bias.

C) DUAL-RB OPENER (less popular in 2026)
- 2 RBs in first 3 rounds.
- Aggressive win-now bias.
- Risk: aging out fast if both are 25+.

D) PAY THE PREMIUM (contender tilt)
- RB1 in round 1, RB2 in round 4-5.
- Front-loaded RB value.
- Best for SHORT-WINDOW contenders.

ROOKIE DRAFT (RB FOCUS)
-----------------------
- RBs in NFL R1-R2 with good landing spots = 1.01-1.06 picks.
- RBs in NFL R3-R4 with good landing = 2nd round picks.
- Late RBs = 3rd-4th round dart throws.
- Workload at college matters - 200+ carries with high YPC vs SEC defense is the gold standard.

LANDING SPOT IS HUGE FOR RBS
----------------------------
RB's NFL landing spot is the #2 predictor of fantasy success after draft capital. Look for:
- Vacated workload (departing starter).
- Run-heavy offense.
- Strong OL.
- No major competition.
- HC committed to a workhorse style.

RB BUY-LOW SIGNALS
------------------
- 25-and-under RB on uninspiring offense.
- RB whose owner is rebuilding and dumping.
- RB coming off injury with clear path to lead role.
- Rookie RB drafted to good situation but trapped behind a Year-1 vet.
- Year 2 RB whose rookie season was buried by veteran starter who's now FA.

RB SELL-HIGH SIGNALS
--------------------
- 27+ RB just had RB1 season. SELL IMMEDIATELY.
- RB coming off career-high in carries.
- RB on team that drafted a rookie RB in NFL R1-R2.
- RB hitting free agency with declining team interest.
- RB whose volume is propped by injury to teammate (regression coming).

RB-SPECIFIC TRADE TACTICS
-------------------------
- Always sell RBs WHILE PRODUCTION IS LIVE. The market will pay for last 4-game success more than career profile.
- Trade RBs to contenders mid-season. They're desperate; you have inventory.
- Don't pay 1st-round picks for 28+ RBs. Buy aging RBs with 2nd-round picks or vet+pick combos.
- Handcuffs are usually overrated. Don't trade more than a 4th rookie pick for a handcuff.

RB DEPTH PHILOSOPHY
-------------------
Counterintuitive: dynasty rosters should have FEWER RBs than other positions. Reasons:
1. RB market is volatile - you can't predict the breakouts.
2. Streaming RB2/RB3 from waivers is viable.
3. Roster spots are better used on WR depth.

Recommended RB count: 4-6 RBs total (vs 8-12 WRs).

THE "ONE LANE" RULE
-------------------
The only RBs worth paying premium for are those in clear, uncontested workhorse roles. The moment a backfield has 2+ legitimate touch-getters, value FRAGMENTS to 60% of expected.

THE "OWNER GHOST" PHENOMENON
----------------------------
Owners who emotionally bond with their RBs lose value over time. The RB they refuse to sell at 26 ages into a 28 yo asset that fetches 30% of original value. RBs are the position where COLD discipline pays the most.

POST-HYPE 2nd/3rd-YEAR RBS
--------------------------
Most RBs reveal their ceiling in Year 1-2. RBs who haven't shown by Year 3 RARELY break out in Year 4. Don't hold post-hype RB lottery tickets past Year 3 without a specific opportunity catalyst.
