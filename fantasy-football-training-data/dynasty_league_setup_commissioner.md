# DYNASTY LEAGUE SETUP - COMMISSIONER GUIDE
Sources synthesized: CBS Sports startup setup guide, Pro Football Network optimal setup, Footballguys commissioner help, FantasyLife "5 Tips for Starting," PlayerProfiler beginner guide, FFPC rules pages, Masters Fantasy Football League rules.

## THE COMMISSIONER'S MANDATE
A dynasty commissioner is responsible for keeping a league running for 5-10+ years. The job is 80% facilitation and 20% rule arbitration. The single most important factor is ASSEMBLING THE RIGHT LEAGUEMATES - not the rules.

## LEAGUE FORMATION CHECKLIST
1. ASSEMBLE 10-14 OWNERS who actually want to play 10+ years.
2. Choose PLATFORM (Sleeper recommended for free; FFPC/RealitySportsOnline for paid).
3. Set BUY-IN tier appropriately (pick a stake everyone can afford for years).
4. WRITE THE CONSTITUTION before the startup draft.
5. Hold the STARTUP DRAFT (most consequential single event in league history).
6. Set REGULAR-SEASON CADENCE (lineups, waivers, trade deadline).

## CONSTITUTION ESSENTIALS
- League size and roster size (settled BEFORE startup).
- Scoring (every detail).
- Starting lineup.
- Trade rules: deadline, pick window (current + 2 years standard), veto policy.
- Waiver rules: FAAB amount, continuous vs weekly.
- Taxi squad rules: spots, eligibility, deadline, retention years.
- IR rules.
- Playoff structure: # teams, # weeks, byes, last week (15 or 17).
- Tie-breakers: standings (record, points for, points against...).
- Dispute resolution: collusion procedure, vote thresholds.
- Buy-in / payout structure.
- Replacement owner protocol (orphan handling).
- Off-cycle dropouts: how do we handle a team that quits mid-season?

## LEAGUE SIZE
- 10 teams: lighter, more weekly stars, easier waiver wire. Best for casual or small-money leagues.
- 12 teams: the gold standard. Optimal balance of depth, scarcity, and competitive dynamics.
- 14 teams: significantly harder, deep dynasty preferred. Bench depth premium.
- 16+ teams: hardcore deep-dynasty. Rookie 4ths and 5ths actually matter.

## ROSTER SIZES
- 22-23: tight. Aggressive churn.
- 25-27: standard.
- 28-30 + 4-6 taxi: full dynasty experience.
- 30+ + taxi + IR: deep dynasty.

### SCORING - WHAT TO DO

ALWAYS:
- Set 1.0 PPR or 0.5 PPR (no zero-PPR in modern dynasty).
- 6 pts for all TDs.
- 0.1 per rush/rec yard.
- Yards per pass: 0.04 standard, 0.05 in QB-friendly leagues.

OFTEN RECOMMENDED:
- Tight End Premium: 1.5 PPR for TE OR 0.5 bonus per TE rec.
- 6 pts for pass TD (instead of 4) - boosts QB scoring.
- First-down bonuses (nice flair, requires platform support).

OPTIONAL (advanced):
- Big-play bonuses (40+ yard play = +5).
- Yardage tiers (100+ yard rusher = +5).

### STARTING LINEUP

1QB: QB / 2RB / 2WR / 1TE / 1-2 FLEX / K / DST (or none).
SF: QB / 2RB / 2WR / 1TE / 1 FLEX / 1 SF / K / DST.

The best modern setup: SF + TEP. Forces QB and TE both to be premium positions.

## TRADE RULES
- Pick window: current year + 2-3 future years.
- Veto: 75%+ league vote required for veto. Or commish-only with bias toward letting trades stand.
- Trade deadline: Week 11 or Week 12. Avoid Week 13+ (collusion risk).
- "Same-year-pick" rule: clarify whether 2026 1st can be traded once 2026 rookie draft has started.
- Future picks: typical max is 2 years out. Some leagues do 3 (more flexible).

## WAIVERS
- FAAB ($100 default, $1,000 for finer granularity). FAAB is best practice.
- Continuous waivers (always run on add) over weekly cycles.
- Tiebreaker: rolling reverse standings is fairest.

## TAXI SQUAD
- 4-6 spots, rookies-only, 2-3 year retention.
- Deadline: Week 1 of regular season (most common) or 1 week after rookie draft.

## PLAYOFFS
- 4 teams, weeks 15-16-17 OR 6 teams, weeks 14-17 with byes.
- AVOID Week 18 (NFL teams rest starters; chaos).
- Top-2 byes incentivize regular-season excellence.

## BUY-IN / PAYOUT
- $50-$100: casual/social.
- $100-$250: intermediate stakes; common.
- $250-$500: high-stakes; engagement very high.
- $500+: premium leagues with deep commitments.

Payout structure (12-team, $250 buy-in = $3,000 pot):
- 1st: $1,500 (50%)
- 2nd: $750 (25%)
- 3rd: $300 (10%)
- Regular-season points leader: $200 (~7%)
- Most points one week: $100 (~3%)
- Last place "shame fund" or league dues forward: $150 (5%)

## ORPHAN PROTOCOL (CRITICAL FOR LONGEVITY)
- Define what "abandoned" means (e.g., 4 weeks of no lineup changes during regular season).
- Put orphan teams on the market via friends/league community before public listing.
- New owners should match buy-in level.
- Some leagues require new owners to "earn" their first FA waiver via small fee.

## DEAD-CAP / CONTRACT LEAGUES
Optional advanced layer:
- Each player has a salary, contract length.
- Bidding contracts in startup auction.
- Annual cap holds each owner accountable.
- Recommended for advanced leagues only (massive complexity).

## IDP (INDIVIDUAL DEFENSIVE PLAYERS)
Optional alternative to team DSTs:
- Adds 4-8 defensive starters to lineup.
- Doubles roster construction complexity.
- Niche but engaged community of players.

## LEAGUE LONGEVITY TIPS
1. Keep buy-in stable for 3+ years. Don't change rules or fees often.
2. Minimum side bets (most points week, etc.) keep regular-season alive for non-contenders.
3. Loyalty bonuses (small payouts after 3+ year tenure).
4. Active commissioner communication via league chat.
5. End-of-season recap post (top 3, awards, "rebuild of the year").
6. Annual rules vote with 2/3 threshold to prevent rule churn.

## COMMON COMMISSIONER MISTAKES
- Frequent rule changes mid-season.
- Allowing collusion to slide (the league's death sentence).
- Vetoing trades too aggressively (kills trade volume).
- Not codifying tiebreakers (creates feuds).
- Not having an orphan protocol when a manager quits.
- Not setting clear payment deadlines (carrying owners destroys leagues).

## THE LEAGUE-DEATH SIGNAL CHECKLIST
A league is dying if:
- 3+ owners haven't traded in a year.
- League chat is silent for weeks at a time.
- Multiple unfilled lineup slots in a week.
- Commissioner stopped enforcing rules.
- Buy-ins not collected on time.

If 3+ of these, the league is in danger. Save it now or merge with another.

## THE LEAGUE-HEALTH SIGNAL CHECKLIST
A league is healthy if:
- 50+ trades per year.
- Active league chat with banter and trade negotiations.
- All lineups set on time.
- Annual rules discussion is robust.
- Multiple competitive teams each year.
- Returning owner rate > 90%.
