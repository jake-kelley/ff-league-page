# DYNASTY DEVY LEAGUES - COLLEGE PROSPECT STRATEGY
Sources synthesized: Draft Sharks devy rankings, PFF devy top 75, Dynasty Football Factory devy rankings, Dynasty League Football devy content, Dynasty Nerds Devy 101, FantasyPros devy primer.

## WHAT IS DEVY
"Developmental" league. You draft college players, hold them through college (no scoring from college games typically), and they convert to your dynasty roster when they declare for the NFL Draft. It's an early-binding option on future fantasy production.

## DEVY VARIANTS
- PURE DEVY: hold college players, no scoring until NFL.
- C2C (CAMPUS-2-CANTON): score college games AND NFL games. Active scoring for college years.
- HYBRID: own college rights + score selected college games (e.g., bowl games).

## WHY DEVY EXISTS
- Get earlier shot at top prospects than rookie drafts.
- Reward college film study and prospect knowledge.
- Stack the same prospect across multiple years (devy + rookie draft).
- Add an extra layer of strategy / prospect betting.

## DEVY DRAFT TIMING
- Late summer / early fall typical.
- Some leagues stagger drafts: devy in summer, rookie in May.
- New devy added each year as freshmen become eligible.

### DEVY EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

### PRIORITY 1: NFL DRAFT CAPITAL EXPECTATION
- Will this prospect be drafted in NFL R1? That's the strongest signal.
- 5-star recruits are drafted in NFL at ~60% rate (very predictive).
- Top-25 college recruits hit NFL R1 frequently.

### PRIORITY 2: COLLEGE PRODUCTION
- Market share at the position.
- Production against top defenses.
- Breakout age (younger = better).

### PRIORITY 3: ATHLETIC PROFILE
- Combine projections (40, vertical, agility).
- Frame size for position fit.

### PRIORITY 4: SCHEME / SYSTEM
- Spread offenses inflate WR stats; pro-style offenses better predict NFL transition.
- Power-5 conferences > Group of 5 typically.

### PRIORITY 5: SCHEDULE STRENGTH
- Production vs SEC/B1G/ACC matters more than vs lesser conferences.

### POSITION-BY-POSITION DEVY

QUARTERBACKS:
- Avoid over-drafting QBs.
- Recent classes have shown high miss rates on highly-touted college QBs.
- Best bets: rushing-upside QBs at top-20 college programs.
- A devy QB with NFL R1 capital projection is meaningful; lottery otherwise.

RUNNING BACKS:
- Top-end devy RBs are typically the best ROI.
- Productive freshmen/sophomores at premier programs are gold.
- Avoid late-round RBs without elite athletic markers.

WIDE RECEIVERS:
- Most consistent devy hits.
- Look for: dominator rating, target share, breakout age <20, NFL R1 trajectory.
- Wide receiver is the position where devy edge is largest.

TIGHT ENDS:
- Slow developing position. Devy stash matches well.
- Rare to find a generational TE in devy; most miss.

## DEVY TRADING
- Devy assets trade like deeply discounted dynasty assets.
- Top devy WR at year before NFL declaration: ~50-70% of rookie 1.01 value.
- 2-3 years from NFL: 25-40% of rookie 1.01 value.
- More uncertainty = bigger discount.

### DEVY MISTAKES TO AVOID

### 1) OVER-EVALUATING COLLEGE PRODUCTION
- Big stats vs MAC opponents are misleading.
- Always check competition.

### 2) IGNORING NFL DRAFT CAPITAL TRAJECTORY
- A 5-star recruit who's underwhelming = still better odds than a 3-star producer.
- NFL-caliber traits matter more than college stats.

### 3) HOLDING PROSPECTS TOO LONG
- If a player is clearly NOT going NFL R1-R3, sell or cut.
- Don't bag-hold devy assets through senior years for hope.

### 4) EMOTIONAL ATTACHMENT TO YOUR ALMA MATER
- Don't draft based on team affinity.

### 5) UNDER-DRAFTING POSITIONS YOU NEED
- If your dynasty roster needs WRs, prioritize WR devy too.

## DEVY ROSTER MANAGEMENT
- Hold limit: most leagues cap devy roster at 4-12 spots.
- Forced declares: most leagues force the player off devy when he declares for NFL Draft (then he goes to rookie draft).
- Cut without penalty in most leagues.

## DEVY VS ROOKIE DRAFTS - WHICH IS BETTER?
- Devy = earlier capture, more uncertainty, longer hold.
- Rookie draft = later capture, more certainty, shorter wait.
- Smart strategy: own a small devy stable (3-4 elite prospects you'd happily roster) AND maintain rookie pick stockpile.
- Don't sacrifice rookie picks for devy unless prospect is elite.

## DEVY-SPECIFIC TIME ARBITRAGE
- Devy values typically RISE as a player approaches NFL declaration.
- Buy in mid-college (sophomore year), sell in junior year if value spiked.
- Conversely: a top devy who has a bad junior year is a buy if you believe the underlying.

## C2C-SPECIFIC STRATEGY (COLLEGE SCORING)
- Production now matters - draft producers, not just future bets.
- Older college players with high production carry weekly value.
- Mix immediate scorers (older) with future bets (younger).

## DEVY VS PRO ASSETS - WHEN TO TRADE BETWEEN
- Devy can be traded for pro players in most leagues.
- A top devy WR (year out from NFL) ~ rookie 2nd round pick value.
- Lower devy assets ~ rookie 3rd-4th value.
- A breakout devy season can spike value 2-3x.

## DEVY CAVEATS / WARNINGS
- Injuries in college are a real risk (no protection).
- Eligibility issues can derail prospects.
- NFL is harder than college; even elite prospects bust.
- Devy DOES NOT guarantee a draft position - the prospect must declare and you must own him.

## THE ANTI-DEVY VIEW
Some dynasty owners argue devy is sub-optimal:
- Long hold periods with no scoring (in pure devy).
- Roster spots tied up.
- Higher variance than rookie picks.

Counter: devy is often a sub-game for engaged players. If your league has it, take advantage; if not, don't agitate to add it.
