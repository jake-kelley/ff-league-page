# DYNASTY WIDE RECEIVER STRATEGY
Sources synthesized: Footballguys "New Reality" WR aging series, Fantasy Footballers WR life-cycle, Dynasty Nerds breakout WRs, FantasyPros dynasty WR tiers, ESPN/PFF WR aging studies, FTN Fantasy WR strategy.

## WR IS THE CORE OF MODERN DYNASTY
WR is the position with the BEST aging curve, the LARGEST liquid market, and the DEEPEST tradable depth. Modern dynasty rosters built around young WRs (24 and under) consistently outperform RB-heavy rosters across 5-year windows.

### WR LIFE CYCLE

YEAR 1 (rookie):
- Most rookies underwhelm. Even NFL R1 WRs average ~ 50-55 catches/720 yards.
- Some break out (Puka, JSN-types) but it's rare.
- Don't overreact to rookie season production.

YEAR 2 (sophomore):
- THE BREAKOUT YEAR for most WR1-tier prospects.
- ~50% of NFL R1-R2 WRs with 600+ rookie yards become Top-24 WRs by Year 2.
- ~30% of "year 2 breakout" candidates actually break out.
- Buy-low Year 1 -> sell-high Year 2 is the classic win.

YEAR 3 (junior):
- Year 3 is the second-best breakout window.
- WRs with 60+ targets in Year 2 break out 50%+ of the time in Year 3 regardless of Year-2 efficiency.
- Late-blooming WRs are still possible (Stefon Diggs / Cooper Kupp templates).

YEARS 4-7 (prime):
- Peak production years.
- Top WRs maintain top-12 production through age 28-29.
- Trade value is highest here AND most stable.

YEARS 8+ (aging):
- Decline begins. ~10% YOY production decline post-peak.
- Cliff: most WRs see WR1/WR2 finish probabilities crater after 30.
- Truly elite WRs (Hopkins-type, Jefferson/Chase trajectory) push the cliff to 31-32.

### WR ARCHETYPES

### A) ALPHA WR1 (Jefferson, Chase, Lamb, ADP top-10)
- 25%+ market share, 130+ targets/year.
- Long-term anchor (8-10 year careers possible).
- Trade value: top-of-board.

### B) ASCENDING YOUNG WR (24-and-under, NFL R1-R2 capital, ascending)
- The MOST APPRECIABLE asset in dynasty.
- Buy aggressively. Hold through breakout.

### C) PRODUCTIVE VETERAN (28+ with steady WR1/WR2 finishes)
- 2-3 year window before cliff.
- Sell-high candidate the moment production peaks.

### D) POSSESSION/SLOT WR (PPR-friendly, low-aDOT)
- High floor, capped ceiling.
- Value steady but unspectacular.
- Better in PPR than 0.5 PPR.

### E) DEEP THREAT (vertical specialist)
- High variance week-to-week.
- TD-dependent often.
- Value rises with QB upgrades.

### F) POST-HYPE SLEEPER (Year 4-5 WR who hasn't broken out)
- BUY-LOW candidates if landing spot opens.
- Avoid otherwise; year 4 is past breakout window for most.

### G) ROOKIE LOTTERY (NFL R3-R7 capital with athletic upside)
- End-of-bench/taxi stash.
- Year 2-3 breakout window.
- Most fail; the ones who hit pay 30-50x.

### KEY METRICS FOR WR EVALUATION
- Target share / market share (>25% = elite signal).
- Air yards share.
- aDOT (average depth of target).
- yards/route run (efficiency proxy).
- Red zone targets.
- Career breakout age (when did dominator status hit).
- Pre-NFL Draft capital.

### DRAFT STRATEGIES (STARTUP)

### LOAD-UP AT WR (most common winning approach)
- 4-6 WRs in first 8 rounds.
- Mix of ascending young (3-4) + 1-2 productive vets.
- Build the longest-window position aggressively.

### YOUNG WR ANCHOR
- 2 WRs in first 3 rounds, both 24-and-under elite.
- Provides dual-anchor stability.
- Hardest to displace in rebuild/contend pivots.

### VETERAN VALUE TARGETING (contender bias)
- 28-30 yo WR1s who are systematically undervalued.
- 2-3 year window of elite production at discount.
- Sell-high the moment they put up another WR1 finish.

## ROOKIE DRAFT (WR FOCUS)
- WRs are the highest-hit-rate position in rookie drafts.
- 1.01-1.06 picks: top WR prospects with NFL R1 capital + good landing spots.
- 2nd round picks: NFL R2 WRs with breakout-age signals.
- 3rd round: NFL R3+ lottery WRs with athletic profiles.

## WR BUY-LOW SIGNALS
- Year-2 WR with NFL R1-R2 capital, sub-50 catches as rookie.
- Productive vet WR (60+ catches) at age 28-30 on a "fading" team.
- Post-injury WR coming off ACL.
- WR who got buried by hot rookie WR on same team (panic over-correction).
- WR on team that just changed QBs to upgrade.
- WR coming off bad weather / scheme stretch.

## WR SELL-HIGH SIGNALS
- WR on hot 4-game streak driven by unsustainable TD rate.
- Aging WR (29+) just put up a top-10 season.
- Hyped post-hype darling who finally had a 70-catch season at age 27.
- WR who just landed in a "perfect" landing spot via FA (market overprices).
- WR on team where a rookie WR just got drafted in NFL R1.

## WR DEPTH PHILOSOPHY
The ONE position where depth pays off:
- Carry 8-12 WRs.
- Lottery rookie WRs hit at high enough rate to matter.
- WR market is wide and active - you can always trade WR.
- Bench depth at WR provides bye-week flexibility.

## LATE-BLOOMER DETECTION
Some WRs break out at age 26-27 after 3-4 years of sub-WR3 production. Common triggers:
- New QB.
- New OC/HC.
- Injury to former target hog.
- Team change via FA.

These are arbitrage opportunities. Watch for:
- WRs in their final rookie contract year on bad teams.
- WRs with strong underlying numbers (yds/route, target share trending up) but weak counting stats.
- WRs whose teams just upgraded QB or OC.

## WR PRINCIPLE - "TARGET SHARE > YARDS"
A 22% target share on a low-volume offense is more predictive of long-term success than 1,000 yards on a high-volume offense. Look at SHARE not raw stats.

## WR-SPECIFIC TRADE TACTICS
- Trade vets at end-of-October before defenses tighten.
- Buy buyers late-November - contenders pay premium for proven WRs.
- Trade rookie WRs in May-June BEFORE training camp narratives compress prices.
- Always know your "next sell" young WR. Liquidity matters.

### WR-SPECIFIC MISTAKES TO AVOID
- Refusing to sell aging stars. WR cliff is later than RB but it's real.
- Drafting too many "post-hype" WRs (Year 4+ with no breakout).
- Ignoring rookie WRs in NFL R1-R2 because they're "blocked." Talent endures.
- Falling in love with one team's WR room - too much exposure.
