DYNASTY ROSTER CONSTRUCTION - DEEP GUIDE
=========================================
Sources synthesized: Dynasty League Football "Roster Construction 101" + "Dynasty GAAP Memo: Roster Construction, Stability and Player Liquidity", Dynasty Nerds "Trade Secrets: Roster Construction", Footballguys "Faceoff: Dynasty Strategy", FTN Fantasy 2025 startup roster construction, FantasyPros build-a-dynasty-roster guides.

THE THREE PILLARS OF A DYNASTY ROSTER
-------------------------------------
1. PRODUCTIVE CORE (40-55% of roster value): Players who score weekly fantasy points right now. These are the lineup-week winners. They MUST be there or you lose every week regardless of how prospect-rich your bench is.
2. APPRECIATING ASSETS (25-40% of roster value): Young players whose value will rise. 22-24 year-old WRs, second-year RBs with growing roles, ascending QBs, draft picks.
3. LIQUIDITY (10-20% of roster value): Tradable mid-tier veterans, end-of-bench lottery tickets, rookie picks. The thing you can move WHEN, NOT IF, an opportunity arises.

THE INSULATING-RISK PRINCIPLE
-----------------------------
Dynasty roster risk is multiplicative. If your two RB1s are both 27 yo, an injury to one collapses your team because the other is also fragile. Insulate by mixing risk profiles:
- Old + young at every starting position.
- Boom-bust + steady at WR.
- Pass-heavy + run-heavy offense exposure.
- Multiple offenses (don't have 3 starters from the same NFL team unless they're all elite).

ROSTER ALLOCATION TARGETS BY PHASE
----------------------------------
CONTENDER (1QB, 25-roster):
- 1-2 elite QBs (or 1 elite + 1 stash)
- 4-5 startable RBs (2 stud + 2 flex + 1 handcuff)
- 7-9 startable WRs (3 stud + 3 flex + 2 lotto young)
- 1-2 starting TEs (1 stud OR 2 mid-tier with matchup advantage)
- 2-3 rookie picks (mostly later picks, since you don't pick early)

REBUILDER (1QB, 25-roster):
- 1 starter QB (mid-tier dual-threat) + 1 ascending young QB
- 2-3 RBs total (do not invest in old RB depth, the position evaporates fast)
- 9-12 young WRs (the backbone of every successful rebuild)
- 1-2 lottery TEs (24 and under)
- 4-6 rookie picks across multiple drafts

RELOAD/RETOOL (mid-pack):
- The rarest and trickiest. 50% productive, 50% youth + picks. You MUST decide by week 5 whether you become a contender or rebuilder before deadline; don't end the season as a 7-7 team holding old vets.

POSITIONAL DEPTH PHILOSOPHY
---------------------------
QB (1QB):
- 2 is typical. 3 only if SF or you have free roster spots. Do not roster 4+ unless you're hoarding for a specific trade target.

QB (SF):
- 4 is the floor. 5-6 is ideal. Streaming a 3rd starter is acceptable in deeper leagues.

RB:
- Unique among positions: chasing depth is a value trap. The "3rd RB" market is so volatile that paying for depth almost always loses to streaming + handcuffing your top RBs.
- Own a starter, his handcuff, and 1-2 lottery tickets. Don't invest more.

WR:
- The ONE position where depth pays. Carry 8-12 WRs. The market is wide and active; lottery tickets hit at high enough rate to matter.

TE:
- Own 1 stud OR 2 mid-tier. Avoid 3 mid-tier TEs - they all underperform mid-round price.

PICKS:
- Always own at least 2 rookie picks per upcoming draft. 3-4 if rebuilding, 1-2 if contending.

PLAYER LIQUIDITY (DLF GAAP MEMO concept)
----------------------------------------
A "liquid" player is one you can trade quickly at fair value. A "stuck" player can be on calc charts for 7,000 but you can't move him for 4,500. Causes of stuck assets:
- Polarizing player profiles (one big year, then crash)
- Aging vets right before the cliff
- Players on perceived bad situations
- Niche players (gadget/3rd down only)

Solution: bias toward LIQUID assets when picking between equally valued options. Liquid = 24-26 yo WR1, top-12 QB in his prime, productive young TE. Illiquid = 30 yo elite WR, post-hype WR2, situational RB.

ROSTER MAINTENANCE RHYTHM (12-MONTH CALENDAR)
---------------------------------------------
JAN-FEB: Buy-low season. Players whose teams missed playoffs are at value bottom.
MAR (NFL Free Agency): Largest single value-shift event of the year. Every team's WRs reprice.
APR (NFL Draft + rookie drafts): Rookie pick values crystallize. Last chance to flip picks for vets.
MAY-JUN: Hype season. Sell post-hype darlings before they disappoint again.
JUL-AUG (training camp): Beat-reporter narratives create buy windows; sell hot camp stars who won't play.
SEP-OCT: Lineup focus, but watch for early sells (e.g., RB on hot 4-game start).
NOV: TRADE DEADLINE OPTIMIZATION. Most leverage of any week. Sell aging vets to contenders for picks.
DEC: Lineup focus, scout next year's rookies on the side.

ROSTER PRESSURE RELIEF VALVES
-----------------------------
When your roster feels stuck:
1. The 3-for-1 fix: trade three meh assets for one stud. Almost always positive long-term EV in dynasty.
2. The "drop-sale": trade end-of-bench player + small piece for a single useful asset. Worse than holding nothing.
3. The pick reset: convert old vets to picks; convert excess picks to one stud. Stay liquid.
4. The young-for-old swap: trade 2 lottery tickets for 1 productive vet you actually start.

THE "MY 9TH-BEST PLAYER" TEST
-----------------------------
Look at your 9th-best player on a 25-man roster. If he's startable, you're championship-tier deep. If he's a "could-start in a pinch," you're average. If he's "wouldn't start," you have surplus you should be trading.

THE SHARPE/SORTINO ANALOGY (advanced)
-------------------------------------
Think of your roster like a portfolio with risk-adjusted return. Each asset has expected value (calc value) and volatility (injury, role, age). Best dynasty rosters maximize expected production per unit of variance, NOT raw expected production. This is why studs win - they have high expectation AND lower variance than two flier-type alternatives.
