DYNASTY - NFL DRAFT CAPITAL REFERENCE
=======================================
Companion file to dynasty_rookie_scouting_guide.txt and dynasty_landing_spot_evaluation.txt.

Sources synthesized: PFF historical hit-rate studies; Hayden Winks/Underdog
draft capital research; Footballguys mortality tables; Sharp Football Rich
Hribar capital analyses; long-running r/DynastyFF community studies; The
Athletic Dane Brugler "The Beast" prospect grades.

THE THESIS
----------
NFL Draft capital is the SINGLE STRONGEST PREDICTOR of fantasy success
for skill-position rookies. It outweighs college production for most
positions. NFL teams have access to medical files, character interviews,
private workouts, and team-specific scheme fit info that public scouts
don't have - and the cumulative wisdom of all 32 NFL teams pricing a
prospect on draft day is enormously informative.

Dynasty owners who weight NFL Draft capital correctly outperform owners
who chase college stats or "their guys" by 15-25% annually.

================================================================
INTERPRETING THESE NUMBERS
================================================================

1. ROUND-OVER-ROUND DROP
   Each round downward roughly halves the hit rate. The gap between R1 and R2
   is enormous - meaningfully larger than the gap between R2 and R3.

2. WITHIN-ROUND VARIANCE
   Top-10 picks > 11-20 picks > 21-32 picks. NFL teams use top picks more
   selectively, often forgoing them unless conviction is high. So slot-within-
   round matters.

3. TEAM-SPECIFIC PICK INVESTMENT
   Teams that traded UP for a player commit more organizationally; they will
   often play him through struggles. Teams that drafted "best available" at
   their slot may bench faster.

4. POSITION-SPECIFIC INTERPRETATION
   - WR: most hit rates compound nicely with draft capital.
   - RB: capital matters MORE than expected (despite RB volatility).
   - QB: capital is critical because team commitment dictates playing time.
   - TE: capital + slow development means hit rates are lower across the board.

================================================================
HOW TO USE THIS IN DYNASTY ROOKIE DRAFTS
================================================================

THE BASELINE RULE
- A NFL R1 prospect at 1.06 of dynasty rookie draft is a better bet than
  an NFL R3 prospect at 1.04, regardless of pre-draft consensus.

THE OUTSIZED IMPACT OF NFL R1
- ~50-65% of NFL R1 skill players become at-least-once-Top-24 dynasty
  starters.
- This means hoarding NFL R1 rookies (via dynasty 1st-round picks) has
  positive expected value over a 5-10 year window.

THE MID-ROUND TRAP
- NFL R3-R4 prospects are often hyped in dynasty rookie drafts due to
  athletic profile or college production.
- But their hit rate is barely 10-20%.
- Don't pay 1st-round dynasty pick prices for NFL R3+ rookies.

THE LATE-ROUND LOTTERY
- UDFAs and NFL R6-R7 picks have <5% hit rate.
- Worth a 3rd-or-later dynasty rookie pick if they're truly compelling
  and landed in a vacated role.

================================================================
DRAFT CAPITAL EXCEPTIONS / CAVEATS
================================================================

1. RBs in TOO-CROWDED LANDING SPOTS
   Even NFL R1 RBs miss when landing spots are bad. Always check
   depth chart context.

2. WRs DRAFTED INTO STACKED ROOMS
   NFL R1 WRs going to teams with 2 established WR1s have lower YEAR-1
   value but still good long-term value (5-7 years). Don't downgrade
   too much.

3. QBs DRAFTED TO BAD TEAMS
   Top-3 NFL Draft QBs usually go to bad teams. They have a longer
   ramp-up but generally hit at 40%+ rates because of organizational
   commitment.

4. TEs DRAFTED IN R2 TO TE-FRIENDLY OFFENSES
   These outperform their R2 baseline because the scheme fit unlocks
   production. R2 TEs to TE-heavy schemes can hit at R1-equivalent rates.

5. UDFAs WHO LAND IN PERFECT SPOTS
   The exception that proves the rule. Once-per-class an UDFA emerges
   into a starter (Brock Purdy, Tyler Higbee historically). Don't
   expect this; appreciate it if it happens.

6. NFL R7 / UDFA TIGHT ENDS
   Some emerge after 3-4 years (Tyler Higbee template). The pattern
   is rare but recurring. Worth a roster spot for 2-3 years if
   athletic profile is strong.

================================================================
COMPARING DRAFT CAPITAL TO OTHER SIGNALS
================================================================

WHEN DRAFT CAPITAL > COLLEGE PRODUCTION
- A NFL R1 WR with 700 college yards beats a NFL R3 WR with 1,200 yards.
- NFL teams have superior info; trust their grade.

WHEN DRAFT CAPITAL = COLLEGE PRODUCTION (TIE)
- Both are R1 with similar college production -> use age + athletic
  profile + landing spot as tie-breakers.

WHEN COLLEGE PRODUCTION > DRAFT CAPITAL (RARE)
- A R3 WR with elite college production AND elite athletic profile AND
  early breakout age MAY have been undervalued by NFL teams (medical
  flag, character concern, scheme bias).
- These can be sneaky picks but the hit rate is still lower than NFL R1.

================================================================
NFL DRAFT CAPITAL OVER TIME
================================================================

DOES IT DECAY?
- The signal weakens slightly as the player's NFL career progresses.
- After 3 NFL seasons, current production matters more than draft slot.
- Until then, draft capital is the dominant signal.

WHEN TO STOP WEIGHTING DRAFT CAPITAL
- Year 4+ of NFL career: stats > draft slot.
- After 2 NFL seasons of nothing: the player has likely missed.
- Exception: position-specific late-bloomers (TEs especially).

================================================================
PRACTICAL APPLICATION CHECKLIST
================================================================

Before any dynasty rookie draft pick, check:

1. NFL Draft slot (round and overall pick number).
2. NFL team draft history (do they hit on this position type?).
3. Trade-up vs trade-down by NFL team (organizational commitment).
4. Combine RAS / athletic profile (validates the NFL grade).
5. Pre-draft media consensus vs actual NFL Draft slot (gaps signal info).
6. Landing spot quality (separate from draft capital but both matter).

================================================================
THE SUMMARY HEURISTIC
================================================================

WHEN IN DOUBT, DRAFT CAPITAL WINS.

In ties, prefer:
1. Higher NFL Draft slot.
2. Top-10 over rest of R1.
3. R1 over R2.
4. R2 over R3.
5. R3 over R4.

Building your dynasty rookie tiers around NFL Draft capital - then
adjusting modestly for landing spot and college production - is the
single highest-EV strategy in dynasty rookie drafts.

This rule has held for 20+ years of dynasty fantasy football and is
unlikely to change.
