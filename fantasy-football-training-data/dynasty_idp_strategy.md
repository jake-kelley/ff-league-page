# DYNASTY IDP (INDIVIDUAL DEFENSIVE PLAYER) STRATEGY
Sources synthesized: The IDP Show beginner guide, IDP+ articles, Dynasty League Football "What Is An IDP League," Dynasty Nerds IDP intro, Draft Sharks IDP best strategy, RotoBaller IDP mock drafts, RotoWire IDP rankings, IDynastyP rankings.

## WHAT IS IDP
A league where you draft Individual Defensive Players (instead of or in addition to team DSTs). You set weekly defensive lineups much like offensive ones.

### IDP IS HARDER THAN STANDARD DYNASTY
- More positions to evaluate.
- Defensive scoring varies more by scheme.
- Beat-reporter info on defensive players is scarcer.
- Most owners don't put in IDP work, creating arbitrage.

### COMMON IDP LINEUP STRUCTURES

A) BASIC IDP (4-6 starters)
- 1-2 LB
- 1 DL
- 1-2 DB (S/CB)
- 1 FLEX (any defensive position)

B) BALANCED IDP (8-10 starters)
- 2-3 LB
- 2 DL
- 2-3 DB
- 1 FLEX

C) DEEP IDP (10-15 starters)
- 3-4 LB
- 3-4 DL
- 3-4 DB
- 2 FLEX

### D) FULL DEFENSE (every position)
- DL, LB, S, CB all required.
- Most complex. Heavy ownership.

### SCORING SYSTEMS

### TACKLE-HEAVY (most common)
- 1-1.5 points per solo tackle.
- 0.5 per assist.
- 4-5 per sack.
- Linebackers and safeties dominate.

### BIG-PLAY HEAVY
- Higher rewards for sacks, INTs, FFs.
- DL pass-rushers shine.
- Cornerbacks gain more value.

### TACKLE FOR LOSS BONUSES (TFL)
- 1-2 bonus points for TFL.
- Boosts middle/strong-side LB.

### POSITION VALUE BY SCORING

LINEBACKERS:
- Highest scorers in tackle-heavy.
- Off-ball LBs get 100+ tackles in a season.
- Top LB1s often outscore Top RB1s.

DEFENSIVE LINE:
- Pass-rushers (edge): variable based on sacks.
- Interior: high-floor in tackle-heavy.
- Sack-only scoring devalues; tackle bonus inflates.

DEFENSIVE BACKS:
- Safeties: tackle volume + INTs.
- Cornerbacks: variable; INT-dependent.
- Slot CBs: more tackles.

### POSITION SCARCITY

LBs: top 5-10 produce well above replacement.
DL: top 5 elite, drop-off after.
DB: deeper position; more replacement-level options.

### DRAFT STRATEGY

ROUND-BY-ROUND APPROACH:
- Top LBs early (rounds 4-6 of dynasty startup).
- Edge rushers mid-rounds.
- Safeties / depth late.

ROOKIE DRAFTS:
- IDP rookies are LATE rookie picks (4th-5th rounds).
- Some IDP leagues have separate IDP rookie drafts.

### VETERAN VALUE
- Most IDP managers undervalue defensive vets.
- 28+ LBs/DBs producing at LB1 level can be acquired cheap.
- Dynasty IDP advice: don't be as age-strict as offensive positions.

### KEY METRICS FOR IDP

LINEBACKERS:
- Tackles per game.
- Snap share (3-down player or rotational?).
- Weak-side vs middle (matters for scheme).

DEFENSIVE LINE:
- Snap counts (rotational vs every-down).
- Pressure rate (pass-rush ability).
- Sack production.

DEFENSIVE BACKS:
- Snap share.
- Tackle volume (safeties).
- INT trajectory.

### SCHEME / TEAM CONTEXT
- Defensive scheme: 4-3 vs 3-4 changes LB types.
- Offensive performance: bad teams' defenses get more snaps and tackle volume.
- Coordinator changes: huge impact, like NFL DC switches mid-season.

### IDP-SPECIFIC TRADE PATTERNS

### OFFENSE-FOR-DEFENSE
- Many leagues separate the markets.
- Some owners under-invest in IDP and overpay for offense.
- Trade your IDP surplus for offensive starters.

UPGRADE LB1 vs DEPTH
- Top-3 LB is a true differentiator in tackle-heavy formats.
- Depth at LB is replaceable.

### ROOKIE IDP DRAFT (when leagues separate)
- Top picks usually go to ATH-tier college LBs.
- DEs with high sack production also early.
- Cornerbacks RARELY worth 1st-round IDP picks.

### WAIVER WIRE IDP

### SCHEME-CHANGE BENEFICIARIES
- New DC = new role for some defenders.
- Watch for snap-share changes.

### INJURY REPLACEMENT
- LB injuries elevate backups quickly.
- Use FAAB on emerging LB starters.

### ROOKIE FA ADDITIONS
- UDFA rookies who emerge week 1-2.

### POSITION-SPECIFIC HEURISTICS

LBS:
- Off-ball, every-down LB on bad team = top-5 production.
- 3-4 ILB > 4-3 OLB usually.

EDGE RUSHERS:
- Pure pass-rushers volatile.
- Hybrid LB/DE in big-play scoring excels.

DTS:
- Cheap; rotational; low value.
- Avoid as primary roster pieces.

SAFETIES:
- Free safety usually higher tackle volume than strong.
- Some strong safeties (slot/box hybrid) also score well.

CORNERBACKS:
- Top CBs minimal tackles (avoided).
- Slot CBs get more tackles.
- Most CBs are CB3-tier; don't chase.

### IDP MISTAKES

### 1) IGNORING SCHEME
- 4-3 vs 3-4 changes everything.

### 2) OVERPAYING FOR EDGE
- Sacks are volatile.
- Tackle volume more reliable.

### 3) UNDER-INVESTING IN LB
- LBs are the lifeblood of tackle-heavy IDP.

### 4) DRAFTING DLINE EARLY
- Often poor ROI vs LBs.

### 5) IGNORING ROOKIE DEFENDERS
- Some are immediate IDP starters.

## LEAGUE SETUP TIP (IDP)
For new IDP players, start with BASIC IDP (4-6 defensive starters). Adding deep defensive lineups is a major complexity jump that turns off many owners.

## WHY IDP IS WORTH IT
- Dimensional gameplay (defense is half the football game).
- Less competition for value (most owners don't optimize IDP).
- Rookie defensive prospect evaluation is a great offseason hobby.
- Differentiation in long-running leagues.

## WHY IDP IS NOT WORTH IT (CON CASE)
- More work for similar payoff.
- Information asymmetry: hard to find current data.
- Defensive scheme volatility (one DC change destroys players).
- Some owners feel "fantasy is offense; defense is for NFL teams."

If your league is currently DST-only and you're considering IDP, the migration is non-trivial. Plan a 2-year transition.
