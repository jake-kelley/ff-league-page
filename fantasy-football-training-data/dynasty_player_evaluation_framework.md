# DYNASTY PLAYER EVALUATION FRAMEWORK
Sources synthesized: PFF prospect grading, Hayden Winks model, Dynasty Football Factory rankings, Sharp Football evaluation criteria, Footballguys "Mortality Tables," PlayerProfiler advanced metrics, Dynasty Nerds tier-based analysis.

### THE EIGHT FACTORS OF DYNASTY VALUATION

When evaluating a player for dynasty, consider EIGHT primary factors:

1. PRODUCTION (current and trajectory).
2. TALENT (underlying ability).
3. AGE (current and projected window).
4. SITUATION (team, scheme, supporting cast).
5. HEALTH (injury history + projection).
6. ROLE (workload, snap share, target share).
7. POSITIONAL VALUE (how scarce is his position?).
8. LIQUIDITY (can you actually trade him?).

Trade calculators give you ONE number that bundles 1-7 imperfectly. Real dynasty evaluation requires looking at ALL EIGHT.

### FACTOR 1 - PRODUCTION

CURRENT STATE METRICS:
- Career year-by-year fantasy points.
- Last 4-game performance.
- Year-over-year delta.

KEY PRODUCTION SIGNALS:
- ASCENDING: producing more each year.
- PEAKING: at career-high.
- DECLINING: producing less than 2 years ago.
- BUSTING: never matched expectations.

TRAJECTORY MATTERS MORE THAN LEVEL.

### FACTOR 2 - TALENT

EVALUATING UNDERLYING ABILITY:
- Athletic profile (combine, speed, strength).
- College production market share.
- NFL Draft capital.
- Pre-NFL projections.
- Coaching/teammate quotes.

TALENT IS DURABLE. A talented player in a bad situation often emerges; a low-talent player in a good situation rarely sustains.

### FACTOR 3 - AGE

PEAK AND CLIFF BY POSITION (REPEAT):
- QB: peak 28-32, cliff 36-37.
- RB: peak 23-26, cliff 27-28.
- WR: peak 25-28, cliff 30-31.
- TE: peak 26-29, cliff 31-32.

ADJUST EVALUATIONS BASED ON DISTANCE FROM CLIFF:
- 4+ years from cliff: full premium.
- 2-3 years: standard valuation.
- 1 year: discount 15-25%.
- Past cliff: discount 40%+.

### FACTOR 4 - SITUATION

DIMENSIONS:
- Offensive system (pass-heavy / run-heavy).
- HC/OC stability.
- QB quality (matters for WR/TE/RB).
- OL quality (matters most for RB).
- Target share competition (matters for WR/TE).

SITUATIONS CHANGE. A bad situation today may be a great one in 18 months.

### FACTOR 5 - HEALTH

DURABILITY METRICS:
- Career games missed.
- Recurring injury patterns.
- Soft-tissue vs structural.

PROJECTION:
- Players with 2+ multi-game injuries average 30-40% more games missed yearly going forward.
- Single major injury (ACL) = 1-2 year recovery curve, then back to baseline.
- "Always healthy" tag durable.

### FACTOR 6 - ROLE

CURRENT ROLE METRICS:
- Snap share (% of team plays).
- Target share (for receivers).
- Routes run %.
- Red zone usage.
- Workhorse vs committee status.

ROLE STABILITY:
- Stable across 2 seasons = real.
- Inflated by injury = regression risk.
- Reduced by free agency / trade = future projection.

### FACTOR 7 - POSITIONAL VALUE

SCARCITY MULTIPLIERS BY FORMAT (rough):
- 1QB: WR most valuable -> RB -> QB -> TE.
- SF: QB most valuable -> WR -> RB -> TE.
- TEP: TE jumps to top-3.

ADJUST RAW FANTASY POINTS BY POSITION SCARCITY.

### FACTOR 8 - LIQUIDITY

CAN YOU TRADE THIS PLAYER?

LIQUID PROFILES:
- Top-12 at position with current production.
- 24-and-under WR/RB.
- Top-3 QB regardless of age.

ILLIQUID PROFILES:
- 30+ aging vets (everyone discounts them).
- Niche players (gadget/3rd-down only).
- Polarizing post-hype WRs.
- Players with off-field issues.

TRADE FREQUENCY data (FantasyCalc) is a proxy for liquidity.

### EVALUATION TEMPLATES BY ARCHETYPE

### TEMPLATE: ASCENDING YOUNG WR
- Talent: top of dynasty board.
- Age: years before cliff.
- Production trajectory: rising.
- Situation: target share growth path?
- Role: full-time player?
- Health: durable?
- Position scarcity: high (esp WR).
- Liquidity: very high.

DYNASTY VERDICT: BUY/HOLD HEAVILY.

### TEMPLATE: AGING WORKHORSE RB
- Talent: proven elite.
- Age: 1-2 years from cliff.
- Production: peak.
- Situation: stable but how long?
- Role: workhorse confirmed.
- Health: workload concern.
- Position scarcity: high.
- Liquidity: moderate (everyone knows the cliff is coming).

DYNASTY VERDICT: SELL HIGH NOW.

### TEMPLATE: POST-HYPE YEAR-3 WR
- Talent: was high pre-NFL but unrealized.
- Age: still has window.
- Production: low.
- Situation: variable.
- Role: limited.
- Health: typically fine.
- Position scarcity: low (he's a WR3-4).
- Liquidity: low (nobody believes anymore).

DYNASTY VERDICT: BUY LOW IF CATALYST EXISTS.

### TEMPLATE: ELITE YOUNG QB (SF)
- Talent: top-3 NFL.
- Age: years from cliff.
- Production: ascending.
- Situation: stable franchise commitment.
- Role: starter for life.
- Health: Pro QB injury rate.
- Position scarcity: SF premium.
- Liquidity: extreme.

DYNASTY VERDICT: KEEP FOREVER OR ONLY MOVE FOR SHOCKING RETURN.

### EVALUATION PROCESS (DECISION TREE)

1. Identify player and ask: which archetype fits?
2. Score each of 8 factors 1-10.
3. Compare your score to calc value.
4. If your score > calc by 15%+ -> BUY candidate.
5. If your score < calc by 15%+ -> SELL candidate.
6. Within 15% -> HOLD or align to roster context.

### TRACKING YOUR EVALUATIONS

WHY: most owners don't track and never improve.

WHAT TO TRACK:
- Date of evaluation.
- Player + your score (1-100).
- Calc value at the time.
- Action taken (buy/sell/hold).
- 6-month and 1-year outcome.

REVIEW QUARTERLY. Identify patterns:
- Are you over-valuing rookies?
- Under-valuing aging vets?
- Missing breakout signals?

### EVALUATION MISTAKES

### 1) FOCUSING ON ONE FACTOR
- "He's young" or "he's productive" - both are partial views.

### 2) IGNORING SITUATION CHANGES
- A coach change can flip a player's projection.

### 3) RELYING ON CALC ALONE
- Calc doesn't know your league or his liquidity.

### 4) ANCHORING TO STARTUP DRAFT VALUE
- "I drafted him in round 2" - irrelevant now.

### 5) NOT UPDATING
- Your January evaluation may be wrong by April after FA + draft.

### ELITE EVALUATION HABITS

### 1. ANNUAL REBUILD OF RANKINGS
- January-February: rebuild your tier-based rankings from scratch.

### 2. WEEKLY UPDATES
- During season: tweak based on production, injuries, role changes.

### 3. POST-NFL-DRAFT REBUILD
- April: completely re-evaluate based on landing spots.

### 4. POST-CAMP TWEAKS
- August: incorporate camp narratives.

### 5. SEASON DEBRIEF
- December-January: identify your big misses; learn.

### SCALING THIS FRAMEWORK

EFFICIENT (limited time):
- Evaluate top 20 players on your roster + top 10 trade targets.
- Score 1-10 on top 4 factors.
- Calibrate.

THOROUGH (engaged owner):
- Full 8-factor scoring on top 50 players.
- Quarterly rebuild.

OBSESSIVE (analyst-tier):
- 200+ player rankings.
- Weekly updates.
- Track every adjustment.

THE GOAL: develop a personal valuation that DIVERGES from calc on enough players that you can systematically extract value.
