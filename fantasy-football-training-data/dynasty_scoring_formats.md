# DYNASTY SCORING FORMATS - HOW SCORING SHIFTS VALUES
Sources synthesized: KeepTradeCut TE Premium explainer, Comanagers TEP analysis, Pro Football Network SF/2QB primer, FantasyPros 2QB strategy, Dynasty Nerds rookie format guides, FantasyPros best ball strategy.

## THE FUNDAMENTAL POINT
Scoring isn't a flavor preference. It rewires the value of every position and every player. The same 75-catch / 850-yard / 4-TD WR can be the WR8 in TE Premium SF and the WR22 in standard 1QB. Adjust accordingly.

### SCORING FORMATS COMMON IN DYNASTY

### 1. STANDARD (NON-PPR)
- 0 points per reception.
- Heavily favors TD-dependent RBs and goal-line workhorses.
- Shrinks WR top-end relative to RB.
- Rare in modern dynasty - most leagues have moved away.

### 2. HALF-PPR (0.5 PPR)
- 0.5 points per reception.
- Compromise format. Slightly favors volume RBs vs standard. Slightly de-emphasizes elite WRs vs full PPR.
- Reasonable middle for new dynasty leagues.

3. FULL PPR (1.0 PPR) - the dataset format
- 1 point per reception.
- Inflates pass-catching RBs (Christian McCaffrey types) and slot/possession WRs.
- TEs gain because they catch shorter passes.
- The dominant format in dynasty 2026.

### 4. TIGHT END PREMIUM (TEP)
- TEs receive 1.5 PPR (or 0.5 bonus on top of 1.0 PPR).
- Boost intended to balance the scarcity at TE.
- Top-3 TEs jump multiple tiers, becoming top-30 overall assets.
- Volume TEs (high reception, low TD) gain MORE than TD-dependent TEs.
- Strategy: pay up for elite young TEs in startups; trade for them in season as they become differentiators.

### 5. SUPERFLEX (SF)
- A flex spot that allows a QB.
- Effectively a 2-QB league: nearly everyone starts 2 QBs every week.
- The most dramatic value shift of any format change.
- Top-12 QBs become equivalent to top-12 RBs in value.
- QB1s often go in the first 5 picks of startups; rookie QBs in good landing spots become 1.01 candidates in SF rookie drafts.
- Rookie drafts: rookie QBs are almost always a tier above their non-SF value.

### 6. 2QB (TRUE 2QB)
- 2 starting QB slots, no flex with QB option.
- Functionally similar to SF in value rewiring.
- Slightly more rigid: you literally cannot start 0 or 3 QBs.

### 7. SF + TEP COMBINED
- The "premium of premiums" format. Most ProFootballNetwork and FFPC dynasty leagues use this.
- Top QBs and top TEs become hyper-valuable.
- Rookie 1.01 in SF + TEP is almost always a QB (if the QB is a top-2 prospect).

### 8. PROS (POINTS PER FIRST DOWN, BIG-PLAY BONUSES)
- Less common, used in some Footballguys/dynasty-only leagues.
- Rewards possession and chunk plays.
- Shifts value toward route-runners and sustained-drive offenses.

### 9. BEST BALL DYNASTY (BBM, FFPC, Sleeper Hot Routes)
- No weekly lineup management; auto-optimal lineup.
- Rewards spike-week / boom-bust profiles. Vertical-threat WRs and explosive RBs gain. Possession safe-floor types lose.
- Late-round upside picks more important than in managed leagues.

## ROOKIE DRAFT FORMAT EFFECTS
- 1QB: rookie 1.01 is the best non-QB prospect (typically RB1 or WR1 of class).
- SF: rookie 1.01 is the best QB IF a top-tier QB exists; otherwise top WR/RB.
- TEP: elite rookie TEs jump 5-15 spots in rookie drafts vs non-TEP.

## LEAGUE DEPTH (NUMBER OF TEAMS)
- 8-team: thin starting requirements; depth doesn't matter; focus on top 50 players.
- 10-team (this dataset): standard. Bench depth matters less than 12-team. Streamers off waivers viable.
- 12-team: the gold-standard. Depth carries premium. Rookie 3rds and lottery picks have real value.
- 14+ team: extreme depth premium. Every roster spot has trade value. Bye-week management is hard.

## ROSTER SIZE EFFECTS
- 22 spots: tight. Rookie 3rds and 4ths approach zero value. You cut productive bench guys.
- 25 spots: standard for many dynasty leagues.
- 28-30 spots (with taxi): the "complete" dynasty experience. Picks 4 and 5 are tradable. Lottery tickets get fairly priced.
- 35+ spots (deep dynasty): every player worth real money has a roster spot. Free agency is dry.

## TAXI SQUAD EFFECTS
- 4-6 taxi spots = standard. Inflates rookie 2nds and 3rds because you can stash without an active roster cost.
- No taxi = compressed roster, rookie picks deflate.

## PRACTICAL TAKEAWAYS
1. Always confirm league scoring before applying any rankings or trade calc. Default settings on Sleeper/MFL/ESPN are NOT identical to your league.
2. The closer your scoring is to extreme (heavy TEP, deep SF), the LESS the public consensus rankings apply - you must adjust manually.
3. Best ball trade rules differ from managed dynasty - boom/bust value > floor value. Reverse for managed.
4. When considering a trade with another manager who normally plays a different format, recognize they may be miscalibrated to your scoring; this is a positive-EV opportunity.
