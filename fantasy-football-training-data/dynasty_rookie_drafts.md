# DYNASTY ROOKIE DRAFTS - COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
Sources synthesized: Dynasty Nerds rookie draft mastering, Dynasty League Football rookie value recap, FantasyPros rookie draft tiers (2026), Yahoo Justin Boone rookie pick value charts, FantasyLife startup-meets-rookie articles, RotoBaller rookie draft strategy, FTN Fantasy rookie content, Underdog Hayden Winks rookie analysis, PFF dynasty draft pick usage, Sharp Football rookie rankings.

## WHEN ROOKIE DRAFTS HAPPEN
- Late April through May, immediately after the NFL Draft.
- Sleeper, MFL, ESPN, Fleaflicker all support rookie-only drafts after the NFL draft.
- Some leagues run snake; some run linear (no reverse), and most go 4 rounds (1 round per major position).
- 5+ round drafts are common in deeper leagues with taxi squads.

## PICK VALUATION FRAMEWORK
Pick value depends on:
1. Year: current-year picks worth more than future picks (typically 1.5x more for 1 year out, 2x+ for 2 years out).
2. Round: round 1 picks are worth materially more than round 2, which is worth materially more than round 3 (geometric, not linear, decline).
3. Class strength: a deep class lifts mid/late round value; a weak class compresses everything to round 1.
4. League format: SF lifts QB-rich classes; TEP lifts TE-rich classes.
5. Slot certainty: an "early 1st" (1.01-1.04) is much more valuable than a "late 1st" (1.09-1.12), often 30%+.

## ROUND 1 vs LATER ROUNDS - HIT RATES
- Round 1 picks have 50-65% hit rate (top-24 finish at position within 3 years).
- Round 2 picks: 25-35% hit rate.
- Round 3 picks: 12-18% hit rate.
- Round 4 picks: 5-10% hit rate.
- Round 5+ are mostly lottery tickets / future flippable picks.

This means hoarding round 1 picks is mathematically the best strategy. Three round 2s is NOT equivalent to one round 1 in expected outcome.

## NFL DRAFT CAPITAL - THE BEST SINGLE PREDICTOR
The most predictive variable of fantasy success in rookie drafts is the player's NFL Draft position.
- NFL R1: highest hit rate; ~45-55% become fantasy starters.
- NFL R2: meaningful, ~25-35%.
- NFL R3: ~15-22%.
- NFL R4+: rapid decline; ~5-10% combined hit rate.

## DON'T LET LANDING SPOTS RUIN YOUR BOARD
- A great prospect in a "bad" landing spot is still likely to break out. Talent endures.
- Bad landing spots produce buy-low windows. The market overreacts to depth charts.
- "Great" landing spots get over-drafted. Be careful chasing perceived role over talent.

## PROSPECT EVALUATION FRAMEWORK (BROAD)
1. Production: dominator rating, market share, college breakout age. "Did he produce in college, especially against good competition?"
2. Athletic profile: combine/pro day metrics in context of position. Speed (40), explosiveness (vertical/broad), agility, size.
3. Translation skills: route running for WRs; vision and contact balance for RBs; arm strength + accuracy + decision-making for QBs.
4. Draft capital: where the NFL valued him.
5. Landing spot/opportunity: target share projection, depth chart, offensive system fit.
6. Age at declaration: younger is meaningfully better (early entry = pre-21 declaration is plus signal).

## ROOKIE WR EVALUATION
- Breakout age in college (when did he hit "dominator" status). <20 = elite signal. 20-21 = strong. 22+ = warning.
- Target share / market share final season. >35% market share = elite.
- Athletic threshold: 40 time meaningfully under 4.55 if not a sure-handed possession type.
- NFL Draft capital: top-50 pick = real signal.

## ROOKIE RB EVALUATION
- Workload at college: 200+ carries with high YPC vs SEC/B1G defenses = signal.
- Receiving usage: PPR-friendly RBs need pass-catching ability.
- NFL landing spot is even more predictive for RBs than WRs (depth chart is everything).
- Avoid RBs whose teams just signed a vet RB or have an entrenched starter.

## ROOKIE QB EVALUATION (SF MOST RELEVANT)
- Rushing upside floors fantasy production (Allen, Hurts, Jackson model).
- Top-10 NFL Draft pick = significant signal of organizational commitment.
- Year 2 is the breakout year for most QBs - rookie usage often suppresses value.

## ROOKIE TE EVALUATION
- Slowest position to develop. Year 2-3 breakouts are normal.
- Athleticism + receiving role in college are critical.
- TE Premium leagues: target TEs in NFL R1 even at TE3-TE4 in dynasty rookie draft.

### PICK TRADING STRATEGIES

A) TRADE BACK (Conservative): from 1.04 to 1.07 + 2.05. Acceptable when tier 1 has only 3 elite players or when your team is rebuilding and wants quantity.
B) TRADE UP (Aggressive): from 1.06 + 2.02 to 1.02. Acceptable when you've identified a clear-tier prospect and the gap to 1.06 is large.
C) PICK FOR PRODUCTION (Win-Now): trade 1.06 + 2.04 for a productive vet. Good for contenders if vet has 2+ year window.
D) FUTURE PICK ARBITRAGE: trade 2026 1.06 for 2027 1st. The 2027 class is stronger AND you don't yet know your slot - upside.

## ROOKIE DRAFT BOARD CONSTRUCTION
- Build tier-based, not slot-based. "These 3 players are interchangeable; these 5 next; etc."
- When your slot comes, pick the top of your active tier; if the tier has 1 left and there's a gap, consider trading back.
- Sleeper picks: identify 2-3 round 3-4 prospects you like more than consensus. These wins are how you get league-defining values.

## POST-DRAFT MAINTENANCE
- Keep an eye on training camp news for rookies you didn't draft. Year 1 waiver-wire pickups can become major assets if a starter gets hurt.
- Resist over-trading post-draft. Markets are inefficient for ~2 weeks post-rookie-draft as people overreact.
- Don't sell rookies for vets in season unless you're specifically a contender. Year 1 rookie value almost always rises year 2 if the player was at all productive.

## ROOKIE DRAFT MISTAKES TO AVOID
1. Drafting QBs early in 1QB leagues. QB hit rate is real but the position scarcity isn't there.
2. Refusing to draft a TE because "TEs take time." If you have the pick to spare and the TE is elite, the long ROI is great.
3. Drafting based on recent draft buzz instead of your own evaluation. Beat reporters move markets, but they're often wrong.
4. Drafting handcuffs in rookie drafts. Use rookie picks for upside, not insurance.
5. Skipping rookie picks entirely (giving them all away for vets) - you lose the option value.
6. Not trading rookie picks immediately when you can. Some rookie pick values plummet as the season approaches if the prospect underwhelms.
