# DYNASTY WAIVER WIRE STRATEGY (FAAB)
Sources synthesized: Draft Sharks dynasty waiver wire columns, Dynasty Nerds weekly waiver wire, FantraxHQ stash list, Pro Football Network top dynasty stashes, Dynasty League Football season-end stash columns.

## THE WAIVER WIRE IS A COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE
Active waiver-wire managers acquire 2-5 useful players per year that meaningfully impact rosters. Passive managers miss them. Over a 5-year window, this compounds to dozens of valuable assets - often the difference between a championship roster and a mediocre one.

### FAAB BUDGET PHILOSOPHY (TYPICAL $100 BUDGET)

ALLOCATION:
- Save 30% for "league-winning" strikes (one or two big claims per year).
- Spend 50% on regular weekly adds.
- Reserve 20% for late-season + dynasty-stash strikes.

NEVER SPEND MORE THAN 40% ON ONE PLAYER unless he's:
- Replacing a confirmed season-ending injured starter.
- A clear breakout with sustained role.

NEVER LET YOUR BUDGET HIT $0 BY WEEK 8.

### WAIVER PRIORITY TYPES

### A) IMMEDIATE STARTER PROMOTION (highest priority)
- Backup RB whose starter just tore an ACL.
- WR3 promoted to WR1 due to depth chart change.
- BID: 25-50% of FAAB.

### B) ROLE EXPANSION (high priority)
- Snap share trending up.
- New OC commits to player.
- BID: 10-25%.

### C) MATCHUP STREAM (medium priority)
- One-week start option.
- BID: $1-5.

### D) DYNASTY STASH (variable priority)
- Long-term project.
- BID: 5-15% if exclusive value.

### E) HANDCUFF SPECULATION (low priority)
- Backup to durable starter.
- BID: $1-3 (don't overspend).

### EARLY-SEASON FAAB STRATEGY (WEEKS 1-4)

### DON'T OVERSPEND
- Sample size is small.
- Many "breakouts" are noise.
- Save budget for confirmed trends.

### DO PURSUE
- RB1 backups when starter goes IR.
- Surprise role players showing 60%+ snap shares.
- Underrated rookies whose role just expanded.

### MID-SEASON STRATEGY (WEEKS 5-10)

### PEAK BUYING WINDOW
- Most breakouts crystallize here.
- Spend 30-50% of remaining budget on confirmed risers.
- Don't be afraid to bid aggressively on a clear winner.

### LATE-SEASON STRATEGY (WEEKS 11-17)

CONTENDERS:
- Stream by matchup.
- Add specific players for playoff-relevant weeks.
- Don't chase Year-2 stashes; you need Week 14-17 production.

REBUILDERS:
- Add Year-2 stash candidates.
- Drop your current vet rentals; bring up rookies for development.
- BID up to 20% for elite stash candidates - your future is here.

### ROOKIE STASH STRATEGY (POST-NFL DRAFT)

POST-DRAFT WAIVER PRIORITY:
- UDFA rookies who landed well = high priority.
- Rookies whose teammates departed = priority.
- Late-round rookies who flashed in preseason = late-season pickup.

USE TAXI:
- Don't waste active roster on stashes.
- Stash via taxi where possible.

### DROPPING FROM ROSTER

WHEN TO DROP:
- Player is 4+ years stale with no breakout signs.
- Player just lost his role permanently.
- Player is on a team with multiple younger replacements.

WHEN NOT TO DROP:
- Player is hurt (use IR instead).
- Player has 1-game role uncertainty.
- Player lost role temporarily but could return.

### SLEEPER WAIVER WIRE TIPS

CONTINUOUS WAIVERS:
- Set bids ahead; runs automatically.
- No need to be online at 3 AM Wednesday.

MULTI-ADD CONDITIONAL:
- Conditional drops = "drop X only if waiver claim wins."
- Use this to maintain optimal roster.

WATCHLIST FEATURE:
- Monitor 5-10 lottery players each week.
- Quick claim before opponents notice.

### FAAB BIDDING TACTICS

BID HIGHER THAN MARKET (slightly):
- If you really want a player, bid 5-10% above expected.
- The +$5 you're paying isn't important; missing the player is.

DON'T BID ON UNNEEDED PLAYERS:
- Some owners "bid for value" on players they won't start.
- Wastes FAAB.

THINK ABOUT THE PLAYER'S CEILING:
- A player whose ceiling is RB3 = $5-10 max.
- A player whose ceiling is RB1 = pay 30-50% of budget.

### CONTRACT-LEAGUE WAIVER NOTE

In contract leagues, waiver bids often set the player's contract:
- Bid amount = year 1 salary.
- 2-3 year length default.

This means waiver decisions are MORE significant - you're signing a contract.

### CONTENDER WAIVER PHILOSOPHY

PRIORITIZE: weekly starters who can hold up in playoffs.
AVOID: long-term stashes (you don't have time).
SPEND DOWN: don't end the season with $40 unused.

### REBUILDER WAIVER PHILOSOPHY

PRIORITIZE: long-term project players.
AVOID: rentals.
PRESERVE BUDGET: into late season for stash strikes.

### WAIVER MISTAKES

### 1) HOARDING FAAB
- Going $100 -> $80 -> $80 -> $80 by Week 12 = waste.

### 2) PANIC BIDDING
- Spending $40 on a 1-game spike player.

### 3) IGNORING DEEP LEAGUE WAIVERS
- 14+ team leagues, every waiver matters.

### 4) DROPPING ELITE STASHES
- Sacrificing Year-2 lottery for Week 7 stream.

### 5) NOT MONITORING DAILY
- Some adds happen Tuesday morning, not Wednesday.

### ELITE WAIVER WIRE HABITS

DAILY:
- Check NFL injury news.
- Update watchlist.

WEEKLY (PRE-WAIVER):
- Identify top 3 add targets.
- Set FAAB bids 24+ hours ahead.

POST-WAIVER:
- Adjust roster (drops, IR moves).
- Identify next week's likely targets.

### WAIVER NETWORK

LEAGUE CHAT:
- Watch for trade signals from opponents.
- Hint that you're shopping a player to plant ideas.

NFL NEWS SUBSCRIPTIONS:
- Beat reporter Twitter / X feeds.
- NFL injury reports (Wed/Thu/Fri).
- Sleeper has built-in news feed.

## THE STREAMING TANGO
For QB/TE/DST stream-eligibility, rotate weekly. Don't worship one streamer; cycle 3-4 candidates by matchup.

EXAMPLE QB STREAMING ROTATION (1QB):
- Week 5: QB-A vs bad-pass-D.
- Week 6: QB-B vs middling-D in shootout.
- Week 7: QB-C vs another bad-pass-D.

This gives you ~ 18-22 ppg from your QB2 slot - acceptable while saving FAAB.

## WAIVER OBSESSION CALCULUS
Time invested in waiver wire study = high ROI.
- 10-20 minutes per week of focused waiver study.
- Pays off in 2-5 league-altering acquisitions per year.
- Best use of dynasty time after rookie drafts.
