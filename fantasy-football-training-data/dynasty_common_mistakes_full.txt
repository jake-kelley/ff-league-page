A. ROSTER CONSTRUCTION MISTAKES

1. CARRYING TOO MANY OLD RBs
- 28+ RBs are depreciating fast. Holding 3-4 of them = 3-4 declining assets.

2. DEPTH AT WRONG POSITIONS
- 5+ TEs is wasteful. 4+ QBs in 1QB is wasteful.
- Depth at WR pays. Depth at TE doesn't.

3. NO YOUTH AT WR
- WR is the appreciating position. If you have zero 24-and-under WRs, your roster is aging.

4. NO RB ANCHOR
- Trying to start 4 RB2-tier guys instead of having one stud.
- Lineup ceiling caps too low.

5. ROSTERING HANDCUFFS WITHOUT THE LEAD BACK
- The handcuff is only valuable if you own the starter.
- Otherwise it's a lottery ticket diluting depth.

6. INSUFFICIENT PICK CAPITAL
- Trading away ALL future picks for production removes optionality.
- Always keep some pick stockpile.

7. AGE-CONCENTRATED CORE
- All starters 28+ = 2-year window before collapse.
- All starters 22-23 = 1-2 years before competing.
- Mix old + young at every position.

8. SAME-TEAM CONCENTRATION
- 3+ starters from same NFL team = correlated risk (injury to QB hurts WR1, WR2, TE).

================================================================
B. TRADE MISTAKES

9. ACCEPTING LOWBALL OFFERS BECAUSE "IT'S BETTER THAN NOTHING"
- Wait. Markets correct. Don't get bullied.

10. SENDING LOWBALL OFFERS
- Poisons negotiations. Open at fair-or-slight-tilt.

11. FALLING IN LOVE WITH "YOUR GUYS"
- Refusing to sell because you drafted them.
- Dynasty rewards COLD RATIONAL trades.

12. TRADING TOO MANY PIECES FOR ONE
- 3-for-1 only works for ELITE asset acquisition.
- 3 mid-tier for 1 mid-tier = depth loss for nothing.

13. TRADING THE WRONG PIECES
- Surplus pieces should leave; core pieces should stay.
- Common error: sending an ascending young player INSTEAD of a productive vet.

14. IGNORING LIQUIDITY
- Acquiring a "stuck" asset (high calc value, low real-world tradability).
- Better to take a 10% discount on a liquid player than full value on illiquid.

15. NEVER TRADING
- Passive owners lose 10-20% relative roster value yearly.
- Aim for 5-10 trades minimum per season.

16. PANICKING AND TRADING AT BOTTOM
- Selling a star after one bad game.
- Almost always a value loss.

================================================================
C. ROOKIE DRAFT MISTAKES

17. REACHING ON HYPE
- Drafting Top-50 rookie WR at 1.05 because of camp buzz.
- Stick to your tier sheet.

18. DRAFTING QB IN 1QB EARLY
- Rookie QB at 1.04 in 1QB rarely justifies the slot.

19. DRAFTING HANDCUFFS IN ROOKIE DRAFTS
- Use rookie picks for upside, not insurance.

20. IGNORING NFL DRAFT CAPITAL
- An NFL R6 RB at 2.05 of a rookie draft is almost always a miss.

21. OVERVALUING LANDING SPOT
- Drafting "perfect spot" rookies above better talent.
- Talent endures; situations change.

22. UNDERVALUING TOP TIER
- Trading 1.01 because "I have too many young pieces."
- The 1.01 holds 90%+ value 2 years later if hit.

23. NOT KNOWING YOUR PICKS
- Some owners don't even check what picks they own.
- Audit annually.

24. IGNORING ROOKIE DRAFT FOR VETS COMPLETELY
- Trading every pick for vets removes future talent infusion.

================================================================
D. STARTUP DRAFT MISTAKES

25. DRAFTING KICKER OR DST EARLY
- These should be last 2 picks, period.

26. LOADING OLD RBs MID-ROUND
- Round 5 RB at age 28 = depreciating asset purchase.

27. SKIPPING PICKS
- Some leagues let you "trade in" startup picks for vets pre-draft.
- Almost always a mistake unless rare opportunity.

28. CHASING ADP
- ADP is average; not an instruction.
- Pick from your tier sheet.

29. OVERPAYING FOR A "BIG NAME" PAST PRIME
- Trying to draft past-tier vets in rounds 1-2 because they were stars.
- Always check age + recent production.

30. NEGLECTING TE
- Only drafting K and DST; punting TE entirely is risky in TEP.

31. TOO MANY SAME-TYPE RBs/WRs
- Drafting 5 small slot WRs - high correlation risk.

================================================================
E. LINEUP / WEEKLY MISTAKES

32. SETTING LINEUPS LATE / FORGETTING
- Saturday/Sunday morning is too late if your platform locks early.

33. NOT CHECKING INACTIVES
- Players ruled out 90 minutes before kickoff destroy lineups.

34. OVER-FADING STUDS IN TOUGH MATCHUPS
- Start your studs every week.

35. UNDER-USING IR/TAXI
- Open roster spots = lost optionality.

36. STREAMING BLINDLY
- Use DvP, not gut feel.

37. NOT CLAIMING WAIVER WIRE
- Active managers gain 1-3 hits/year via WW.
- Passive managers miss them.

================================================================
F. STRATEGIC PHASE MISTAKES

38. STUCK IN THE MIDDLE
- 7-7 with old vets, no picks, no path.
- Force yourself to choose phase each offseason.

39. PREMATURE TEARDOWN
- Selling stars at first sign of struggle.
- Often you're closer to contention than you think.

40. ETERNAL REBUILD
- 5+ years of "rebuilding" without ever pivoting to contend.
- Set milestones; pivot when met.

41. TRADING FUTURE PICKS WHEN BAD
- Dumping a 2027 1st as a rebuilder = giving up your best asset.

42. REFUSING TO REBUILD
- Bad teams should sell, not stand pat.

43. TRADING WIN-NOW WHEN AGING
- Old roster + trading future for vets = doubles down on aging.

================================================================
G. COMMISSIONER MISTAKES

44. NO LEAGUE CONSTITUTION
- Disputes have no resolution path.
- Write rules BEFORE the startup.

45. INCONSISTENT RULE ENFORCEMENT
- Vetoing one trade and not another.
- Kills league trust.

46. ALLOWING COLLUSION TO SLIDE
- Single most damaging commissioner inaction.
- Once tolerated, league dies.

47. TOO MANY RULE CHANGES
- Yearly rule churn destabilizes.
- Maximum 1-2 rule changes per year.

48. NOT COLLECTING DUES
- Carrying owners destroys trust.
- Fees due before rookie draft.

49. NO ORPHAN PROTOCOL
- When a manager quits mid-season, chaos.

50. TOO LATE TRADE DEADLINE
- Week 14+ deadlines invite collusion (eliminated teams give to contenders).

================================================================
H. PSYCHOLOGICAL / RELATIONSHIP MISTAKES

51. PUBLIC TRADE COMPLAINTS
- Embarrassing leaguemates kills future trades.

52. ATTACKING REJECTED OFFERS
- "Why would you reject that?" - bad form.
- Move on; revisit later.

53. NOT RESEARCHING OPPONENTS
- Sending offers without knowing their roster needs.

54. TREATING DYNASTY AS REDRAFT
- 1-year mentality on multi-year roster.

55. CHASING SHINY ROOKIES YEARLY
- Rookie hype every year; some hit, most don't.
- Discipline > hype-chasing.

56. EMOTIONAL ATTACHMENT TO STARTUP STARS
- "I drafted him" is not a holding strategy.

57. IGNORING METRICS
- "Eyes test only" is a luxury for elite analysts.
- Use available data.

58. NOT TRACKING DECISIONS
- Owners who don't journal trades don't improve.

59. JEALOUSY-DRIVEN TRADES
- Trying to "match" another owner's move.
- Stay focused on your team.

60. OVERCONFIDENCE
- Won championship -> assumes can repeat without effort.
- Dynasty rewards continuous work.

================================================================

THE TOP 5 MOST COMMON MISTAKES (PER ANALYSTS)

1. UNDERVALUING ROOKIE PICKS - especially future picks.
2. OVER-TRADING FOR YOUTH at expense of competing roster.
3. STUCK IN THE MIDDLE - neither contender nor rebuilder.
4. NOT TRADING - passive owners lose ground.
5. IGNORING THE OFFSEASON - winners work year-round.

THE 5 MISTAKES MOST UNIQUE TO DYNASTY

1. Falling in love with startup picks.
2. Hoarding old RBs.
3. Refusing to rebuild after aging.
4. Skipping rookie drafts for vet trades.
5. Treating each year as redraft.

LEARNING FROM MISTAKES
----------------------
- Document every major decision (trades, draft choices) with rationale.
- Review at end of each season.
- Identify your top 3 errors of the year.
- Commit to one specific change for next year.
- Track results.

The owners who improve year-over-year do this. The owners who don't, repeat the same mistakes.
