# DYNASTY WIN-NOW vs RELOAD vs REBUILD - DECISION FRAMEWORK
Sources synthesized: RotoWire dynasty rebuild guides, The League Winners 6-step rebuild guide, Dynasty Nerds offseason calendar, Fantasy Points midseason rebuilding, Footballguys forum threads on "blow it all up," RotoBaller dynasty rebuild expert strategy, Draft Sharks dynasty strategy.

## THE THREE PHASES - DEFINITIONS
WIN-NOW (CONTENDER): You expect to compete for the title in the next 12-18 months. Your roster has elite production now and the championship odds are real (top 25-30% of league win-equity).

RELOAD (RETOOL): You expect to compete in the next 24-30 months. Your roster has good production but is aging or thin in spots. You need targeted moves to get back to contender.

REBUILD: You don't expect to compete this season; you're building for 2-3+ years from now. Your roster is youth-heavy, picks-rich, and production-light.

## THE WORST POSITION: THE MIDDLE
The single most-cited mistake across every dynasty source is "stuck in the middle" - 7-7 teams who think they can sneak into the playoffs with one tweak, then end the season older, with worse picks, and no closer to a title. The MIDDLE is the most expensive place to be in dynasty. Every offseason, force yourself to pick: contender or rebuilder. NOT BOTH.

## SIGNALS YOU'RE A CONTENDER
- 3+ top-12 starters at WR/RB combined.
- Top-5 QB in 1QB or top-12 SF QB.
- Avg starter age <28 at skill positions.
- Real bench depth at WR.
- 2+ unowned championships in your last 3 finals projections.
- Calc says you're top-3 in total roster value.

ACTION: trade picks for missing pieces. The 1.04-1.08 in next year's draft is a DEPRECIATING asset for you because you'll never be drafting that high.

## SIGNALS YOU'RE A REBUILDER
- More 30+ year-old starters than 25-and-under stars.
- Heavy-vet bench, missing rookie picks, no QB-of-the-future.
- Coming off a 4-13 season or worse.
- Roster value is bottom-3 in league.
- Inheriting a poorly built orphan team.

ACTION: trade everything 28+ for picks/youth. Tank purposefully if league rules allow. Plan a 2-year window with explicit milestones.

## SIGNALS YOU'RE A RELOADER (the hardest call)
- Coming off a deep playoff run that drained future picks.
- Roster value top-5 but aging.
- 1-2 elite young pieces + several 28+ vets.
- Realistic championship odds 10-20% (could win, but not favorite).

ACTION: identify 2-3 vets to flip for ascending youth, but DON'T do a full teardown. Maintain enough current production to be playoff competitive in year 2.

## THE 5-WEEK ASSESSMENT (IN-SEASON)
Multiple sources (Fantasy Points, RotoWire) align: by Weeks 4-6, you have enough data to know your team. If you're 1-4 with old vets and middling production - sell. If 4-1 with surging young pieces - hold and accumulate next year's picks. If 2-3 - DECIDE within 2 weeks: contender or seller.

### THE 12-MONTH OFFSEASON CALENDAR

### JANUARY (Post-Title)
- Honest team-value audit. Compare to league.
- Identify top 3 sell candidates.
- Identify top 3 buy targets.

### FEBRUARY (Combine)
- Rookie research deep dive. Most owners haven't ranked rookies yet.
- Begin trade outreach with "are you done for the year?" framing - rebuilders want out NOW.

### MARCH (NFL Free Agency)
- HUGE shift. Players reprice based on landing spots. Trade volume surges.
- Sell win-now vets to contenders before their team's FA shake-up.
- Target buy-low on players who landed in negative situations (panic discount).

### APRIL (NFL Draft)
- Largest single-event reprice of the year. Rookies, vets affected by draft picks.
- Sell rookies you don't want before rookie drafts crystallize their price.
- Target rookies in good landing spots BEFORE picks.

### MAY-JULY (Rookie Drafts + Slow Period)
- Lock rosters. Identify offseason hype overpriced players.
- Set training camp watchlist.

### AUG (Camp + Preseason)
- Beat-reporter narratives reprice players. Buy injury panic, sell camp hype.

### SEP-NOV (Season + Trade Deadline)
- Sept-Oct: lineup focus, weekly research.
- Oct-Nov: trade-deadline aggression.
- A contender should pay a 1.5-2x premium for proven vets at deadline.
- A rebuilder should accept a 1.3-1.5x premium for selling vets to contenders.

### DEC (Playoffs + Offseason Prep)
- Lineup hyper-focus.
- Begin scouting next year's rookie class.
- Plan post-season teardown if elimination.

## REBUILD EXECUTION (6 STEPS)
1. AUDIT: List every player + age + value. Identify 5+ vets to sell.
2. DUMP TRUCK: Trade aged vets for picks. Don't quibble on calc - just MOVE the assets. Quantity over quality of picks. Aim for 4-6 picks per draft.
3. PROSPECT: Identify 4-6 young players (24 and under) you want to acquire via picks/trades.
4. PICK MAXIMIZE: Stockpile 1sts and early 2nds. Trade 3rds and 4ths up for fewer better picks if possible.
5. TANK SMART: Lose intentionally late-season WITHOUT damaging your future production. Sit injury-questionable starters. Don't add WW pieces. (Note: some leagues penalize obvious tanking - check rules.)
6. RE-PIVOT: After 1-2 drafts of accumulating youth, identify when you're "rebuilt." Stop trading future for now and start integrating.

## WHEN TO RE-PIVOT FROM REBUILD TO CONTEND
- 4+ ascending young starters at skill positions.
- Roster value back in top half of league.
- Two consecutive offseasons of "wins" (good rookie pick hits + good buy-low purchases).
- A 1-2 year championship window visible.

ACTION at re-pivot: trade FAR-FUTURE picks for veteran upgrade. Now is when you transition.

## WIN-NOW EXECUTION
- Trade every pick beyond next year's 1st (and even that one, if needed) for proven production.
- Acquire WR3-tier weekly starters at premium - they're the marginal lineup difference makers.
- Roster handcuffs to your top RB.
- Streamline bench to functional pieces, not lottery tickets.
- Push trade volume in late October/early November when rebuilders are most willing.

## WIN-NOW DANGER ZONES
- "Two more years" syndrome. Many win-now teams convince themselves of a 3-year window. The window is usually 1-2 years before the cliff hits at 1+ position.
- Trading multiple firsts for one star. Keeps you from rebuilding if the title doesn't materialize.
- Ignoring the bench. A win-now team that can't survive an injury is a 1-week-away-from-rebuild team.

## REBUILD VS RELOAD HEURISTIC
A reload is a rebuild that starts from a higher asset base. If you have 1-2 elite young anchors AND can win 4+ games this year, you're a reloader. If neither true, you're a rebuilder. Be honest.

## THE 15% DELTA RULE
A useful gut-check: estimate roster total value vs league average. If you're 15%+ below average, you're a rebuilder. If you're 15%+ above, you're a contender. Anything in between - you're a reloader and the call is hardest. (Older write-ups quote 50%/30% bands; in practice real dynasty leaderboards rarely spread that wide, so 15% in either direction is the more useful threshold.)
