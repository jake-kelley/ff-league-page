# DYNASTY POST-CHAMPIONSHIP / POST-SUCCESS STRATEGY
Sources synthesized: Fantasy Life "What's Next After a Successful Season," Pro Football Network contender management, Footballguys championship retention articles.

## THE POST-TITLE PROBLEM
Winning a championship in dynasty is exciting - but it's also the moment when MANY managers make their biggest strategic mistakes. The roster that just won often has aging stars, depleted picks, and a 1-year window to repeat. Without disciplined post-title planning, you'll be a 6-8 team next year scrambling for a rebuild.

### STEP 1 - HONEST CHAMPIONSHIP ANALYSIS

How did you win?
- DOMINANT PATH: led regular season + cruised playoffs.
  - Implication: roster is strong, well-built; minor tuning needed.

- HOT STREAK / LATE SURGE: snuck into playoffs and got hot.
  - Implication: roster may be weaker than record shows; harder to repeat.

- LUCKY MATCHUPS: faced soft schedule in playoff weeks.
  - Implication: structural issues remain; need real upgrades.

Be HONEST. Skipping this step leads to over-confidence.

### STEP 2 - POSITION-BY-POSITION ROSTER ANALYSIS

QUARTERBACK (especially SF):
- Age + job security check for each QB.
- Identify QB1's window remaining.
- Review backup quality.

RUNNING BACK:
- Age cliffs especially fast at RB.
- Are your RB1s 26+? Sell windows narrow.
- Identify which RBs need replacement in 1-2 years.

WIDE RECEIVER:
- Best aging curve - probably stable.
- Identify any 30+ vets to consider trading high.
- Identify rising young WRs to keep.

TIGHT END:
- Are you over-paying mid-tier TEs?
- Is your TE1 30+?

PICKS:
- How depleted are you?
- Picks 2-3 years out are critical for sustaining.

### STEP 3 - LEAGUE-WIDE COMPETITIVE MAP

Build a spreadsheet:
- Team-by-team: what's their phase?
- Identify likely 2026 contenders.
- Identify likely 2026 rebuilders.

This map guides who to TRADE WITH:
- Trade with rebuilders to acquire vet production cheap.
- Trade with contenders to sell aging vets.

STEP 4 - SHORT WINDOW vs RELOAD DECISION

### OPTION A: GO BACK-TO-BACK (short window)
- Trade FUTURE picks for productive vets.
- Acquire QB-needy, RB-needy depth.
- Risk: 1-year window then crash.
- Reward: potential dynasty.

### OPTION B: RELOAD (extending the window)
- Trade aging vets for younger pieces + picks.
- Maintain core 24-and-under stars.
- 2-3 year contender window.
- Lower risk; lower instant ceiling.

### OPTION C: TROPHY HUNTING (one more push, then teardown)
- Push everything for one more title.
- Plan post-2026 teardown.
- Highest variance.

CHOOSE BEFORE FEBRUARY. Don't drift.

### STEP 5 - PLAN THE OFFSEASON CALENDAR

JANUARY-FEBRUARY:
- Sell-high candidates: 28+ vets coming off elite seasons.
- Buy-low targets: ascending youngsters who slumped late.

MARCH (NFL FA):
- Reprice your roster after FA.
- Sell vets who lost favorable situations.
- Buy youngsters who landed in upgraded spots.

APRIL (NFL Draft):
- Trade rookie picks pre-draft if you don't want them (peak value).
- Use rookie picks for selective adds (best landing spots).

MAY (Rookie Drafts):
- Don't reach. Stick to your tier sheet.
- Trade back if multiple options at same tier.

JUN-AUG:
- Identify Year-2 breakout candidates.
- Don't make panic trades.

### STEP 6 - REPEAT-CHAMPIONSHIP CHECKLIST

For another title run, your roster needs:
- 1-2 elite young anchors (pre-cliff).
- 2-3 productive vets (with at least 1 more elite year).
- Bench depth at WR.
- 1 stud TE OR strong streamer plan.
- Clean pick situation (at least 2 picks per upcoming draft).

If missing 2+ of these, you're a RELOAD candidate, not back-to-back contender.

### POST-TITLE TRADE TARGETS

THINGS TO ACQUIRE:
- 1-year-window superstars (28+ at WR, 26+ at RB).
- Productive QBs in their prime.
- Elite TE for weekly advantage.

THINGS TO TRADE AWAY:
- Future picks beyond 2 years.
- Excess rookie 2nds and 3rds.
- Bench depth you won't start.

## THE POST-TITLE TRAP
DO NOT:
- Refuse to trade because "the team won."
- Hold all your aging vets thinking they'll repeat.
- Trade YOUR YOUNG STARS for more vets.
- Make zero offseason moves.

ALL FOUR are predictable disasters that turn champions into 6-8 teams.

## THE 24-MONTH RULE
Look at every roster spot and ask: "Will he be a startable dynasty asset 24 months from now?"
- YES -> hold or extend.
- NO -> aggressively shop.

This question alone reveals 30-40% of your champion roster as future trade bait.

### CONTENDER OWNER PHILOSOPHIES (POST-TITLE)

A) THE DYNASTY-MINDED:
- Trades 1-2 declining vets for picks/youth.
- Maintains 80% of core.
- Keeps window open.

B) THE WIN-NOW MAXIMIZER:
- Trades all future picks for vets.
- 1-year shot.
- Plans rebuild after.

C) THE COMPLACENT:
- Makes minimal moves.
- Roster ages out.
- Worst long-term outcome.

THE CHOICE between A and B is acceptable. C is a slow death.

### CHAMPIONSHIP-RETENTION DATA POINTS

ANALYSIS OF MULTI-CHAMPIONSHIP ROSTERS:
- ~70% of repeat champions had at least 2 sub-25 anchor pieces.
- ~80% had 3+ rookie picks across the next 2 drafts.
- ~85% made 3+ post-title trades.

PASSIVE retention does not work in dynasty.

### POST-CHAMPIONSHIP MISTAKES (RANKED BY FREQUENCY)

### 1) NOT TRADING ENOUGH
- "I won; I don't need to do anything."

### 2) HOLDING AGING STARS TOO LONG
- "He won me a title; I'll keep him forever."

### 3) TRADING YOUNG ASSETS FOR MORE VETS
- "Double down on win-now."

### 4) IGNORING PICKS
- "I don't need rookies; I'm contending."

### 5) OVERESTIMATING CHAMPIONSHIP REPEATABILITY
- Variance is real; assuming you'll repeat is hubris.

### POST-CHAMPIONSHIP COMMUNICATIONS

LEAGUE CHAT:
- Be gracious. Don't gloat.
- Mention you're "looking to retool" - signals trade interest.
- Don't telegraph your moves.

WITH OPPONENTS:
- Some will try to "fleece the champion" with lowballs.
- Don't engage. Wait for fair offers.

## THE ULTIMATE POST-TITLE WIN
Repeat-champion seasons are RARE in dynasty (~5-10% chance any given title-winner repeats next year). The truly elite path is:
- Win this year.
- Strategically retool to 2-year contender (not 1-year all-in).
- Win again year 3-4.
- Repeat.

This pattern produces 2-3 titles in 5 years for the elite. Year-by-year all-in is too high variance.
