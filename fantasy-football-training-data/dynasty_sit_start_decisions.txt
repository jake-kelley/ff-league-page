DYNASTY-SPECIFIC SIT/START DECISIONS
=====================================
Sources synthesized: Dynasty Nerds weekly start/sit columns, KeepTradeCut Start/Sit Tool, Footballguys weekly cheatsheets, Dynasty Daddy lineup tools, FantasyPros expert consensus rankings.

WHAT MAKES DYNASTY SIT/START DIFFERENT
--------------------------------------
Most weekly sit/start advice is REDRAFT focused: maximize this week's points. Dynasty owners face additional layers:

1. DEVELOPMENT REPS: starting your rookie even if he's not the highest-projected gives him reps that grow value.
2. VALUE PRESERVATION: starting an aging vet you plan to sell can keep his perceived weekly value high.
3. TANK CONSIDERATIONS: late-season eliminated teams may have different optimal lineups (carefully - within rules).
4. INJURY PROTECTION: dynasty assets matter for years; risking them in meaningless games is bad EV.

FRAMEWORK: THE FIVE QUESTIONS
-----------------------------
For each lineup decision, ask:
1. WHO PROJECTS HIGHER THIS WEEK? (the redraft answer)
2. WHO HAS THE BETTER MATCHUP DELTA? (vs season average)
3. AM I IN PLAYOFF CONTENTION? (if not, dynamics shift)
4. DO I PLAN TO SELL EITHER PLAYER? (perception management)
5. IS THERE INJURY RISK FOR EITHER? (impact on dynasty value)

DYNASTY-SPECIFIC LINEUP DECISIONS

WHEN TO START THE LOWER-PROJECTED PLAYER

A) DEVELOPMENT REPS FOR ROOKIES
- Your rookie WR is a long-term asset.
- Starting him in matchups where he might score 8 PTS vs your vet's 12 PTS is a small weekly cost for development reps.
- Best when you're out of contention and the vet is plant-it scout-team material.

B) PERCEPTION MANAGEMENT
- You plan to sell vet WR this week or next.
- Starting him locks in his "weekly starter" perception.
- Even if your rookie projects similarly, START THE VET YOU'RE SELLING.

C) BREAKOUT BUILDING
- A young player (year 2-3) is showing breakout signs.
- Starting him through a slow week consolidates the breakout narrative for your eventual sell-high or your roster confidence.

WHEN TO SIT THE HIGHER-PROJECTED PLAYER

A) INJURY RISK
- Your top RB is questionable but expected to play.
- Game is meaningless to your standings.
- Sit him - protect the dynasty asset.

B) HE'S BEING TRADED
- You've already agreed to a trade pending lineup.
- Sit him to avoid an injury that voids the deal.

C) LATE-SEASON IRRELEVANCE (REBUILDER)
- You're 3-9, no playoff path.
- Top-12 RB is on the IR/Q list; not worth the risk.
- Sit and play your healthy bench.

POSITION-SPECIFIC LINEUP HEURISTICS

QB:
- Start your top QB always (no matchup-fading QB1s).
- For QB2/streamer: matchup matters more (DvP, total).
- In SF: start your healthiest 2 QBs unless one is on a clear bye.
- Mobile QBs have safer floors against bad defenses.

RB:
- Workhorses (18+ touches) in good matchups: must-start.
- Committee backs: matchup-dependent. Use snap share trends.
- Pass-catching RBs: start in PPR if their team is trailing-projection.
- Goal-line specific RBs: start in TD-rich matchups (red zone projections).

WR:
- Target share trumps matchup for top WRs.
- Slot WRs: matchup-dependent against zone defenses.
- Vertical threats: matchup-dependent against deep defenses.
- Start WRs with high TPRR (targets per route run).

TE:
- Stud TEs: must start every week.
- Mid-tier TEs: matchup-dependent (game total, opponent vs TE).
- Streamers: start anyone facing a bad TE defense.

K/DST:
- Stream by matchup against weak offenses (DST) or domes (K).

ADVANCED SIT/START METRICS TO USE

TPRR (Targets Per Route Run)
- WR/TE specific. >0.20 = startable.

I-20% (Inside 20 Rushing Share)
- RB-specific. >35% = TD upside.

EXPECTED FANTASY POINTS / GAME (xFP/G)
- Volume-based projection. >12 = startable.

OPP DvP (Defense vs Position) WITH SCHEDULE ADJUSTMENT
- Crucial. Use schedule-adjusted DvP, not raw.

PRESSURE-SPLIT QB DATA
- Mobile QBs vs blitz-heavy defenses = floor protection.
- Pocket QBs vs blitz-heavy defenses = bust risk.

GAME SCRIPT PROJECTIONS
- Total + spread → project pass volume.
- High total / close spread = pass volume = WR/TE upside.
- Low total / blowout = QB/WR fade in losing scripts.

BYE WEEK PLANNING (DYNASTY EXTRA LAYER)

PROACTIVE STREAMING
- Identify byes weeks early (October).
- Pick up streamer QB/TE/D 2 weeks ahead, not reactively.

DON'T OVERPAY FOR BYE-WEEK FILLERS
- A bye-week starter is a 1-week investment.
- Use waiver-wire claims, not trades.

TRADING FOR BYE COVER (rare but real)
- If your QB1's bye week aligns with a contender's QB1 bye AND he has 3 startable QBs, you might trade for a 1-week loaner.

PLAYOFF MATCHUP PLANNING (WEEKS 14-17)

SCHEDULE STUDY EARLY (October)
- Identify who has soft Week 14-17 schedules.
- Buy these players if available.
- Sell players with hard playoff schedules.

PEAK OUTPUT IN PLAYOFFS
- Start your STUDS regardless of matchup.
- Don't matchup-chase in playoffs - regression.

DEFENSE MATCHUPS BECOME MORE PREDICTIVE LATE
- By Week 14, defenses have established patterns.
- DvP is most predictive at this point.

INJURY MANAGEMENT (DYNASTY)

SHORT-TERM INJURY (1-2 WEEKS)
- IR if eligible to free up roster spot.
- If not eligible: start replacement; hold the injured player.

LONG-TERM INJURY (3+ WEEKS)
- Definitely IR.
- Consider trading for a replacement (depth helps long-term).

SEASON-ENDING INJURY
- IR for the duration.
- Don't sell injured stars at fire-sale prices.
- Their dynasty value is mostly intact for next season.

WAIVER DECISIONS - DYNASTY CONTEXT

CHASING POINTS (current week):
- Streamer who'll start next week.
- Use FAAB sparingly: $5-10 per stream.

CHASING ROLE EXPANSION (multi-week):
- Backup who just got promoted.
- Use FAAB more aggressively: $15-30%.

CHASING LONG-TERM VALUE (dynasty stash):
- Rookie who flashed.
- Use FAAB significantly: $20-40%.
- Worth burning a roster spot for 4-6 weeks to evaluate.

DYNASTY-SPECIFIC STREAMERS

QB STREAMER
- 1QB league: rotate by matchup using QB13-20 range.
- SF: only stream if you have a clear QB3 with role; don't stream blind.

TE STREAMER
- Bottom-tier TEs vs bottom-tier TE defenses.
- Goal: 8-10 PTS replacement.

DST STREAMER
- Always stream against bottom-5 offenses.
- Drop after week of use.

K STREAMER
- Always stream domes / kicker-friendly weather.

LATE-SEASON LINEUP DECISIONS (REBUILDER)

WEEKS 12-17 IF ELIMINATED:
- Sit injury-questionable starters.
- Start younger bench players for development reps.
- DON'T outright tank lineups (integrity / league rules).
- Document your reasoning if challenged.

LATE-SEASON LINEUP DECISIONS (CONTENDER)

WEEKS 12-17:
- Start your studs, period.
- No matchup-chasing. Floor and ceiling matter.
- Avoid streamers in playoffs - need known quantities.
- Verify all questionable players' status BEFORE Sunday.

COMMON SIT/START MISTAKES (DYNASTY)

1) Treating dynasty like redraft EVERY WEEK.
- Even contenders should think about long-term reps.

2) Refusing to start rookies.
- "I don't trust him yet" - they need reps to develop.

3) Over-fading studs in tough matchups.
- Top-5 RBs/WRs are top-5 regardless of matchup.

4) Streaming poorly.
- Use data, not gut feel, for streamers.

5) Missing inactive lists.
- Some leagues lock lineups Sunday morning. Set Friday/Saturday.
