# DYNASTY CONTRACT / SALARY CAP / AUCTION LEAGUES
Sources synthesized: League Tycoon "How salary caps work in dynasty," Fantasy Footballers Dynasty 101 salary cap, Dynasty League Football auction approach, FFToolbox salary cap leagues, Reality Sports Online auction system, Footballguys forum auction threads.

## WHAT IS A CONTRACT / SALARY CAP DYNASTY
A dynasty league where each player has:
1. A salary (cap cost).
2. A contract length (years).
3. A team-imposed cap (usually $200-$300 in fantasy dollars).

These leagues replicate NFL cap mechanics. Owners must balance star players with depth, manage contract expirations, and budget across years.

### KEY CONCEPTS

### SALARY
- Set by auction draft bid in startup.
- New free agents priced via in-season auction or set FAAB-style.
- Veteran players can be re-signed at certain points.

### CONTRACT LENGTH
- 1-5 years typical.
- Longer contracts come with per-year discounts (8% for 2yr, 16% for 3yr, 24% for 4yr, 32% for 5yr typical).
- At expiration, player goes to free agency.

### CAP HOLD / DEAD CAP
- Some leagues impose dead-cap charges if a player is cut early.
- Forces strategic depth.

### ROOKIE CONTRACTS
- Usually fixed-cost based on draft slot.
- Cheaper than auction prices, creating value at rookie picks.

### FRANCHISE TAGS
- Some leagues allow 1-2 franchise tags per year to retain free agents.
- Costs ~ a markup on prior salary.

### AUCTION STARTUP MECHANICS

### NOMINATION
- Each owner takes turns nominating players.
- Bids continue until everyone passes.

### BIDDING DYNAMICS
- Smart owners nominate players THEY DON'T WANT to drain opponents' budgets.
- Stars bid up to fair value typically; mid-tier varies.

### OUT-OF-POSITION OWNERS
- An owner who's spent 80% of cap on 3 stars must roster scrubs.
- Conversely, owners who saved find depth bargains.

### KEY STRATEGIC APPROACHES

### A) STARS-AND-SCRUBS
- Spend 80% of cap on 3-5 elite players.
- Fill rest with $1-3 fliers.
- High variance, high ceiling.

### B) BALANCED ROSTER
- Spend 60-70% of cap on top 8 players.
- Maintain mid-tier depth at every position.
- More consistent week-to-week.

### C) "ZIG WHILE OTHERS ZAG"
- If league goes WR-heavy, scoop top RBs/QBs at discount.
- Profit from market mismatches.

### CONTRACT-LENGTH STRATEGY

### LONG CONTRACTS
- Best for: cheap players with breakout potential.
- 5-year on $5 player is amazing if he becomes a star.
- Bad for: aging stars (locked into decline).

### SHORT CONTRACTS
- Best for: aging stars (1-2 year window).
- High-priced players (you'll need flexibility).
- Risky lottery picks.

### POSITION-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE

QB:
- Long contracts on young QBs.
- Short contracts on aging starters.
- Don't lock 30+ QBs into 4-5 year deals.

RB:
- AVOID multi-year contracts on aged 26+ RBs.
- Volatility makes long contracts risky.
- 1-2 year contracts only on RB1s.

WR:
- The position with longest aging curve - long contracts reasonable on 25-and-under WRs.
- 4-5 year contracts on ascending 22-24 yo WRs are gold.

TE:
- Slow developing - 4-year contracts on ascending young TEs.
- Don't lock aging TEs.

## ROOKIE CONTRACT VALUE
Rookie contracts typically cost much less than auction prices for similar production:
- Rookie 1.01: ~$15-30 cap hit (vs $40-60 auction).
- Rookie 1.06: ~$8-15 cap hit (vs $20-30 auction).
- Rookie 2nd: ~$3-6 cap hit (vs $10-15 auction).

This creates a STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGE for owners who hit on rookie picks - you have years of cheap production.

### IN-SEASON ROSTER MOVES

### WAIVERS
- FAAB still typical.
- Salaries set on add (often based on bid amount + some auto-rule).

### TRADES
- Cap-aware. Both teams must remain under cap after trade.
- Trading expensive contracts requires offsetting cheaper assets.

### CAP DUMPS
- Trade a 30+ vet at high cap hit + a pick to a rebuilder for a younger cheap asset.
- Common pattern.

### MID-SEASON FRANCHISE TAGS
- Lock free agents to 1-2 year deals before they leave.

### OFFSEASON DECISIONS

### CUTS
- Decide who to cut at end of season (some leagues impose dead cap).
- Ruthless cuts of underperformers free up cap for FAs.

### EXTENSIONS
- Extend in-prime players before contracts expire (cheaper than auction).

### FRANCHISE TAGS
- Use sparingly - they're expensive.

### ROOKIE DRAFTS
- High-ROI in salary cap leagues.
- Multiple picks = multiple cheap contracts.

### LEAGUE-SPECIFIC RULES TO CHECK

### SALARY ESCALATION
- Some leagues increase player salaries year-over-year (10-15%).
- Forces churn.

### ROSTER CONSTRUCTION REQUIREMENTS
- Min/max # at each position?
- Bench size?
- Taxi for rookies (often allowed)?

### DEAD CAP LIMITS
- How much dead cap allowed?
- 0-30% varies by league.

### COMMON MISTAKES

### 1) OVERPAYING IN STARTUP
- Spending 30% of cap on one player.
- Lose flexibility for years.

### 2) IGNORING CONTRACT LENGTHS
- "I have McCaffrey on a 5-year contract" = locked into decline.

### 3) NOT LEVERAGING ROOKIES
- Not drafting / trading for rookies = missing cap arbitrage.

### 4) HOARDING DEAD WEIGHT
- Aged-out vets at high salaries you can't trade.

### 5) UNDER-CAP TEAMS NOT BIDDING
- Some owners save cap to "save cap" and never deploy it.

### BEST PRACTICES (CHAMPIONSHIP RECIPE)

1. Auction startup: target 3-4 elite young + balanced depth.
2. Multi-year contracts on 22-25 yo ascenders.
3. Annual cap audit: identify cuts and extensions.
4. Maximize rookie picks every year (cheap production).
5. Trade expensive vets EARLY (before depreciation).
6. Maintain $20-40 cap surplus annually for FA strikes.

CONTRACT vs NON-CONTRACT DYNASTY DIFFERENCES

CONTRACT LEAGUES:
- Year-round cap management.
- More complex, more rewarding for engaged owners.
- Trade volume HIGHER because cap relief drives moves.
- Rookie picks worth MORE because of cheap-contract value.
- Aging vets worth LESS at full salary.

PURE DYNASTY:
- Only roster size constrains.
- Simpler.
- Trade dynamics driven only by talent + age.

## MIGRATION FROM PURE DYNASTY TO CONTRACT
Some leagues add contracts to mature dynasty leagues. This:
- Forces existing owners to reassess rosters.
- Creates one-time massive trade volume.
- Often produces league-defining moves.

## SALARY CAP DYNASTY MENTALITY
- Treat the cap as a budget.
- Maximize value per dollar.
- Don't get sentimental about expensive dead weight.
- Re-sign in-prime players; let aging vets walk.
- Use rookie picks as cheap production engines.
- Trade expensive contracts when their value > their cap hit.
