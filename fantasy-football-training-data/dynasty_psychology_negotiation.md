# DYNASTY TRADE PSYCHOLOGY AND NEGOTIATION
Sources synthesized: Fantasy Footballers "Negotiation Strategy: Five Tips," PlayerProfiler trade comprehensive guide, Dynasty Football Factory "Manifesto of a Trade Enthusiast," Dynasty Nerds "Trade Secrets," Upper Hand Fantasy trade framework, FantasyTradeAnalyzers 12-team strategies, Big Knowledge Football podcast.

## CENTRAL INSIGHT
Trades happen between PEOPLE, not calculators. Numbers are anchors but human factors close deals. The owners who do the most trades aren't the best valuers - they're the best COMMUNICATORS.

### THE GAME-THEORETIC FRAMEWORK

### DYNASTY = REPEATED GAME, NOT ONE-SHOT
- You'll trade with these owners for years.
- Reputation compounds.
- Hard-but-fair > swindler.
- Walking away has long-term value too.

### CREDIBLE THREATS
- "If you don't accept, I'm working on a deal with [other owner]" - if true, leverage.
- "I can sit on this asset for 2 years" - if true, leverage.
- BUT: don't bluff. If your bluff is called, your credibility is destroyed.

### ASYMMETRIC INFORMATION
- You know your roster better than they do.
- They know theirs better than you.
- The trade is about finding asymmetries (each thinks they're winning).
- Best trades = both sides win in their phase.

### NEGOTIATION TACTICS

### OPENING

### 1. ASK FIRST, OFFER SECOND
- "Are you interested in moving [Player X]?" - elicits info.
- "What are you looking for from your team this year?" - phase intel.

### 2. NEVER START WITH AN OFFER
- Anchors negotiation.
- Lock yourself out of upside.

### 3. POSITION YOURSELF FIRST
- "I'm looking to upgrade my RB." - signals direction.
- "I'm shopping my [vet]." - markets your asset.

### OFFER CONSTRUCTION

### 4. AGGRESSIVE BUT FAIR (NOT LOW-BALL)
- 5-15% slight tilt your way.
- Below 80% is a slap; above 100% surrenders leverage.

### 5. SHOW YOUR WORK
- "Calc says X = 5,200; my package = 5,400. Slight tilt your way."
- Backed-up offers get accepted ~40% more often than naked ones.

### 6. MULTIPLE OPTIONS
- "Would you do A+B for X, OR C+D for X?"
- Lets opponent pick THEIR favorite shape.
- Increases acceptance rate dramatically.

### COUNTERS AND RESPONSES

### 7. ACCEPT IF FAIR
- Don't haggle for marginal additional value.
- Speed builds trade reputation.
- "Sounds good, sending it now."

### 8. COUNTER WITH ONE CHANGE
- Don't blow up the deal.
- "Same package but swap C for D?"
- Easier to negotiate single variable.

### 9. ALWAYS HAVE FALLBACK
- If they say no, don't be tied to one target.
- Send to someone else within 24-48 hours.

### CLOSING

### 10. LOCK IT FAST
- Once verbal agreement, send the trade immediately.
- Owners reconsider; speed kills second-guessing.

### 11. DON'T NEGOTIATE OPENLY POST-AGREEMENT
- "I changed my mind, can you add a 2nd?" = bad form.
- Either accept the deal or walk away.

### PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILES (KNOW YOUR LEAGUEMATES)

### THE NUMBERS GUY
- Lives on calc.
- Send calc-anchored offers with backed-up math.
- They respect data; not emotion.

### THE HEART GUY
- Plays favorites; rarely sells "his guys."
- Don't try to pry his stars.
- Look for surplus he's emotionally indifferent to.

### THE OVER-TRADER
- Trades 20+ times per year.
- Send rapid-fire offers; they say yes faster.
- Be careful: they're often shopping the same asset elsewhere.

### THE PASSIVE OWNER
- Trades 1-3 per year.
- Send simple, well-explained offers with clear "fix" for them.
- Don't expect rapid back-and-forth.

### THE STUBBORN OWNER
- Inflated values for his guys.
- Don't waste many offers; revisit when his team's struggling.

### THE NICE OWNER
- Wants to be helpful.
- Offers may be MORE generous than market.
- Don't exploit but capitalize on good-faith asymmetry.

### EMOTIONAL TRIGGERS TO MANAGE

YOUR OWN:
- DON'T CHASE LOSSES. Bad trades happened; move on.
- DON'T REVENGE-TRADE. Hurts you.
- DON'T LOWBALL OUT OF PETTINESS. Reputation cost > one trade.
- DON'T BAG-HOLD. If a buy-low went bad, sell at fair price; don't insist on recovery.

OPPONENT'S:
- HOT-STREAK PRIDE. Just won 4 in a row; thinks team is title contender. Sell vets at premium NOW.
- LOSING-STREAK PANIC. 0-3 start; ready to sell stars cheap.
- DRAFT-DAY HYPE. Just drafted a stud; willing to trade depth.
- FA-LANDING JOY. Player just landed in dream spot; will overpay to add.

### OWNER ARCHETYPES IN COMPLEX TRADES

### THE CONNECTOR
- Trades with everyone.
- Use as broker: "If you trade me X, I think Y will trade you Z."

### THE MARKET MOVER
- His trades reset the league market.
- Know his recent deals.
- Bait him with similar deals to others.

### THE STUBBORN HOLDER
- Acquires; doesn't trade.
- Avoid pursuing his stars; target his bench.

### NEGOTIATION TIMING

### GOOD TIMES TO NEGOTIATE
- Sunday evening (after games when emotions vivid).
- Tuesday night (waivers settled, eye on next week).
- Late October / early November (deadline pressure).
- After NFL Draft / FA (reset values).

### BAD TIMES TO NEGOTIATE
- Saturday before game day (lineup focus).
- During / immediately after their loss (they're tilted).
- Days after a player they own had a bad game (over-anchoring).
- Just before bye weeks (focused on coverage).

## THE "SIDE PAYMENT" CLOSER (in money leagues)
Cash payments outside fantasy can clear logjams:
- "I'll pay $20 to make this trade work."
- Within league rules (most allow side payments).
- Be clear, transparent, document.

### WALKING AWAY

### WHEN TO WALK
- Repeated lowball offers from same owner.
- Demand for irrational additions.
- Asking you to overpay your own valuation by 20%+.

### HOW TO WALK
- "Thanks for considering. I'll keep looking."
- Don't burn bridges.
- Revisit in 2-4 weeks if circumstances change.

### REPUTATION MANAGEMENT

BUILD TRUST:
- Honor verbal agreements.
- Don't shop a player you've offered.
- Be transparent about your roster intent.
- Post trades publicly when fair.

DESTROY TRUST:
- Renege on agreements.
- Hide injury info.
- Hyperbolic asks.
- Tank trades that don't work out.

## THE LONG-VIEW LEDGER
In a 10-year league:
- 70% of major trades are with the same 3-4 owners.
- Build relationships with them year 1.
- Cultivate trust; trade often; share win-wins.
- The 3-4 you build trust with become your trade engine.

### ETHICAL BOUNDARIES

OK:
- Hyping a player you're shopping.
- Asking if X is available before offering.
- Walking away without explanation.

NOT OK:
- Lying about player's status (injury, role).
- Coordinating multi-team trades to favor one owner (collusion).
- Going back on accepted trades.
- Trash-talking opponent in chat.

### COMMON NEGOTIATION MISTAKES

### 1) ANCHORING TOO LOW
- Lowball offers poison negotiations.

### 2) NOT KNOWING ROSTER
- Asking about a player they don't have.

### 3) IGNORING THEIR PHASE
- Offering rebuild assets to a contender; vice versa.

### 4) OVER-TALKING
- Sending 5 messages back-to-back.
- Lets them disengage.

### 5) PUBLIC HUMILIATION
- Calling out an owner's bad trade in chat.
- Kills future trades.

### 6) OBSESSING ON ONE TARGET
- Refusing alternatives; getting stuck.

### 7) ACCEPTING UNFAIR OFFERS
- Out of guilt or social pressure.

## THE BIG IDEA
Trade negotiation in dynasty is a SKILL like draft preparation. Owners who study negotiation, build trust, and execute consistently extract 15-30% more value annually than those who don't.

Don't just calculate. COMMUNICATE.
