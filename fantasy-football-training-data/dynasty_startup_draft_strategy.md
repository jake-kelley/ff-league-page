# DYNASTY STARTUP DRAFT STRATEGY
Sources synthesized: FantasyPros 2026 startup guide, FTN Fantasy startup roster construction, Footballguys dynasty articles, RotoWire dynasty primer, Fantasy Life startup strategy, Dynasty Nerds startup, Dynasty League Football fundamentals.

## GOAL OF A STARTUP
A dynasty startup is the SINGLE most important event of your league's life. Your startup roster is the chassis you'll iterate on for a decade. The goal is NOT to win year 1. The goal is to come out with the highest sum of multi-year asset value AND a credible 1-3 year championship window.

### GENERAL FRAMEWORKS

### A) BEST PLAYER AVAILABLE (BPA) WITH SOFT POSITIONAL GUARDRAILS
- Default approach for startups, especially deep rookie classes are months away.
- Take the highest dynasty value at every pick. Solve positional balance through trades later.
- Caveat: don't end up with 8 elite WRs and zero RB depth - hard to fix via trade unless you sell stars.

### B) ANCHOR YOUTH STRATEGY (most popular winning approach in 2024-2026)
- First 3 rounds: prioritize 24-and-under elite anchors at WR (best long-term hold) and 1 RB1.
- Mid rounds: pivot to highest-upside young WRs and a few productive vets you'll flip.
- Late rounds: rookie picks (esp 2027 1sts and 2026 2nds) and lottery ticket WRs.
- Result: a roster you can win with year 2-3 and that holds value if you have to retool.

### C) PRODUCTIVE STRUCT (PSS) - SF SPECIFIC
- In SF, lock in 2 long-term QBs in the first 5 rounds. Modern preferred move: ONE QB in rd 1-2, second QB in rounds 4-7.
- Old "double tap QB" (2 QBs in first 3 rounds) is now considered suboptimal because the QB middle class is deep.

### D) ZERO RB DYNASTY (controversial, niche)
- Skip RB until rounds 4+, hammer WRs and TEs early.
- Rationale: RBs decline faster, so locking in old RB1s in startup is buying assets you'll have to dump in 2 years.
- Risk: you can be unwinnable for 1-2 years if WRs underperform and you have no RB volume.

### E) "WIN NOW" STARTUP (rare but viable in $$ leagues)
- Old vets at discount in middle rounds (Cooper Kupp at age 32, Travis Kelce types).
- Trade future picks pre-season for more current production.
- Pay-out: realistic 1-2 year title window before forced rebuild.

### POSITION-BY-POSITION GUIDANCE

### QUARTERBACK (1QB)
- QBs are devalued. You can wait until rounds 6-10 and still get a good QB1.
- Target: one QB with rushing upside (plus floor + ceiling) and one QB with great offense + decent rushing.
- Don't pay round 1-2 for a QB in 1QB unless he is a top-3 dual-threat (Allen / Jackson / Hurts tier).

### QUARTERBACK (SF)
- QB is THE most important position. Top-12 QBs are essentially unstartable elsewhere.
- Round 1 QB is acceptable for tier 1 dual-threats.
- Aim for 3 startable QBs by end of draft for bye/injury insurance.

### RUNNING BACK
- Highest weekly ceiling but worst aging. Target 23-or-younger workhorse types in rd 1-3.
- Avoid 26+ RBs in startup unless you're explicitly punting future and going win-now.
- RB hit rate compresses fast - by round 6, RB selections are almost as random as round 12. Lean rookie picks vs rd 6+ RBs.
- Rule of thumb: get 3-4 startable RBs total. Don't try to be deep at RB - you can't be.

### WIDE RECEIVER
- The lifeblood of dynasty rosters. 24-and-under ascending WRs are the single most appreciable asset class.
- Target 5-6 WRs with weekly start potential. Reach for upside over floor at WR.
- Elite WRs (Jefferson/Chase/Lamb tier) hold value into age 28-29; this is the longest-window position outside QB.

### TIGHT END
- Two camps: pay up for the elite (one-of-one weekly advantage) or punt entirely (stream).
- Mid-round TEs are dynasty quicksand - you pay round 4-5 capital and get a weekly bench player.
- TE premium leagues: pay up. 1QB standard: lean punt + draft 2 lottery ticket young TEs.

### PICKS (in startup that includes them)
- Rookie 1sts trade roughly equivalent to mid-tier WR2/RB2 in startups. The best owners stockpile them.
- Future picks (2027, 2028) trade at a discount even though they should not - exploit this in startups.

### STARTUP ROSTER COMPOSITION TARGETS (1QB, 25-roster, 1.0 PPR)
- 2-3 QBs (1 stud + 1 mid-tier + 1 lottery young)
- 5-7 RBs (2 anchors + 2 fliers + 1-2 lottery + handcuffs to your guy)
- 8-10 WRs (2-3 elite/young + 4-5 startable + 2-3 lottery young)
- 2-3 TEs (1 starter + 1 backup OR 2 lottery young)
- 1 K, 1 DST in leagues that use them
- 2-4 rookie picks held for current rookie draft

### STARTUP MISTAKES TO AVOID
- Drafting a kicker before round 25.
- Loading old RBs in rounds 4-8.
- Hoarding handcuffs without owning the lead RB.
- Skipping picks entirely. You always want to be in the rookie draft - even just owning a 3rd lets you participate.
- Drafting "your guys" you irrationally love (homerism). Set a pre-draft tier sheet and discipline yourself.
- Falling for ADP. ADP is a market average, not a value statement. If your tier sheet says no, listen.

### POST-STARTUP IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (NEXT 30 DAYS)
- Identify 2-3 holes in your starting lineup and shop your worst startable player to fill them.
- Identify 1-2 "I won't start him" assets and shop them for picks while their startup-draft narrative price is high.
- Reach out to all 9 (or 11) other managers individually. Ask "what are you trying to accomplish?" - this gives you the league map for the next 12 months.
