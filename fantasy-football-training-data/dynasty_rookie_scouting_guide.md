# PART 1 - HOW TO READ COLLEGE PRODUCTION

## THE GOLDEN RULE
Counting stats lie. Market share, age, and competition tell the truth.

A 1,200-yard senior WR vs Group-of-5 defenses is LESS valuable than an
800-yard true sophomore WR who took 30% of his team's targets vs SEC defenses.
Always normalize for:
1. AGE at production (younger = better).
2. COMPETITION quality (Power-5 > G5 > FCS).
3. MARKET SHARE (% of team's offensive production).
4. ROLE EVOLUTION (rising or already maxed out).
5. PRODUCTION EFFICIENCY (yards per opportunity, not just totals).

# 1A. WIDE RECEIVER COLLEGE PRODUCTION

THE FOUR-METRIC FRAMEWORK FOR WRs

### 1) BREAKOUT AGE
   - Age at which a WR first earned >20% of his team's receiving yards OR >20%
     of receiving TDs in a single season.
   - <19.0 = ELITE signal. Generational profile.
   - 19.0-20.0 = STRONG signal.
   - 20.0-21.0 = GOOD signal.
   - 21.0-22.0 = AVERAGE.
   - >22.0 = WARNING. Late-bloomers rarely transition.
   - WHY: NFL fantasy hits are dominated by WRs who dominated college EARLY.

### 2) FINAL-SEASON DOMINATOR RATING
   - (Receiving yards % + Receiving TD %) / 2 of the player's team total.
   - >40% = elite (1st round NFL signal).
   - 30-40% = good (early Day 2 NFL signal).
   - 20-30% = average.
   - <20% = warning unless explained by team scheme.

### 3) MARKET SHARE TRAJECTORY
   - Did his target/yard share GROW year over year?
   - Rising trajectory = ascending player.
   - Flat or declining = capped profile.

### 4) PRODUCTION VS RANKED DEFENSES
   - Yards/game vs Top-25 defenses ÷ Yards/game vs all defenses.
   - >0.85 = legit producer.
   - 0.70-0.85 = average.
   - <0.70 = padded against weak schedule.

### WR PROFILE TYPES (FROM COLLEGE TAPE)

### A) ALPHA X (Outside boundary WR)
   - 6'1"+, 200+ lbs, contested-catch wins.
   - Early breakout as primary outside option.
   - Best NFL projection: WR1.

### B) BIG-SLOT YAC WR
   - 6'0"-6'2", explosive YAC ability.
   - Mismatch role; safety blanket.
   - Best NFL projection: WR1-2 in PPR.

### C) PURE-SLOT QUICK-TWITCH
   - 5'8"-5'11", elite agility.
   - Short-area YAC, slot-only.
   - PPR floor; capped ceiling.

### D) DEEP-THREAT BURNER
   - 4.30-4.40 speed, sub-180 lbs typical.
   - Vertical-only; high TD volatility.
   - Best NFL projection: WR2-3 with boom/bust.

### E) BIG-PLAY HYBRID
   - 6'0"+, sub-4.45 speed, contested-catch + speed.
   - Rare archetype - combines outside + slot.
   - Highest dynasty ceiling.

### F) POSSESSION-CHAIN MOVER
   - Average athleticism, route-running emphasis.
   - High floor, low ceiling.
   - Best NFL projection: WR2 in PPR.

### WHAT TO LOOK FOR ON FILM

BLUE-CHIP SIGNS:
- Wins contested catches (50-50 balls).
- Creates separation against PRESS coverage.
- Catches outside his frame (high points, low traps).
- Adjusts to off-target throws.
- YAC creativity (broken tackles, change of direction).
- Routes that "set up" defenders (deception).

YELLOW FLAGS:
- Drops on simple routes.
- Body-catches everything.
- Production all comes via screens / manufactured touches.
- Same college team has 2-3 other receivers with similar production.
- Production almost entirely vs zone (untested vs press).

RED FLAGS:
- Rounded routes (no NFL-level subtlety).
- Loses 50-50 balls regularly.
- Inconsistent hands week-to-week.
- "Big games against air" pattern.

SNEAKY-PICK SIGNALS FOR WRs (DYNASTY ARBITRAGE)

The community over-drafts these patterns:
- "Senior who dominated his fifth year."
- "Big production vs G5 schedule."
- "5'8 slot-only with 4.45 speed."
- "Ran 4.5 at the combine but had college production."

The community UNDER-DRAFTS these (sneaky picks):
- TRUE SOPHOMORE who declared early after 2 strong years.
- WR who shared a target room with another NFL R1 WR (limited his stats but
  shows he can produce under competition).
- WR with rising trajectory (Y2 < Y3 < Y4) at a Power-5.
- WR who earned 20%+ market share at age 19-20 even on a deep team.
- 6'0"+ WR with sub-4.50 speed AND contested-catch tape.
- WR whose offense ran 50% under his market share (capped by scheme, not
  ability).

# 1B. RUNNING BACK COLLEGE PRODUCTION

### THE FOUR-METRIC FRAMEWORK FOR RBS

### 1) WORKLOAD (% of team carries)
   - >70% = workhorse profile (NFL workhorse projection).
   - 50-70% = lead-back profile.
   - 30-50% = committee profile.
   - <30% = niche / situational.
   - WHY: NFL teams pattern-match to college workload.

### 2) RECEIVING USAGE
   - 30+ catches over 2 college seasons = pass-catching back.
   - 50+ catches in single year = elite PPR profile.
   - <15 catches per year = early-down only profile.

### 3) YARDS PER CARRY VS POWER-5
   - >5.5 vs Power-5 schedule = elite efficiency.
   - 4.8-5.5 = good.
   - 4.3-4.8 = average.
   - <4.3 = warning unless explained by OL.

### 4) BREAKOUT AGE
   - <20 = elite.
   - 20-21 = strong.
   - 21+ = warning (RB careers are short; older breakout = less runway).

### WHAT TO LOOK FOR ON RB FILM

BLUE-CHIP SIGNS:
- Contact balance (broken tackles, YAC).
- Vision/cutback ability (finds the right hole).
- Pass protection effort.
- Ability to catch out of backfield.
- Acceleration through hole.
- "Second gear" speed.
- Goal-line strength (does he find pay dirt?).

YELLOW FLAGS:
- All production behind elite OL.
- Zero pass-pro tape (concerning for 3-down role).
- Heavy fumbler.
- Breaks down at end of long runs (no second gear).
- Avoids contact (will struggle with NFL pursuit angles).

RED FLAGS:
- 250+ carry seasons accumulated (workload tax).
- Significant injury history at college (usually carries forward).
- Speed but no vision (needs perfect blocking).

SNEAKY-PICK SIGNALS FOR RBs

UNDER-DRAFTED:
- Pass-catching ability buried in run-heavy scheme.
- Early-declared after just 2 college seasons (lower workload tax).
- 4.45-4.55 speed with 220+ lb frame (NFL workhorse profile).
- Strong receiving role + good YPC (PPR upside).
- Ran behind a bottom-20 college OL but still produced (talent above scheme).

OVER-DRAFTED:
- 5'10" 200lb scat back with 100+ catches (great college, niche NFL).
- Workload-monster SEC vet at 23+ years old.
- One-year wonder senior season.

### ARCHETYPE TYPES (RB)

### A) BELL-COW (250+ carries projection)
   - 220+ lbs, 4.45-4.55 speed, contact balance.
   - Best dynasty asset (when they hit).

### B) PASS-CATCHING SPECIALIST
   - 200-215 lbs, route-running ability.
   - Elite PPR floor.

### C) 3-DOWN BACK (combo)
   - 215-225 lbs, receiving ability + power.
   - Highest possible dynasty ceiling.

### D) COMMITTEE BACK
   - Specific role (2-min, goal-line, change of pace).
   - Limited dynasty value.

### E) BIG-PLAY SPECIALIST
   - 200-210 lbs, sub-4.40 speed.
   - High variance week-to-week.

# 1C. QUARTERBACK COLLEGE PRODUCTION

### QB IS THE HARDEST POSITION TO PROJECT FROM COLLEGE
- College QB success rate -> NFL fantasy QB1 = ~30%.
- Far more bust risk than other positions.
- Rushing upside is the cheat code.

### KEY QB METRICS

### 1) ADJUSTED YARDS PER ATTEMPT (AY/A)
   - >9.0 vs Power-5 = elite.
   - 8.0-9.0 = good.
   - 7.0-8.0 = average.
   - <7.0 = warning.

### 2) RUSHING YARDS PER GAME
   - >40 = elite floor (Allen/Jackson/Hurts profile).
   - 25-40 = good rushing upside.
   - 10-25 = some scrambling ability.
   - <10 = pure pocket passer.

### 3) PRESSURE PERFORMANCE (PFF QB grade under pressure)
   - High under-pressure grade = NFL-translatable.
   - Low = will collapse against NFL pass rushes.

### 4) DECISION-MAKING METRICS
   - Turnover Worthy Play % (lower = better).
   - Big-Time Throws % (higher = better).

### WHAT TO LOOK FOR ON QB FILM

BLUE-CHIP SIGNS:
- Reads through progressions (not just first read).
- Throws with anticipation (ball arrives before WR breaks).
- Drives ball down the field (deep accuracy).
- Stays in pocket under pressure.
- Mobile but throws first, runs second.
- Pre-snap recognition.

YELLOW FLAGS:
- One-read system (Air Raid pattern).
- Manufactured rushing yards (designed runs > scrambles).
- Limited deep-ball touch.
- Inconsistent footwork.

RED FLAGS:
- Locks onto first read.
- Eyes downfield only (no progression).
- Mechanics break under pressure.
- Inaccuracy on intermediate routes.

### QB ARCHETYPE TYPES

### A) DUAL-THREAT ELITE
   - 40+ rushing YPG, 8.5+ AY/A.
   - Generational profile (Allen/Jackson/Hurts).

### B) MOBILE PASSER
   - 25-40 rushing YPG, accurate passer.
   - High dynasty value.

### C) POCKET PASSER ELITE
   - <15 rushing YPG, 9.5+ AY/A.
   - Mahomes-tier upside; low floor of mobility.

### D) GAME MANAGER
   - Solid efficiency, no rushing.
   - QB2 dynasty ceiling.

### E) RAW PROJECT
   - Tools without production.
   - Lottery ticket only.

# 1D. TIGHT END COLLEGE PRODUCTION

### TE IS THE LATEST-DEVELOPING POSITION
- Most TEs need 2-3 NFL years before useful.
- College production is LESS predictive than for WR.

### KEY TE METRICS

### 1) RECEIVING YARDS PER GAME
   - 60+ in final season = elite.
   - 40-60 = good.
   - 20-40 = average.
   - <20 = blocking-TE profile.

### 2) MARKET SHARE
   - >18% of team's receiving yards = elite TE production.
   - 12-18% = good.
   - <12% = warning.

### 3) ATHLETIC PROFILE
   - 4.65 or faster 40 with 240+ lb frame = elite.
   - Vertical 35+, broad 9'6"+ = athletic markers.

### 4) ROUTE PARTICIPATION
   - >70% = "WR-like" usage. Better.
   - <60% = blocker-first. Worse for fantasy.

### WHAT TO LOOK FOR ON TE FILM

BLUE-CHIP SIGNS:
- Catches outside the frame.
- YAC ability (rare for TE, valuable).
- Run-blocking effort (signals NFL coaches will trust them on field).
- Used in slot, flexed out, in-line.
- Wins matchups against safeties (size + speed combo).

YELLOW FLAGS:
- "TE in name only" (3rd WR who's listed at TE).
- All production from screens.
- Limited route tree.

RED FLAGS:
- Drops.
- Cannot block at all (will be 3rd-string in NFL).
- College team had 2 TEs with similar production.

# PART 2 - HOW TO GAUGE NFL DRAFT LANDING SPOT

## WHY LANDING SPOT MATTERS - BUT TALENT > SPOT
NFL Draft capital is the SINGLE STRONGEST predictor of fantasy success
(stronger than college production for most positions). The TEAM that drafts
a player matters too, but TALENT ENDURES across landing spots.

The dynasty mantra: weight DRAFT CAPITAL heavily; weight LANDING SPOT
moderately. Don't let "bad spot" panic move a clearly elite prospect down
your board more than 1 tier.

# 2A. NFL DRAFT CAPITAL TIERS - HIT RATES BY ROUND

The single highest-signal data point in dynasty rookie scouting.

CAREER FANTASY HIT RATES (TOP-24 SEASONS AT POSITION):

NFL ROUND 1:
- WR: ~50-55% become Top-24 dynasty WR.
- RB: ~45-55%.
- QB: ~35-45%.
- TE: ~30-40%.

NFL ROUND 2:
- WR: ~25-35%.
- RB: ~25-35%.
- QB: ~15-25%.
- TE: ~15-25%.

NFL ROUND 3:
- WR: ~15-22%.
- RB: ~15-20%.
- QB: ~5-12%.
- TE: ~10-15%.

NFL ROUND 4:
- WR/RB: ~8-12%.
- QB/TE: ~3-7%.

NFL ROUND 5+:
- All positions: ~3-7% combined.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATION: A NFL R1 RB at 1.06 of dynasty rookie draft is a
better bet than an NFL R3 WR at 1.04, regardless of pre-draft consensus.

DRAFT SLOT WITHIN ROUND ALSO MATTERS:
- Top-10 NFL pick: organizational commitment + premium talent grade.
- Picks 11-32 (rest of NFL R1): elite talent grade but more situational variance.
- NFL R2 early (33-45): late R1 talent in many cases; treat similar to mid-R1.
- NFL R2 late (60-64): borderline R3 talent; less committed by team.

# 2B. EVALUATING THE TEAM CONTEXT

After draft capital, the QUALITY OF LANDING SPOT is the next layer.
Score each rookie's situation across SIX dimensions:

### 1. DEPTH CHART CLARITY
   - Is the role open (no veteran ahead)? Best.
   - Is there a vet on his way out? Good.
   - Is there competition? Concerning.
   - Is he buried 3rd or 4th? Bad.

### 2. OFFENSIVE QUALITY
   - Top-10 NFL offense the prior year? Elite.
   - Middle of pack? Average.
   - Bottom-10? Concerning unless rebuild signs.

### 3. QUARTERBACK PLAY (matters most for WR/TE)
   - Top-10 QB in NFL? Elite.
   - Mid-tier QB? Average.
   - Below-replacement QB? Bad signal.
   - Aging QB on year-to-year? Variable.

### 4. OFFENSIVE LINE (matters most for RB)
   - Top-10 NFL OL? Elite.
   - Middle? Average.
   - Bottom-10? Bad RB landing spot.

### 5. SCHEME / OC FIT
   - Run-heavy scheme + RB? Good.
   - Pass-heavy + WR/TE? Good.
   - Run-heavy + WR? Bad fit.
   - Coordinator stability (returning OC)? Better.

### 6. CONTRACT/ROSTER FUTURE STABILITY
   - Are there other young players competing in 2027+?
   - Did the team sign aging vets to short-term deals (signaling rookie
     succession)?
   - Is the team building or tearing down?

# 2C. POSITION-SPECIFIC LANDING SPOT FRAMEWORKS

### WIDE RECEIVERS - BEST LANDING SPOT CHECKLIST
- WR room thin (only 1 established starter or fewer).
- Established or ascending QB.
- Pass-friendly OC.
- Top-half of NFL in pass attempts.
- NO competing rookie WR drafted same year by same team.
- HC/OC who has produced fantasy WR1s before.

### WR LANDING-SPOT GREEN/YELLOW/RED FLAGS

GREEN: drafted to a team with vacated WR1 role + good QB (e.g., a team
whose top WR just left in FA). Examples include teams that haven't
extended their veteran WR1 and use a young rookie as the replacement.

YELLOW: drafted into a stacked WR room with established WR1 and WR2.
Production may still emerge by Year 2-3 as roles open or coaching
changes shift target distribution.

RED: drafted to a run-heavy team with weak QB and another WR drafted
in the same class. Y1 production likely <500 yards.

WHAT MARKETS GET WRONG WITH WR LANDING SPOTS:
- Owners over-correct on "stacked rooms." But target shares EQUALIZE
  over 2-3 years, especially if rookie has real talent. Buy these dips.
- Owners over-correct on "bad QB." QBs change. The WR's talent endures.
- Owners over-pay for "perfect spot" rookies who were really just OK
  prospects. Fade these.

### RUNNING BACKS - BEST LANDING SPOT CHECKLIST
- Vacated lead-back role (departing FA, retirement, suspension).
- Top-15 NFL OL.
- Run-heavy or balanced offensive scheme.
- HC committed to a workhorse (not a committee guy).
- No long-term-contracted competition.
- Receiving usage in college + a HC who uses RBs in passing game.

### RB LANDING-SPOT GREEN/YELLOW/RED FLAGS

GREEN: drafted in NFL R1-R2 to a team with no entrenched starter,
strong OL, and a balanced offense. Year 1 workhorse projection.

YELLOW: drafted to a team with a vet under contract for 1-2 more years.
Could emerge year 2 if vet's contract ends.

RED: drafted as the 3rd RB on a team with 2 productive starters under
long-term deals. Limited fantasy value for 3+ years.

WHAT MARKETS GET WRONG WITH RB LANDING SPOTS:
- They over-credit the "great offense" without checking if the RB will
  actually get carries.
- They under-credit "bad team" RBs who will get touches by default.
- They over-correct on rookie RBs drafted to teams with vets - many of
  those vets get cut/traded mid-year.

### QUARTERBACKS - BEST LANDING SPOT CHECKLIST
- Top-32 NFL Draft pick (organizational commitment).
- Decent WR/TE corps (gives him weapons).
- Functional OL (won't get killed Year 1).
- HC/OC with QB-development track record.
- Rebuilding team that will be patient.
- Mobile QB-friendly scheme (RPO / play-action heavy).

### QB LANDING-SPOT GREEN/YELLOW/RED FLAGS

GREEN: top-3 NFL pick to a rebuilding team with WR investment, OL
investment, and a QB-friendly OC. Year 1-2 development with Year 3
breakout projection.

YELLOW: NFL R1 pick to a team with an aging vet ahead. Sit-and-learn
year 1; starter year 2.

RED: NFL R3+ pick to a team without commitment to him as future starter.
Backup-only projection.

WHAT MARKETS GET WRONG WITH QB LANDING SPOTS:
- Over-correct on "team that traded up" narrative without checking
  organizational stability.
- Under-credit late-round QBs who land in great spots (Brock Purdy
  template).
- Apply 1QB heuristics to SF leagues where backup QBs have real value.

### TIGHT ENDS - BEST LANDING SPOT CHECKLIST
- TE-heavy offensive scheme (Andy Reid template).
- Aging or injured veteran TE ahead.
- QB who relies on TE as safety blanket.
- HC/OC with history of TE production (not just blocking).
- Team weak at WR (TE becomes target hog).

### TE LANDING-SPOT GREEN/YELLOW/RED FLAGS

GREEN: NFL R1-R2 to a team with a vacated TE1 role + TE-friendly scheme.

YELLOW: NFL R2 pick to a team with a young TE already in place. Could
emerge Y3-4.

RED: NFL R3+ to a team with established TE1 under long contract. 5-year
fantasy desert.

# 2D. THE LANDING SPOT REPRICE (POST-NFL DRAFT MOVEMENT)

The NFL Draft causes the LARGEST single-event reprice of the dynasty year.
Pre-draft tier sheets reshuffle dramatically based on landing spots.

EXPECT THIS PATTERN:

### PRE-NFL DRAFT TOP RANKINGS (talent-only) -> POST-NFL DRAFT TOP RANKINGS
- Talent #1 lands well -> stays #1 or rises.
- Talent #1 lands poorly -> drops to #4-6 typically.
- Talent #4-5 lands perfectly -> rises to #2-3.
- Talent #10 lands perfectly -> rises to #5-7.

BUYING WINDOWS POST-DRAFT:
- A talent-tier-1 prospect who fell to 1.06 due to "bad" landing spot is a
  LIKELY BUY (talent endures; spot can change).
- A talent-tier-2 prospect who rocketed to 1.02 due to "great" spot is a
  LIKELY SELL (overpriced for actual long-term value).

THE 2-WEEK MARKET ARBITRAGE:
- Markets overcorrect within 7-14 days post-NFL Draft.
- Smart owners can buy mispriced players in this window.
- After 2 weeks, the market normalizes and the arbitrage closes.

# PART 3 - HOW TO CHOOSE BETWEEN ROOKIES WITH SIMILAR PRODUCTION

When two prospects look "the same" on paper, the dynasty owner who
correctly picks the better one wins thousands of dynasty value units
over the next 5-10 years. Here's the tie-breaker decision tree:

# 3A. THE HIERARCHICAL TIE-BREAKER LIST

When two rookies look equal in college production, apply these
tie-breakers IN ORDER. The first one that produces a clear answer
breaks the tie.

### TIER 1 TIE-BREAKERS (apply first)

### 1) NFL DRAFT CAPITAL
   - The single biggest tie-breaker.
   - NFL R1 > NFL R2 > NFL R3, period.
   - Within R1: top-10 > rest of round.
   - Within R2: top-10 > rest of round.

### 2) AGE AT DECLARATION
   - Younger ALWAYS wins.
   - 21 yo at NFL Draft > 23 yo with same production.
   - Why: more career runway.

### 3) BREAKOUT AGE (production-based)
   - Earlier breakout > later breakout.
   - <19 dominator > 20 dominator > 21 dominator > 22 dominator.

### TIER 2 TIE-BREAKERS (apply if Tier 1 ties)

### 4) QUALITY OF COMPETITION
   - Power-5 (SEC, B1G, ACC, Big-12, PAC-12 historical) > Group of 5.
   - SEC/B1G > ACC/Big-12 > PAC-12 (historical performance).

### 5) ATHLETIC TESTING (combine, pro day)
   - Speed (40-yard dash).
   - Explosiveness (vertical, broad jump).
   - Agility (3-cone, shuttle).
   - Composite athletic score (RAS - Relative Athletic Score).
   - >9.0 RAS = elite athlete; 8.0-9.0 = strong; <7.0 = below position avg.

### 6) SIZE / FRAME
   - Position-specific size standards.
   - 6'1"+ for outside WR > 5'10" outside WR.
   - 220+ lbs RB > 200 lbs RB for workhorse projection.
   - 245+ lbs TE for in-line use.

### 7) PRODUCTION TRAJECTORY
   - Rising (Y2 < Y3 < Y4) > flat > declining.
   - Indicates ascending player vs maxed-out.

### TIER 3 TIE-BREAKERS (apply if Tier 1 + 2 tie)

### 8) SCHEME-FIT NFL TRANSITION
   - Did the player do things in college that are translatable to NFL?
   - Pro-style college scheme > Air Raid only.
   - Played in slot AND outside (versatile) > only one.

### 9) PASS-PROTECTION / BLOCKING TAPE (RB/TE)
   - Willing blockers stay on the field longer.
   - Coaches reward 3-down ability.

### 10) HEALTH HISTORY
    - Clean injury history > 2+ multi-game injuries.
    - Workload accumulated > workload preserved.

### 11) CHARACTER / WORK ETHIC INDICATORS
    - Coach quotes, team leadership, off-field cleanliness.
    - "He's the first guy in the building" matters.

### 12) FAMILY / GENETIC FACTORS
    - NFL-pedigree families (older brothers, dads) often translate.
    - Less reliable but a tie-breaker.

# 3B. SPECIFIC TIE-BREAKER SCENARIOS

SCENARIO 1: Two WRs with similar college production and similar size.

Apply: NFL Draft capital first. Then age. Then RAS. Then breakout age.

SCENARIO 2: Two RBs with similar workload.

Apply: Receiving usage (PPR floor). Then NFL Draft capital. Then age.
Then size + speed combo.

SCENARIO 3: Two QBs with similar AY/A.

Apply: Rushing yards per game (rushing upside is the cheat code).
Then NFL Draft capital. Then age. Then under-pressure performance.

SCENARIO 4: Two TEs with similar production.

Apply: NFL Draft capital. Then athletic profile. Then route participation
(WR-like usage). Then age.

# 3C. THE "PRODUCTION-DENIAL" QUESTION

When evaluating between two rookies, ask:

"If their college production were swapped, which would still be the better
NFL prospect?"

This question forces you to weight TALENT/SITUATION over RAW PRODUCTION.

Example:
- Player A: 1,300 yards, 6'1", 4.45 speed, 8.5 RAS, NFL R1.
- Player B: 1,300 yards, 6'0", 4.55 speed, 7.0 RAS, NFL R3.

If their production were swapped:
- Player A in B's situation would still test better and be drafted higher.
- Player B in A's situation would still test worse and be drafted lower.

Conclusion: Player A is the better dynasty bet, even if their college
production looks identical.

# 3D. THE "OPPORTUNITY-WEIGHTED" PRODUCTION VIEW

Raw production hides huge differences in opportunity:
- WR A: 1,000 yards on 30% target share.
- WR B: 1,000 yards on 18% target share.

Both produced 1,000 yards. But WR B did it with FAR LESS opportunity. He's
the better prospect.

Analogous calculations:
- For RB: yards-per-carry vs schedule-adjusted average.
- For QB: yards-per-attempt with same supporting cast quality.
- For TE: yards-per-route-run.

When in doubt, use EFFICIENCY-PER-OPPORTUNITY rather than raw production.

# 3E. THE "FLOOR vs CEILING" QUESTION

Sometimes you're choosing between a high-floor / low-ceiling prospect
and a low-floor / high-ceiling prospect.

CONTENDERS should prefer high-floor, since they need year-1 contributions.
REBUILDERS should prefer high-ceiling, since they're betting on year-2-3
breakouts.

Match the prospect to your phase, not your gut.

# PART 4 - WHICH ROOKIES TO TAKE WITH WHICH PICKS

This section covers PICK-BY-PICK decision frameworks for dynasty rookie
drafts. Generic principles apply year-to-year regardless of class.

# 4A. THE 1.01 PICK

### WHO YOU TAKE AT 1.01

The 1.01 should be:
1. The highest-talent prospect in the class.
2. With NFL R1 draft capital.
3. With a starting role from Day 1 (or the next-best landing spot in class).
4. Format-adjusted (SF favors QB; TEP favors TE).

DECISION TREE FOR 1.01:

STEP 1: Is there a generational prospect (top-3 NFL pick + elite athletic
        profile + dominant college production at age 21 or younger)?
        - YES: take him regardless of position or format.
        - NO: continue.

STEP 2: What format are you in?
        - 1QB / TEP: continue to Step 3.
        - SF: skip to Step 4.

STEP 3: (1QB / TEP) Among the top WR, RB, TE, who has best landing spot
        + NFL R1 capital + early breakout?
        - Pick him.
        - Default tie-breaker: WR > RB > TE (longest career, best aging).

STEP 4: (SF) Is there a top-3 NFL Draft QB?
        - YES: he is your 1.01 unless the WR/RB is generational.
        - NO: apply Step 3 logic.

### WHEN TO PASS ON 1.01 (TRADE BACK)
- Class has no clear elite talent (flat top tier).
- You have multiple options at same tier.
- A rebuilder offers you 1.04 + 2.02 + 2026 1st - that's massive value if
  you're choosing among similarly-tiered players.

### 1.01 EXECUTION
- Don't overthink. The 1.01 is the highest-EV pick of the entire dynasty year.
- Mistake of last 10 years: trying to outsmart the obvious 1.01 = usually wrong.
- Trust consensus on the 1.01.

# 4B. PICKS 1.02 - 1.04 (TIER 1 PICKS)

These picks should land you a Tier-1 prospect (the top 3-4 talents in the class).

### DECISION TREE
- Apply same logic as 1.01.
- If your 1.01 was the top WR, your 1.02 should be the top RB or QB.
- Format matters: in TEP, top young TE may slot here.

### POSITIONAL ALLOCATION
- Don't take a worse player just to fill a roster need.
- Best player available is the rule for rookie drafts.

### NEVER REACH FOR 1.02-1.04
- These picks should produce real fantasy starters.
- Reaching = picking outside your tier sheet.

# 4C. PICKS 1.05 - 1.08 (MID 1ST)

These picks are real assets but represent a SIGNIFICANT drop from
1.01-1.04. Hit rates fall from 50%+ to 30-40%.

### DECISION TREE
- Identify your tier-2 prospects (5-8 talents typically).
- Pick the BEST AVAILABLE at any position you don't have surplus.
- WR-heavy strategy: lean WR.
- Trade back if multiple tier-2 prospects remain.

### WHO TO TARGET AT MID-1ST
- NFL R1 talent that fell due to landing spot concerns (TALENT ENDURES).
- NFL R2 early-round picks who landed in workhorse roles.
- Skill-position rookies on rebuilding NFL teams (volume opportunities).

### WHO TO AVOID AT MID-1ST
- NFL R3 picks (regardless of pre-draft consensus).
- "Picks based purely on landing spot" without underlying talent.
- Rookies behind multi-year veteran starters.

# 4D. PICKS 1.09 - 1.12 (LATE 1ST)

Late 1sts are the most VOLATILE picks. Hit rate drops to 20-30%.
These should be HIGH-UPSIDE swings, not safe picks.

### DECISION TREE
- Identify your tier-3 prospects (8-12 in most classes).
- Take HIGHER-CEILING over higher-floor here.
- Lottery picks make sense at late-1st.

### WHO TO TARGET AT LATE 1ST
- NFL R2 athletes with strong landing spots.
- NFL R3 prospects with dominant college production AND elite athleticism.
- Rookie QBs in SF leagues with NFL Draft commitment.
- Year-1 workhorse RBs with NFL R3 capital but vacated lead-back role.

### THE LATE-1ST ARBITRAGE
- These picks often trade down for early 2nds + 3rds.
- This is FINE if class is shallow at late-1st tier.
- Don't reach for a "name" - take the BPA at the tier.

# 4E. ROUND 2 PICKS (2.01 - 2.12)

Round 2 picks have ~25-35% hit rate at the top, dropping to ~12-18% at
the bottom. They're meaningful assets but require selectivity.

### DECISION TREE
- Identify tier-4 prospects (10-20 in most classes).
- Round 2 should take HIGHER-CEILING swings, NOT depth fillers.

### WHO TO TARGET IN ROUND 2
- NFL R2 capital with clear path to starter role.
- NFL R3 with elite athletic markers + production trajectory.
- Year-1 backup RBs on aging starter teams.
- WRs in NFL R2 who landed in WR1-trajectory roles.
- Rookie QBs in SF leagues even if NFL Day 3.
- Rookie TEs in TEP leagues with NFL R2 capital.

### WHO TO AVOID IN ROUND 2
- NFL R5+ prospects (almost never hit).
- Niche-role specialists (gadget players, 3rd-down only).
- "Camp body" UDFAs (extremely low hit rate).

# 4F. ROUND 3 PICKS (3.01 - 3.12)

Round 3 picks hit at ~12-18% at the top, ~5-10% at the bottom. These are
LOTTERY TICKETS, not starters.

### DECISION TREE
- Identify tier-5 prospects (lottery tickets).
- Take HIGH-VARIANCE upside.
- Stash on taxi if eligible.

### WHO TO TARGET IN ROUND 3
- Athletic profile + opportunity (rare combination).
- NFL R3-R4 prospects with workhorse-projected roles.
- UDFA / late-round NFL picks who landed in vacated roles.
- Rookie WRs with sub-4.45 speed AND college breakout.
- Rookie RBs with 220+ lbs + receiving usage.

### WHO TO AVOID IN ROUND 3
- Pure gadget players.
- Backups behind durable young starters.
- Camp bodies with no clear NFL path.

# 4G. ROUND 4+ PICKS

Round 4+ picks hit at <5%. These are deep lottery tickets where you
should:
- Take ONE prospect you genuinely like.
- Stash on taxi.
- Cut after Year 2 if no breakout.

# 4H. SUPERFLEX-SPECIFIC PICK STRATEGY

In SF, rookie QBs become MORE valuable. The pick strategy shifts:

1.01: Top NFL R1 QB if one exists; otherwise top non-QB.
1.02-1.04: Other top non-QB OR 2nd-tier QB if NFL R1.
1.05-1.08: NFL R1 QB (even if "lesser" prospect) > NFL R3 WR.
Round 2: Backup QBs to aging starters can have real value.
Round 3+: Lottery QBs (NFL R5-R7) can become future starters in SF.

In SF, NEVER pass on a top-3 NFL Draft QB if he's available.

# 4I. TEP-SPECIFIC PICK STRATEGY

In TE Premium, rookie TEs become significantly more valuable:

1.01-1.04: Elite TE prospects can slot into 1.04 in TEP (vs 1.10 in non-TEP).
Mid-1st: NFL R1 TE prospects with elite athletic profiles.
Round 2: NFL R2 TEs with vacated TE1 paths.
Round 3: TE lottery tickets become more relevant.

In TEP, rookie TEs jump 5-15 spots compared to non-TEP rankings.

# 4J. CONTENDER vs REBUILDER PICK STRATEGY

CONTENDERS should:
- Trade rookie picks for proven veteran production.
- Use remaining picks on year-1-impact prospects (RBs especially).
- Avoid lottery picks (need wins NOW, not in 3 years).

REBUILDERS should:
- Hold all rookie picks.
- Focus on long-window WR prospects.
- Take high-ceiling lottery tickets in late rounds.
- Stockpile future picks (next year's class is often stronger).

# 4K. ROOKIE DRAFT PICK CHECKLIST (THE PRE-PICK QUESTIONS)

Before submitting any rookie draft pick, ask:

1. Is this player in my pre-built tier sheet?
2. Is he the BEST AVAILABLE on my tier sheet?
3. Have I evaluated his NFL Draft capital?
4. Have I evaluated his landing spot (positive/neutral/negative)?
5. Have I checked age + breakout age?
6. Could I trade back and still get a same-tier player?
7. Have I considered my roster context (do I need this position)?
8. Am I avoiding hype / FOMO?

If you can answer YES to all 8, submit the pick. If any are NO, pause.

# APPENDIX A - TIER-BUILDING WORKFLOW

### PRE-NFL DRAFT TIER SHEET (build in February-March)

1. Read 3-5 prospect-grade publications (PFF, The Athletic, Hayden Winks,
   Sharp Football, FantasyPros).

2. List all prospects with:
   - College production stats.
   - Athletic profile (combine).
   - Pre-draft NFL Draft projection.
   - Age at NFL Draft.

3. Group into tiers (5-7 tiers typically):
   - Tier 1: Generational.
   - Tier 2: Future stars.
   - Tier 3: Solid starters.
   - Tier 4: Year-2+ projections.
   - Tier 5: Lottery tickets.

4. Note any positional preferences (WR-heavy, etc.).

### POST-NFL-DRAFT TIER SHEET REBUILD (re-do in late April / early May)

1. Rebuild based on NFL Draft capital (now confirmed).
2. Reshuffle based on landing spots.
3. Cap upward movement at 1 tier (don't over-rate landing spots).
4. Identify tier breaks (gaps between tiers signal trade-back opportunities).

### ROOKIE DRAFT EXECUTION

1. Have your tier sheet open.
2. Trade ONLY when you can stay in same tier post-trade.
3. Pick BPA on your sheet.
4. Don't deviate based on draft-day momentum.

# APPENDIX B - SCOUTING MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. OVER-WEIGHTING AGAINST-G5-DEFENSE STATS.
2. FALLING IN LOVE WITH HIGHLIGHT REELS (low signal vs full-game tape).
3. BELIEVING DRAFT-NIGHT NARRATIVES (hype peaks at NFL Draft).
4. DOWNGRADING AFTER ONE BAD GAME OR SCOUTING REPORT.
5. UPGRADING BASED ON ONE GREAT GAME.
6. ASSUMING "GREAT LANDING SPOT" GUARANTEES PRODUCTION (it's a ~30% bump).
7. ASSUMING "BAD LANDING SPOT" KILLS A PROSPECT (it's a ~20% bump down).
8. CHASING ATHLETIC FREAKS WITHOUT PRODUCTION.
9. CHASING PRODUCERS WITHOUT ATHLETIC PROFILES.
10. IGNORING NFL DRAFT CAPITAL (the strongest signal).
11. BIASES (alma mater, conference, name recognition).
12. RECENCY BIAS (the player you watched this weekend).
13. FOMO (drafting the player your friend likes).
14. NOT REBUILDING TIER SHEET POST-NFL-DRAFT.
15. REACHING FOR POSITIONAL NEED OVER VALUE.

# SUMMARY - THE ROOKIE SCOUTING WORKFLOW

1. JANUARY-FEBRUARY: Build pre-NFL-Draft tier sheet from college production
   + athletic profile + projected NFL Draft slot.

2. LATE APRIL: Watch NFL Draft. Note landing spots and exact NFL Draft slots.

3. LATE APRIL / EARLY MAY: Rebuild tier sheet. Apply landing spot and draft
   capital adjustments. Identify tier breaks.

4. EARLY MAY (rookie draft week): Execute picks based on tier sheet.
   - Take BPA at your tier.
   - Trade back if same tier has multiple options.
   - Trade up only for clear-tier upgrades.
   - Use SF/TEP-specific weighting if applicable.

5. POST-DRAFT: Monitor camps and preseason for breakouts. Buy rookies you
   missed before market reprices.

6. THROUGHOUT YEAR 1: Don't overreact to rookie production. Most year-1
   numbers are noise. Year 2-3 is the real evaluation window.

THE GOAL: hit on 50%+ of your 1st-round picks, 30%+ of 2nds, 15%+ of 3rds.
That hit rate compounds over 5-10 years into a dynasty roster that
consistently contends.
